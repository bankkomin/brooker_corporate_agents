# Next-Gen Economy of Programmability

## Summary

Brooker's "convergence" thesis: the next-generation economy will be increasingly
**programmable** — systems that can be redesigned, automated, scaled, and connected
digitally. It is the conclusion of Brooker's [[macro-outlook-geopolitical-recalibration|
macro outlook]] and explains *why* Brooker concentrates on blockchain and venture exposure.

## Key Metrics

The deck states Brooker wants "a sector exposure of top 500 companies' index" within the
programmable-money domain — i.e. broad index-style exposure to the blockchain build-out
rather than single-name bets.

## The Four Domains of Programmability

| # | Domain | What it programmes | Notes |
|---|--------|--------------------|-------|
| 1 | Programmable Intelligence | AI / AI agents | Fastest-growing but most hyped; high valuations |
| 2 | Programmable Health | Precision medicine, longevity | Personalised care at the cellular level; cure of diseases |
| 3 | Programmable Materials | New compounds, new industries | Physical impact of AI; space and defence |
| 4 | Programmable Money | Blockchain | Frictionless payments, financial inclusion, RWA tokenization |

## Historical Context

Brooker prioritises **programmable money (blockchain)** — the "Financial Renaissance 2.0" —
because money sits at the centre of economic activity: improvements to money and financial
rails have outsized effects, and "money drives transactions that grow GDP." Blockchain is
framed as "not just about coins going up and down" but about moving value faster,
transferring ownership more easily, reducing payment friction, and tokenizing real-world
assets.

Brooker's chosen access route is **leading global venture capital managers** investing in
the companies building these new rails — operationalised through the
[[2026-04-21-pantera-x-brooker-investor-lunch|Pantera partnership]] and the VC Fund of
Funds.
