# The Internet of Value Thesis

## Summary

Brooker's signature public narrative: just as the 1990s internet democratized
*information* and moved data at the speed of light, blockchain is now creating the
**Internet of Value** — democratizing *value* and moving capital at the speed of light.
It is the rhetorical centrepiece of the CEO's [[the-audacity-of-value-speech|BNB Gala
keynote]] and a comms-facing expression of [[brooker-worldview-2026]] Worldview #6.

## Historical Context

The thesis frames crypto as the **"final phase of internet democratization" (Web 3.0)** —
a "fundamental rewriting of the global operating system." Supporting observations from the
speech:

- **The Spillover.** Crypto's "walls are coming down" — the digital is overflowing into
  the traditional. Tokenization creates global, 24/7 markets for assets that "used to
  sleep on the weekends."
- **Capitalizing intangibles.** The "Trump Coin" example (politics aside) shows the
  business mechanic: attaching a token to a brand "capitalizes a community" — turning an
  intangible asset (loyalty, brand equity) into a liquid, globally tradable market value.
  The speech invites business owners to imagine customer loyalty as a liquid owned asset.
- **Programmable money.** The deeper signal is not the transfer itself but that the money
  is *programmable* — money "computers and AI agents can use," the rails of the machine
  economy.
- **Remittance as the no-brainer.** Stablecoin remittance — value to anyone with a phone
  in seconds for near-zero fees — beats Western Union fees and SWIFT's T+3 correspondent
  banking; a "100x improvement."

## Anchor Lines

- "The internet democratized information… today, a technology democratizes value."
- "The Internet of Value is changing how we build wealth."
- "Let us not be the generation that watched it go by. Let us be the generation that
  built the tracks."
