# Macro Outlook — Geopolitical Recalibration & Reinforcing Dollar Hegemony

## Summary

The macro framework Brooker presents to investors — delivered as the opening segment of
the [[2026-04-21-pantera-x-brooker-investor-lunch|April 2026 Pantera investor lunch]] and
authored for "second-generation Thai business owners new to macro, crypto, and venture
investing." Its thesis: today's apparent chaos (wars, tariffs, chip bans, inflation) is
not random — it is a structured US–China competition to control the systems the world
runs on, and the US is recalibrating geopolitics to **reinforce dollar hegemony**. This is
a comms-facing companion to the CEO's [[brooker-worldview-2026]].

## Historical Context — The Argument

**Generational strategic assets.** Old wealth came from land, factories, distribution, and
relationships (1st generation) then capital allocation and networks (2nd generation). This
decade adds a new strategic asset: **ownership of technology** and the firms building the
new rails.

**Three sources of modern power.** Power now flows from (1) chokepoints and trade routes
(Panama Canal, Strait of Hormuz, Malacca Strait), (2) commodities (oil, gas, metals,
fertiliser, food), and (3) computation (chips, AI models, data centres, electricity).
Advanced chips are now treated "almost like strategic weapons."

**Why the US must defend the dollar.** The US carries very large debt and cannot stop
spending (defence, social programs, global role) while ageing demographics shrink the
workforce. Its survival model depends on the world continuing to use dollars, hold US
bonds, and invest in US markets — giving it the flexibility to borrow, run deficits, and
finance new growth aggressively.

**Four interacting pillars.** Every headline maps to one of four pillars — Military,
Economy & Trade, Politics & Policy, Technology. Events look disconnected on the surface
but are "random, but not random": parts of the same power struggle. Slide 5 lists ~20
"key events to position and win Midterms," each tagged to a pillar (e.g. Clarity/Genius
Acts → Technology; Greenland rare-earth deal → Economy; Gulf States defence alliance →
Military).

**The K-shaped outcome.** The gap between winners and losers widens. Industries near the
new centres of power — increasingly technology — grow stronger; others face slow growth,
debt pressure, and AI/automation disruption. Technology is "the layer that now sits on top
of many other sectors."
