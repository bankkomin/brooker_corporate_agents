# BICL Going Concern, Audit Basis & Crypto-Asset Accounting

## Summary

How [[bicl-corporate-profile|BICL]]'s audited financial statements are prepared — the
reporting framework, the going-concern basis, and the accounting treatment of its largest
asset class, crypto-assets. Drawn from the FY2024 and FY2025 audited reports by Philip
Leung & Co. Limited.

## Reporting Framework & Audit

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Auditor | Philip Leung & Co. Limited, Certified Public Accountants (Hong Kong) |
| Framework | HKFRS for Private Entities; Hong Kong Companies Ordinance |
| Auditing standards | HKSAs issued by the HKICPA |
| Opinion | Unqualified — "true and fair view" (both FY2024 and FY2025) |
| Consolidation | Exempt from preparing consolidated statements as a wholly-owned subsidiary |
| Reporting exemption | Qualifies for the Companies Ordinance reporting exemption (no business review) |
| FY2024 report date | 20 February 2025 |
| FY2025 report date | 12 February 2026 |

The statements are **separate (non-consolidated)** financial statements of BICL only;
BICL's own subsidiaries are accounted for at cost less impairment, not consolidated.

## Going Concern

The statements are prepared on a **going-concern basis**. BICL's ability to continue as a
going concern is supported by the continuing availability of **unsecured, on-demand loans
from its parent**, The Brooker Group Plc (see [[bicl-related-party-financing]]). The
FY2025 loss of USD 15.66M (see [[bicl-audited-financials-2025]]) draws down retained
earnings to USD 0.34M and equity to USD 5.94M, making continued parent support central to
the going-concern assessment.

## Crypto-Asset Accounting

- Crypto-assets are treated as **inventories held for trading**, stated at the **lower of
  cost and net realisable value** (NRV); NRV is based on current demand and market value.
- Movements in carrying value flow through profit or loss as write-downs or write-backs:
  - FY2023: write-back of USD 8,296,779
  - FY2024: write-back of USD 2,001,175 (carrying amount USD 32,539,786)
  - FY2025: write-**down** of USD 3,616,535 (carrying amount USD 29,857,714)
- Gains on trading of crypto-assets are shown net in the income statement.

## I-RECs (International Renewable Energy Certificates)

BICL also trades I-RECs, held as inventory. FY2025 saw USD 5,084,110 of I-RECs
surrendered due to unsuccessful tokenisation, with USD 600,000 inventory remaining at
year end — a material drag on the FY2025 result.
