# BICL Related-Party Financing — Parent-Company Loans

## Summary

[[bicl-corporate-profile|BICL]] is financed largely by **unsecured loans from its parent
company**, [[brooker-group|The Brooker Group Plc]]. The loans are documented as numbered
**Promissory Notes** and disclosed as "Loans from parent company" in BICL's audited
financial statements. This is the recurring intercompany-financing structure for the
Hong Kong subsidiary.

## Standard Loan Terms

Per Note 13 of the FY2024 and FY2025 audited reports, and confirmed by the promissory
note instruments:

| Term | Value |
|------|-------|
| Lender | The Brooker Group Plc (parent) |
| Borrower | Brooker International Co., Ltd. |
| Security | Unsecured |
| Interest rate | 3.50% per annum |
| Repayment | Repayable on demand |
| Expected timing | Not anticipated to be wholly repayable within 12 months (per Note 13) |

## Why It Matters

- Because the notes are **payable on demand**, the entire parent-loan balance is a
  **current liability** on BICL's balance sheet — a structural feature, not a sign of
  short-term repayment intent (Note 13 expects it to persist beyond 12 months).
- The 3.5% interest is a material, recurring **finance cost** for BICL (~USD 1.4M/year)
  and corresponding interest income for [[brooker-group]].
- Related-party loan/quasi-loan disclosures: FY2024 and FY2025 report "USD Nil" loans or
  quasi-loans made in favour of directors or controlled bodies (Note 15).
