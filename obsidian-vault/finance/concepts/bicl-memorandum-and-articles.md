# BICL Memorandum & Articles of Association

## Summary

The constitutional document of [[bicl-corporate-profile|Brooker International Company
Limited]] — its Memorandum and Articles of Association, adopted on incorporation in Hong
Kong. It sets out the company type, share structure, and internal governance rules under
the Hong Kong Companies Ordinance (Cap. 32 at the time of incorporation).

## Constitutional Provisions

- **Private company restrictions** — the Articles incorporate the Companies Ordinance
  Table A defaults (as modified): the right to transfer shares is restricted; the number
  of members is limited; and any invitation to the public to subscribe for shares or
  debentures is prohibited.
- **Shares** — shares are issued under the directors' authority and subject to the
  Ordinance; the company may vary class rights and pay commission as the Articles permit.
- **The Office** — the registered office is in Hong Kong, fixed by the directors.
- **Governance** — the Articles govern directors, members' meetings, dividends, and the
  use of the company seal.

## Why It Matters

The M&A is the governing instrument behind every other BICL record — it underpins the
share structure recorded in the [[bicl-corporate-registry]] (the 600,000-share founding
subscription, since increased to the 5,600,000 issued shares in the audited accounts) and
the director-appointment rules. As a private company limited by shares, BICL cannot raise
capital from the public — consistent with its wholly-owned status under [[brooker-group]].
