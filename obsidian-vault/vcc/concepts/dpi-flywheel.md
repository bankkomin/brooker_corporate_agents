# The DPI Flywheel — Liquidity & Distributions

## Summary

The "DPI Flywheel" is [[brook-limited-partners-fof]]'s argument that a 10-year closed-end
fund term does **not** mean a 10-year lock-up. DPI (Distribution to Paid-In capital)
typically begins well before the fund's final year because the fund is a
**self-liquidating vehicle** — capital is returned to LPs as portfolio milestones are
reached.

## Myth vs Reality

| The Myth | The Reality |
|----------|-------------|
| An 8-year "zero-coupon bond" with a single payout at the end | A self-liquidating vehicle returning capital progressively as portfolio milestones are reached; DPI begins well before the final year |

## Why Liquidity Cycles Are Compressing

- **The Equity Era (2010s vintages):** investments were primarily in company equity;
  liquidity tied to IPO/M&A windows taking 7–10 years to mature.
- **The Token Era (2020s vintages):** projects now use Token Generation Events (TGEs);
  tokens can list on global crypto exchanges shortly after launch, compressing the
  "seed → liquid asset" path to **2–4 years** and enabling faster DPI cycles.

## Accelerated DPI by Design

The fund is **seeded with a mature 2022–2023 vintage portfolio**, bypassing the early
"valley of death" failure point, and uses [[barbell-strategy|vintage diversification]] —
acquiring LP interests in older vintages closer to liquidity. The deck describes this
seeded position as a "harvest-ready" portfolio (~20% GP commitment, cited as 5–10x
industry standard) constructed for earlier cash distributions than a typical fund of
funds that starts from zero.

## Distribution Mechanics

Distributions follow the waterfall set out in [[brook-lp-fof-terms]]: 100% return of
Capital Contributions first (pro rata across Class A and Class B), then a tiered
Carried-Interest split to Class B holders, with remaining cash to Class A.
