# The Barbell Strategy — Manager Selection

## Summary

The Barbell Strategy is the manager-selection doctrine for [[brook-limited-partners-fof]].
The fund deliberately allocates to **two ends** of the manager spectrum — established
flagship funds and early-stage emerging specialists — and avoids the "crowded middle" of
mid-size funds, which the deck argues lack both the structural alpha of mega-funds and the
hands-on value of emerging specialists.

## The Two Ends

| End | Role in portfolio | Allocation |
|-----|-------------------|------------|
| **Established funds** | Anchor with brand, scale and market-wide access; hybrid structures blending venture, liquid tokens and arbitrage; "Special Opportunities" alpha (discounted-token purchases, PIPE deals) | ~50% |
| **Early-stage / emerging funds** | Nimble, specialized, hyper-focused teams; deep technical expertise and hands-on founder support; concentrated high-conviction asymmetric upside | ~50% |

## Manager Evaluation Framework

The deck describes a six-area scoring matrix used to identify top-quartile managers and
their "Right to Win" the best deals:

1. Team, judgment & founder support (cycle-resilient judgement, high founder NPS)
2. Thesis clarity & intellectual depth (second-order insight, systematic underwriting)
3. Track record & execution history (DPI / TVPI / IRR, deployment discipline)
4. Competitive moat & "Right to Win" (technical/incubation/network/ecosystem advantage)
5. Portfolio construction & pricing discipline (rational entry valuations)
6. Operational & technical due diligence (security, scalability, governance)

## Structural Alpha — Hybrid Funds

Established managers can layer value drivers — purchase discount, staking yield and
strategic PIPE deals — to multiply returns above a simple spot hold. The deck's FTX-estate
SOL case study illustrates a ~9.5x outcome versus ~1.9x for holding spot.
