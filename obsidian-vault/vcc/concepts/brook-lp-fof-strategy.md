# Brook Limited Partners FoF I — Investment Strategy

## Summary

[[brook-limited-partners-fof]] is a closed-end **blockchain & Web 3.0 venture fund of
funds** that invests as a Limited Partner across a globally diversified portfolio of VC
managers. Its investment objective is superior, risk-adjusted capital appreciation plus
accelerated return of capital, achieved through a [[barbell-strategy|barbell]] of emerging
and established managers and a vintage-diversification approach designed to mitigate the
J-curve.

## Investment Objective

The Supplement states the objective as long-term capital growth and income, investing
directly or indirectly through third-party managed funds ("Underlying Funds"). The deck
narrows the focus to a single core theme: **next-generation internet infrastructure and
applications** powered by blockchain, digital assets and decentralized systems, with
selective frontier exposure (AI, longevity) where it structurally intersects with Web 3.0.

## Strategy Pillars

| Pillar | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Barbell construction | ~50% emerging specialist funds + ~50% established flagship funds; deliberately minimize the "crowded middle" — see [[barbell-strategy]] |
| Diversification target | 20+ underlying VC funds, 500+ underlying portfolio companies, to win the venture power-law game |
| Vintage diversification | Primary commitments to new vintages **plus** strategic acquisition of LP interests in older vintages closer to liquidity, accelerating DPI |
| Seeded & warehoused portfolio | ~US$11.2M GP commitment via transferred 2021–2023 vintage VC interests; up to a further US$10M of sponsor capital across 2024–2025 vintages |
| Power-law math | A single VC fund (~30 startups) has ~21% odds of one unicorn; a fund of funds (500+ startups) has ~97% (deck slide 36) |

## Investment Vehicles & Mandate (deck slide 49)

- **Primary focus:** blockchain & digital assets — venture capital and opportunity/growth
  strategies.
- **Secondary focus:** other frontier technologies (AI, longevity).
- **Other instruments:** secondaries LP interests, equity shares or warrants — **no
  direct token holdings**.
- **Ticket size:** generally 1%–20% of total fund size per underlying fund.
- **Geography:** global.

## Why Now — Market Thesis

The deck frames a "US-Led, Regulatory-Driven Era": regulatory clarity (Genius Act July
2025, March 2026 SEC/CFTC joint 5-category taxonomy), infrastructure maturation, and a
favorable post-2021/22 "buyer's market" investment cycle. Cambridge Associates benchmarks
cited: blockchain venture net IRR 21.3% (10-yr) vs traditional venture 13.6%.
