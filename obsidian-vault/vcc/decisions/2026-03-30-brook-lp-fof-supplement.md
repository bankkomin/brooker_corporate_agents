## What the Supplement Establishes

The Supplement is the source of the binding fund terms catalogued in
[[brook-lp-fof-terms]]. Key provisions:

- **Structure** — closed-end registered sub-fund of [[brook-technology-capital-vcc]] with
  statutory asset/liability segregation under the VCC Act.
- **Economics** — 1.5% p.a. Management Fee on aggregate Commitments; tiered Carried
  Interest paid to Class B "Founder Shares"; 2% marketing-cost cap amortised over 3 years.
- **Share classes** — Class A (open to Eligible Investors) and Class B (Directors'
  discretion).
- **Capital** — US$1,000 Initial Offer Price; US$100,000 minimum subscription; expected
  launch size US$10M–US$100M; hard cap US$150M.
- **Closings** — Final Closing one year after the Initial Offer Period; New Investors pay
  an Equalisation Amount + 8% p.a. compounded Subsequent Closing Premium.
- **Distribution waterfall** — return of capital first, then tiered carry to Class B, with
  a Directors' clawback right over Class B distributions.
- **Service providers** — Fund Manager [[ternary-fund-management]]; Administrator
  [[formidium]]; Custodian DBS Bank; Auditor EY.

## Rationale

The Supplemental Memorandum, alongside the Information Memorandum, forms the offering
document required to privately offer Participating Shares to Accredited and Institutional
Investors. The Supplement governs where it conflicts with the Information Memorandum.

## Impact

- The tracked draft is the working legal basis for the FoF I capital raise.
