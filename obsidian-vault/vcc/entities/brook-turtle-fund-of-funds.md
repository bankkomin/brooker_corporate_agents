# Brook Turtle Fund of Funds

## Overview

Brook Turtle Fund of Funds is a multi-strategy, yield-oriented alternative-investment
fund of funds — an "Alternative Investment Platform Combining Third-Party Manager
Expertise with In-House Strategic Capabilities". It is the yield-focused product line of
Engine 1 (see [[three-engine-model]]) and is the deck-level expression of the fund the
CEO/IC vaults track as the [[yield-fund-of-funds]] / "Yield FoF" Sub-Fund 2 of the VCC.

> Cross-reference: the [[yield-fund-of-funds]] (CEO vault, "14% Yield Fund of Funds") and
> [[brook-limited-partners-fof]] (IC vault, where the yield fund is named "Brook Turtle
> Yield FoF" Sub-Fund 2) describe the same family of vehicles. This article holds the
> Brook Turtle deck detail.

## Strategy & Risk Framework

- **Dual approach:** a fund-of-funds core (5–20 vetted external multi-strategy managers)
  complemented by proprietary in-house strategies for additional alpha and customization.
- **Manager-selection process:** initial quantitative screening → due diligence → final
  selection (on-site visits, reference checks) → portfolio allocation by correlation/risk
  contribution → ongoing monitoring and rebalancing.
- **Risk mitigation:** manager concentration limits, strategy diversification
  requirements, regular correlation monitoring, dynamic rebalancing protocols.
- **Residual risks acknowledged:** alpha-source concentration, counterparty/exchange
  exposure, market-wide liquidity events, regulatory and operational risk.

## Structure & Operations

- Recently **transitioned to a VCC structure** after the successful close of its first
  fund, moving into a new VCC-structured fund for enhanced operational flexibility.
- Institutional-grade operations: reporting, compliance, risk monitoring, regular audits
  and third-party oversight; investor portal with performance attribution and analytics.
