# VAT on Cancelled Cross-Border Service Contracts (Form ภพ.36)

## Summary

This is a tax-analysis note (not a statute) on the **Value Added Tax (VAT)**
consequences when a Thai company **cancels a cross-border service contract** and
returns what it received — using the worked case of a Thai company that bought
**Renewable Energy Certificates (REC)** from a Singapore supplier, had an outstanding
balance, and wished to cancel and return the RECs. The key instrument is **Form ภพ.36**
(VAT the buyer must self-remit). It reflects guidance as of **5 August 2025**.

## Key Points

### Form ภพ.36 — self-remitted VAT on imported services
- When a Thai buyer purchases goods/services from abroad that count as a **service
  used in Thailand**, the Thai buyer must self-remit VAT on behalf of the foreign
  seller by filing **Form ภพ.36** (Revenue Code Section 83/6).
- REC is generally treated as an **intangible right** ("right to count carbon-
  reduction credit") — i.e. a **service**, not tangible goods — and, where used in
  Thai activity, an **imported service** under Revenue Code Section 77/1(10).

### VAT treatment of cancellation
| Situation | VAT outcome |
|-----------|-------------|
| REC used in Thailand, treated as service | File ภพ.36, self-remit **VAT 7%** of the amount paid |
| Contract cancelled and goods/service returned, parties agree to refund/offset | May request a refund or adjust previously remitted ภพ.36 VAT (Section 82/3, para. 2) |
| Service fee not yet fully paid and VAT not yet remitted | No VAT liability on the unpaid portion |
| Contract fully cancelled and all RECs returned, no rights used in Thailand | **No VAT liability (ภพ.36)** — the service is treated as not having occurred |

### Conclusion
If a company **fully cancels** the contract and **returns all RECs** without using any
right or benefit in Thailand, it has **no VAT (ภพ.36) liability** from the
cancellation. If ภพ.36 VAT was already remitted for part of it, the company may seek a
refund or adjustment per Revenue Department practice (Section 82/3; orders ป.110/2545
and ป.36/2544).

## Why It Matters

Relevant to Brooker entities transacting in carbon credits / RECs or other intangible
cross-border services — the import-VAT and contract-cancellation mechanics determine
the self-remittance obligation and refund route. Documentary support (cancellation
letter, REC-return evidence, confirmation of non-use in Thailand) should be retained.
