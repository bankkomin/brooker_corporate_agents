# Possible Delisting & Removal of Delisting Grounds (การเข้าข่ายอาจถูกเพิกถอน)

## Summary

The Stock Exchange of Thailand (SET, ตลาดหลักทรัพย์) may delist a listed company if it
judges the company unfit to retain listed status. This concept summarises the **grounds**
that put a company at risk of delisting, the **SP/NC procedure** SET applies, and the
**criteria for removing the delisting grounds** (พ้นเหตุเพิกถอน). Source: SET's simplified
regulations page, captured 2020-02-19.

## Grounds That Trigger Possible Delisting

A listed company may face delisting where it has any of:

1. **Financial condition / operating results** below SET's standard — including
   shareholders' equity **below zero**; an auditor **disclaiming or adversely opining** on
   the financial statements for **3 consecutive years**; operating assets significantly
   reduced or about to be; or a near-total cessation of business.
2. **Loss of qualifications, violation, or neglect** of SET requirements — including
   disclosing false information, failing to disclose material information, or failing to
   appoint an audit committee within the required time.
3. **Liquidation / dissolution**, or a court receivership order.

Late submission of financial statements is also a triggering event handled under a
separate timeline (see below).

## SET's Procedure — SP and NC Marks

- SET posts an **SP (Suspension)** sign on the security from the date the audited
  financials showing negative equity are filed.
- SET declares the security in **NC Stage 1 (Non-Compliance)** within **7 business days**
  of the delisting ground appearing, and bars trading for a further **30 days** or until
  the company announces a rehabilitation plan.
- After the 30-day SP and a filed rehabilitation plan, SET permits trading for **30 days**,
  then re-applies SP until the company cures the delisting ground.
- The **rehabilitation period is 3 years**, split into three 1-year stages (**NC Stage 1
  to Stage 3**). A company may apply **once** to extend a given NC stage by up to **1
  year**, filing **7 business days** in advance and meeting qualifying conditions
  (e.g. shareholders' equity ≥ THB 20M, or one year of core-business net profit).

### Late Financial Statements

If financials are **more than 180 days late**, the security is barred from trading; if
still not filed within 180 days of the due date, SET posts NC and keeps SP. After a
further 180 days from the NC declaration with no filing, SET orders delisting (with a
possible 30-day trading window before removal).

## Removing the Delisting Grounds (พ้นเหตุเพิกถอน)

To exit delisting risk, a company must meet **all** of:

- **Shareholders' equity** ≥ THB 20M (to trade on **mai**) or ≥ THB 300M (to trade on
  **SET**), after adjusting for the auditor's opinion.
- **Net profit** from normal core-business operations, continuously under substantially
  the same management for **at least 1 year** before the application; for SET, net profit
  **≥ THB 30M** plus cumulative-period net profit.
- **Debt restructuring of more than 75%** of total debt, with debts paid on schedule, and
  the plan preserving existing shareholders' stake at **no less than 10%** of paid-up
  capital after restructuring.
- A clean audit report (no qualified opinion on internal-control/accounting deficiencies,
  no disclaimer due to scope limitation, no adverse opinion).

### Silent Period on Exit

Strategic shareholders (those involved in management) are barred from selling securities
totalling **55% of paid-up capital**. The silent period is **1 year** for a general exit;
**1 year** where a new joint-venture partner already meets listing qualifications, or
**2 years** for a backdoor-listing-type exit applied for within 1 year of the new
business/asset entering. Locked-up holders may release **25%** of locked securities every
**6 months** after trading resumes.

## Why It Matters

These criteria define the worst-case regulatory outcome for a listed entity. A
Brooker-type holding company must monitor shareholders' equity, audit-opinion quality, and
financial-statement filing deadlines as hard early-warning indicators — equity below zero
or a 3-year qualified audit opinion are direct delisting triggers.
