# Source: Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535 (Consolidated)

## Document Overview

**As-of:** consolidated version (ฉบับประมวล) of the **Securities and Exchange Act
B.E. 2535** (พระราชบัญญัติหลักทรัพย์และตลาดหลักทรัพย์ พ.ศ. 2535), the foundational
Thai capital-markets statute, enacted 1992 and amended repeatedly (notably Act No. 4,
B.E. 2551 / 2008). Held in the `Collection of Laws` folder of the Thai SEC regulations
archive.

## Key Findings

- The Act is the **primary law** establishing the Securities and Exchange Commission
  (ก.ล.ต.), the Capital Market Supervisory Board (คณะกรรมการกำกับตลาดทุน), and the
  framework for securities offerings, the Stock Exchange of Thailand, intermediaries, and
  enforcement.
- It is the **enabling authority** cited by virtually every SEC/Capital Market
  Supervisory Board notification in this archive — e.g. bond private-placement rules cite
  Sections 16/6, 89/27, 35, 40, 41, 42 and 43; bondholder-representative rules cite
  Sections 16/6, 41, 46 and 49; the audit-committee mandate derives from it.
- The consolidated edition incorporates all amendments through the date of compilation.

## Relevance

This is the constitutional layer of Thai securities regulation. Every other concept in the
regulations area — debentures, listing, delisting, audit committees, connected
transactions — is a notification issued *under* this Act. It is the reference point for
the legal basis of any SEC rule.
