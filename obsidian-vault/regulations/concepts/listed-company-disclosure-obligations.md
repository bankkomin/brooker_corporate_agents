# Listed-Company Disclosure Obligations (Periodic and Event-Based)

## Summary

The Stock Exchange of Thailand requires every listed company to disclose material
information so investors can make informed decisions and the market trades fairly and
with equal access to information. SET classifies the duty into two streams: **periodic
disclosure** (การเปิดเผยข้อมูลตามระยะเวลา) and **event-based disclosure**
(การเปิดเผยข้อมูลตามเหตุการณ์). Disclosures are filed electronically through SET's
information system (SET Portal / SETLink), in both Thai and English.

## Periodic Disclosure — Filing Deadlines

| Report | Deadline |
|--------|----------|
| Annual financial statements (audited) | 2 months after period-end (3 months if a Q4 statement is also filed) |
| Quarterly financial statements (reviewed) | 45 days after quarter-end (Form F-45) |
| Annual registration statement (Form 56-1) | 3 months after year-end |
| Annual report (Form 56-2) | within 4 months, with the AGM notice |

A company whose revenue or net profit differs by **more than 20%** from the same prior-year
period must also file an interim management discussion and analysis (Interim MD&A).
Filings go to both SET and the SEC; where a due date falls on a holiday it rolls to the
next business day.

## Event-Based Disclosure — Three Tiers

Event-based information is graded by urgency and price-sensitivity:

1. **Tier 1 — immediate.** Information on company operations, or affecting the share price,
   investment decisions or shareholder rights. Must be reported **immediately** — without
   delay on the day the event occurs, or at the latest before 9:00 a.m. the next business
   day.
2. **Tier 2 — within 3 business days.** Information with no direct price impact but worth
   disclosing to investors.
3. **Tier 3 — within 14 days.** Information SET collects as reference evidence.

Disclosure must be complete, accurate, clear and sufficient; companies must avoid
concealment, presenting uncertainties as certainties, promotional language, or news that
inflates the share price. If a company fails to disclose significant information on time,
SET may post an **"H" or "SP"** sign to halt trading, or an **"NP"** sign to warn that
undisclosed material information remains.

## Financial-Assistance Disclosure

A separate SET circular on **public companies providing financial assistance**
(บมจ.ให้ความช่วยเหลือทางการเงิน) addresses disclosure where a listed company gives loans,
guarantees or other financial support — an area now also covered by the MT and connected-
transaction rules (see [[material-transactions-rpt-2026-reform]]).

## Agent Notes

These deadlines predate later form revisions (Form 56-1 is now the "56-1 One Report").
Treat the tier structure and the immediate-disclosure 9:00 a.m. rule as the durable core;
verify exact form numbers against current SET guidance before relying on them.
