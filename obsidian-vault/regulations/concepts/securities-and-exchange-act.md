# Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535

## Summary

The **Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535** (พระราชบัญญัติหลักทรัพย์และตลาด
หลักทรัพย์ พ.ศ. 2535 — "SEA") is Thailand's primary capital-market statute. It
establishes the **Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)** and the legal framework
for securities offerings, the securities business, the **Stock Exchange of Thailand**,
listed-company conduct, takeovers, and enforcement. It is the statute under which
substantially all rules in the `regulations` knowledge area are issued.

## Key Points

- **Amendment No. 4, B.E. 2551 (2008)** — a significant amendment effective **31
  August 2008**. It introduced, among other things, the **good-governance / directors'
  duties regime** and **Section 89/29**, the basis for the rules on material
  acquisition/disposal of assets — see [[acquisition-disposal-of-assets]].
- The Act empowers the **Capital Market Supervisory Board** (คณะกรรมการกำกับตลาดทุน)
  to issue notifications (the "ทจ." series) on securities offerings, share/warrant
  distribution and allotment, and listed-company obligations.
- **Section 170** authorises the SET Board (with SEC approval) to make rules on
  delisting of listed securities — see [[set-delisting-regulation-2564]].
