# Revenue Code Section 40 — Categories of Assessable Income

## Summary

A reference capture (27 September 2023) of the Thai **Revenue Code** (ประมวลรัษฎากร),
Chapter 3 income-tax provisions, focused on **Section 40** — which enumerates the categories
of **assessable income** (เงินได้พึงประเมิน), and specifically **Section 40(4)**, the
category covering investment income such as interest, dividends, and gains on securities
and digital tokens. The folder is the source the wiki ties to questions about tax treatment
of investment income.

## Assessable Income Framework (Sections 38–39)

- **Section 38** — Income tax is an assessment tax; a tax assessment officer assesses it.
- **Section 39 — definitions:**
  - "Assessable income" — income liable to tax under this Chapter, including property or
    other benefits computable in money, tax borne by the payer, and tax credits under
    Section 47 bis.
  - **"Companies/partnerships in the same group"** — two or more legal entities related
    such that: more than half the shareholders/partners of one are also more than half of
    another; a shareholder/partner holding > 50% of one entity's capital holds > 50% of
    another's; one entity holds > 50% of another's capital; or more than half the managing
    directors/partners of one also manage another.
  - "Listed company," "investment management company," and "mutual fund" are defined by
    reference to the law on the Stock Exchange of Thailand.

## Section 40(4) — Investment Income

Section 40 lists income categories 40(1)–40(8). **Section 40(4)** captures investment-type
income — including interest, dividends, shares of profit, and gains on the sale or transfer
of securities. It is the category that determines withholding and assessment treatment of
returns from share and bond investments, and is the reference point used when classifying
income from **digital tokens** for tax purposes (see the related folder item "Token
decree").

## Why It Matters

Section 40(4) classification governs how investment returns earned by Brooker entities are
taxed and withheld. Whether a return is ordinary business income or Section 40(4) investment
income — and whether group-company relationships apply — directly affects the effective tax
on portfolio income.
