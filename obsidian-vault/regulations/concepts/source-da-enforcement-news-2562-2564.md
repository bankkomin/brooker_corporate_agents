# Source — SEC Digital Asset Enforcement News (B.E. 2562–2564)

## Document Overview

A set of Thai SEC ([[thai-sec]]) press releases (as collected 27 August 2021) documenting
enforcement under the [[digital-asset-emergency-decree-2561]]. They are point-in-time
illustrations of how the SEC applies the Decree.

## Key Findings

- **Binance criminal complaint (SEC News No. 125/2564, 2 July 2021)** — The SEC filed a
  criminal complaint against Binance with the Economic Crime Suppression Division. Binance
  was found to operate a digital asset exchange platform soliciting Thai investors via its
  website and the "Binance Thai Community" Facebook page **without a licence**. The SEC had
  sent an inquiry letter on 5 April 2021; Binance did not respond in time. This breaches
  **Section 26**, an offence under **Section 66**: imprisonment 2–5 years, fine THB
  200,000–500,000, plus THB 10,000/day for continuing violation.
- **Huobi temporary suspension** — The SEC ordered the Huobi exchange to temporarily
  suspend digital asset exchange services.
- **BX (Bitcoin Co., Ltd.) closure** — SEC News No. 105/2562 and 106/2562 plus a Q&A
  address the wind-down of Thailand's BX exchange and its impact on users.

## Relevance

Confirms the SEC actively enforces the licensing requirement against offshore and domestic
platforms alike. The SEC stresses that any party offering digital-asset trading, exchange,
custody, transfer, or withdrawal services in Thailand must be licensed; users can verify
licensed operators via www.sec.or.th and the "SEC Check First" app.
