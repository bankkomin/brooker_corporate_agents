# Connected Transaction Rules (Legacy Framework)

## Summary

A **connected transaction (รายการที่เกี่ยวโยงกัน)** is a transaction between a listed company
(or its subsidiary) and a *connected person* of the company. Because such deals carry an
inherent conflict-of-interest risk, Thai law subjects them to enhanced governance: arm's-
length pricing, exclusion of interested parties from decision-making, and tiered
board/shareholder approval and disclosure.

This article covers the **legacy framework** in force from **31 August 2008** until **30
June 2026** — Section 89/12 of the Securities and Exchange Act and Capital Market
Supervisory Board Notification **TorChor. 21/2551**, amended four times (TorChor. 8/2552,
20/2553, 79/2558). From **1 July 2026** this regime is replaced — see
[[material-transactions-rpt-2026-reform]] and [[source-tc-46-2568-connected-transactions]].

## Key Metrics

**Who is a connected person** (per the SET guidance and TorChor. 21/2551): directors,
executives, major shareholders, controlling persons of the listed company, and persons
nominated to those roles — plus their related persons and close relatives; any juristic
person whose major shareholder or controller is such a person; persons acting on behalf
of or under the influence of the foregoing; and persons who benefit financially from a
company transaction alongside its directors/executives/controllers.

- **"Executive"** is defined as the manager or the top-four executives below the manager
  (and equivalents), plus accounting/finance heads at department-manager level or above.
- The framework was issued under SEA Sections 16/6, 89/1, 89/12 and 89/13 (as amended No.
  4, B.E. 2551), and aligns with the SET Board notification on disclosure and conduct of
  listed companies in connected transactions (B.E. 2546).
- Government-entity counterparties (90%+ state-owned) receive special treatment.

## Historical Context

The connected-transaction regime was established when the Securities and Exchange Act
(Amendment No. 4) B.E. 2551 took effect on 31 August 2008, transferring rulemaking on
connected transactions to the SEC ([[thai-sec]]). The TorChor. 21/2551 codified version
(ฉบับประมวล) consolidates the original notification with amendments No. 2–4. An English
translation (BorJorPor 22-01) was published 25 February 2009. The 2014 SET summary
(SET_ConnectedTransactions) is the practitioner-facing digest of this regime.

The regime is **superseded from 1 July 2026** by TorChor. 46/2568, which repeals the
entire TorChor. 21/2551 series.
