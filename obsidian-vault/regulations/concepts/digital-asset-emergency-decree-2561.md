# Digital Asset Business Emergency Decree B.E. 2561

## Summary

The **Emergency Decree on Digital Asset Businesses B.E. 2561** (พระราชกำหนดการประกอบ
ธุรกิจสินทรัพย์ดิจิทัล พ.ศ. 2561, the "DA Decree") is Thailand's primary statute regulating
cryptocurrency, digital tokens, and digital asset intermediaries. The Cabinet approved it on
27 March 2018; it was published in the Royal Gazette on 13 May 2018 and took effect
**14 May 2018**. It is administered by the Thai SEC ([[thai-sec]]) with the Minister of
Finance as licensing authority. Its purpose: regulate public fundraising through digital
token offerings, license digital asset businesses, and prevent use of digital assets for
money laundering and fraud.

## Scope — What Is a Digital Asset

A "digital asset" (สินทรัพย์ดิจิทัล) is **cryptocurrency** or a **digital token** (Section 3):

- **Cryptocurrency (คริปโทเคอร์เรนซี)** — an electronic data unit created to serve as a
  medium of exchange for goods, services, or other digital assets.
- **Digital token (โทเคนดิจิทัล)** — an electronic data unit defining a holder's right to
  participate in a project/venture ("investment token") or to obtain specific goods/services
  ("utility token"), per the issuer–holder agreement.

**Exclusion (Section 5):** Securities under the Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535 are
**not** digital assets, even when in electronic-unit form — they remain under the SEA. The
SEC guideline (DA-guideline, data as of 19 April 2021) classifies tokens by whether the
coin defines a right (no right + no medium-of-exchange intent = not a digital asset) and
whether a utility token is "ready-to-use" or "not-ready-to-use."

## Key Requirements

| Activity | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Digital token offering to the public (Chapter 3) | Only a limited or public company; SEC approval; file registration statement + draft prospectus; offer via an approved **ICO portal** (Sections 17–19) |
| Digital asset business (Chapter 4) — exchange, broker, dealer | License from the Minister of Finance on SEC recommendation (Section 26) |
| Director/executive appointments | No prohibited characteristics; SEC consent required (Sections 28–29); replacement within 15 days of consent revocation |
| Customer asset segregation | Separate per-customer accounts; client assets remain client property and are protected in insolvency (Section 31) |
| AML | DA operators and ICO portals are "financial institutions" under AML law (Section 7) |

The Decree also prohibits unfair trading practices (Chapter 6): false dissemination,
insider trading, front running, and market manipulation — mirroring the SEA. It carries
criminal penalties (Chapter 8) and civil sanctions (Chapter 9).

## Why It Matters

This is the controlling regime for any Brooker digital-asset activity in Thailand. Operating
an unlicensed exchange, broker, or dealer is a criminal offence (Section 26, penalty under
Section 66: 2–5 years imprisonment and THB 200,000–500,000 fine plus THB 10,000/day). The
SEC has enforced it against unlicensed offshore platforms — see
[[source-da-enforcement-news-2562-2564]].
