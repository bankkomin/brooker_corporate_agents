# Thai Listed Company Shareholder Meeting & Record Date Rules

## Summary

Thai listed companies must give shareholders — as owners — a vote on significant
matters beyond those delegated to the board. The SET / SEC framework sets out **two
meeting types**, the mechanics for **fixing who is entitled to attend** (Record Date),
and **notice timing**. These rules are summarised from SET / CGThailand guidance
(as of August 2022).

## Two Meeting Types

- **Annual General Meeting (AGM)** — must be held **every year within 4 months** of the
  company's accounting year-end (Public Limited Companies Act). Standard agenda:
  prior-meeting minutes, annual financial statements, profit allocation /
  dividend, election of directors retiring by rotation, director remuneration,
  auditor appointment and audit fee.
- **Extraordinary General Meeting (EGM)** — held when a significant matter must be put
  to shareholders outside the AGM cycle.

## Fixing Shareholder Rights — RD vs BC

Two mechanisms determine which shareholders receive a right (to attend, to a dividend,
to subscribe for new shares, etc.):

- **Record Date (RD)** — names a date for the shareholder roll (SEC Act ss. 89/26 &
  225). The register is closed to transfers for 1 day; the meeting must be held
  **within 2 months** of the RD.
- **Book Closing (BC)** — closes the register until the meeting completes (Public
  Limited Companies Act s. 60); the meeting must be held **within 21 days** of the BC.

RD is generally preferred — it gives the company more time to prepare and circulate
documents. Either way, the company must announce the RD or BC **at least 14 days in
advance**. The trading system displays **XM / XD / XR / XW / XB** marks 2 business days
before book closing so investors know which rights a purchase no longer carries.

## Disclosure & Notice Timing

- Board resolutions on meeting date, agenda and the rights-record date must be disclosed
  **immediately** — by 9:00 a.m. the next business day at the latest — via **SETLink**.
- Any cancellation, addition or material change of an agenda item must be notified to
  the SET **at least 14 days before** the RD / BC.
- **Meeting notice delivery:** general agenda items — **at least 7 days** before the
  meeting; **special items** governed by other rules (connected transactions,
  acquisition/disposal of assets, delisting, ESOP issuance, low-price private
  placement) — **at least 14 days** before the meeting.
- If foreign shareholders exist, an English-language notice is also required. Proxy
  forms follow Ministry of Commerce templates (Form Kor. / Khor. / Khor.).

## Why It Matters

Meeting and record-date timing constrains the calendar for any Brooker capital action —
capital increase, asset acquisition/disposal, connected transaction, or takeover-related
item — and feeds the staging pipeline's scheduling of corporate-action data.
