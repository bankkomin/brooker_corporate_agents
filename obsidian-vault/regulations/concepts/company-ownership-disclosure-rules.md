# Listed-Company Information Disclosure Rules

## Summary

The Stock Exchange of Thailand ([[stock-exchange-of-thailand]]) rulebook **บจ/ป 11-00 —
"Rules, Conditions and Procedures on Information Disclosure and Conduct of Listed
Companies"** sets out the events on which a listed company must report information to the
SET. The folder in this batch concerns one application of this rulebook: disclosure when a
listed company **holds more than 10% in a joint-venture company**.

## Key Metrics

The rulebook is issued under **Section 170(2) of the Securities and Exchange Act B.E.
2535**, by the SET Board with SEC concurrence. Key definitions and triggers:

- **Major shareholder (ผู้ถือหุ้นรายใหญ่)** — a holder, directly or indirectly, of more
  than **10%** of a listed company's paid-up capital, counting shares held by related
  persons. This 10% threshold is the basis for the JV-stake disclosure case.
- **Related person (ผู้ที่เกี่ยวข้อง)** — a person or partnership under Section 258(1)–(7)
  of the SEA (pre-amendment text).
- **Financial assistance (ให้/รับความช่วยเหลือทางการเงิน)** — giving or receiving
  assistance via lending, borrowing, guarantees, or pledging assets as security.

**Reportable events (Clause 3)** include: board resolutions setting AGM/EGM dates;
record-date / book-closure resolutions; capital-increase or share-allotment resolutions;
issuance of new securities or any act affecting convertible-securities holders' rights;
and acts affecting redemption/expiry of listed securities (≥30 days' advance notice).

## Historical Context

The rulebook replaced two 1993 (B.E. 2536) SET notifications on listed-company disclosure.
It has been amended repeatedly: Amendment No. 4 (B.E. 2551, effective 31 Aug 2008) revised
the record-date provision; Amendment No. 6 (B.E. 2553, effective 1 Oct 2010) revised the
core definitions; Amendment No. 7 (B.E. 2558, effective 16 May 2015) revised the
"listed company" definition and added the holding-company/core-company definitions (see
[[holding-company-listing]]).
