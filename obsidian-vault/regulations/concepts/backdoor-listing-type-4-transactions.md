# Backdoor Listing — Type 4 Acquisition Transactions

## Summary

A **backdoor listing** (การเข้าจดทะเบียนกับตลาดหลักทรัพย์โดยอ้อม) is when a non-listed
business gains a stock-exchange listing indirectly, by being acquired into an existing
listed company instead of running its own IPO. The Stock Exchange of Thailand
([[stock-exchange-of-thailand]]) governs this as **"Type 4 transactions"** under the SET
notification on disclosure and conduct of listed companies in the acquisition or disposal
of assets (B.E. 2547, effective 15 November 2004 — see
[[source-set-asset-acquisition-regulation-2547]], which classifies asset transactions
into Types 1–4 by size).

## What Triggers a Type 4 Transaction

Under Clause 20, an acquisition of assets from a non-listed company is a Type 4 transaction
if it has **any one** of these features:

1. **Size ≥ 100%** when calculated under the four size tests (net asset value, net profit,
   total consideration, or securities issued as consideration).
2. The acquisition **transfers control** from the listed company's controlling person to
   the controlling person of the non-listed company (or to the asset's former owner).
3. The acquisition results from a business combination leaving the listed company's
   existing shareholders holding **below 50%** of the paid-up capital of the combined
   entity, or transferring control as in (2).

The SET may aggregate transactions within **12 months** as one (Clause 21) and may treat a
transaction as Type 4 by **substance** if its real intent is to evade the rules
(Clause 21/2, added by Amendment No. 6, effective 1 April 2018) — a 30-day review.

## Required Actions

A company entering a Type 4 transaction must (Clause 22):

- Apply to the SET for re-admission of securities — the SET reviews it as if it were a
  **new listing application** (Clause 23). The combined entity must meet listing-eligibility
  criteria (except the shareholding-distribution criterion, where the maintenance-of-status
  threshold applies); the non-listed assets must have an operating track record under a
  consistent management group.
- Convene a **shareholder meeting** to approve the transaction (Chapter 5), with an
  **independent financial advisor (IFA)** opinion sent to the SET and shareholders covering
  reasonableness, fairness of price, a vote recommendation, and working-capital adequacy.
- Send the meeting notice ≥ 14 days in advance.

## Exemption — Type 4 Without New-Listing Application

Clause 24 exempts a Type 4 transaction from the new-listing application if **all** apply:
the acquired business is similar/complementary to the listed company's; no major change in
core business is planned; the post-acquisition group is suitable for listing; and there is
no significant change in the board or controlling persons. Such companies still report and
seek shareholder approval (Clause 25).

## Why It Matters

Backdoor listing is a recognised "shortcut" to a listing but the SET re-tests the merged
entity against IPO-grade standards to prevent shell companies from being used to bypass
listing scrutiny. Any Brooker reverse-takeover structure must be screened against the four
size tests and the substance test.
