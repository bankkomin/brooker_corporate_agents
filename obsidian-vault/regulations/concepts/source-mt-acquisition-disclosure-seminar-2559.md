# Source — MT/RPT Reform Seminar & SET Acquisition-Reporting Notices (2559)

## Document Overview

A folder of material around the 2016 (B.E. 2559) reform of the rules for **Material
Transactions (MT)** and **Related Party Transactions (RPT)** of listed companies, plus two
SEC notices on securities-acquisition reporting and tender-offer opinions. It includes the
SET/SEC public-hearing seminar held **2 February 2559** and a 2017 covering email.

## Key Findings

**MT/RPT reform seminar (2 February 2559)** — A public-hearing seminar on improving the MT
and RPT rules for listed companies. Three primary objectives:

1. **Clearer rules aligned with regional markets** — the size-calculation method, the
   thresholds requiring shareholder approval, the counting of connected persons, the IFA
   opinion, and shareholders' **veto rights**.
2. **Better process and use of directors' fiduciary duty** — interest reporting and meeting
   attendance, opinions from individual directors, and disclosure in the meeting notice.
3. Implementation phased over three years (Year 1 — 2559 hearing/draft; Year 2 — 2560
   communication; Year 3 — 2561 effect and monitoring).

A flagged scope change: extending MT coverage to **giving/receiving financial assistance
to outside parties** that is not the company's ordinary business — previously outside the
acquisition/disposal rules.

**SEC notices** — ที่ ทก.52/2559 amends (No. 2) the requirements for **reporting acquisition
or disposal of securities**; ที่ ทก.54/2559 amends (No. 2) the **form and timeframe for
preparing opinions on a tender offer** (คำเสนอซื้อ). Both are short notice extracts.

## Relevance

Documents the reform trajectory between the original [[source-set-asset-acquisition-regulation-2547]]
and the modern MT/RPT regime ([[material-transaction-related-party-2026]]). The
extension of MT to financial-assistance transactions and the introduction of shareholder
veto rights are the substantive shifts to note.
