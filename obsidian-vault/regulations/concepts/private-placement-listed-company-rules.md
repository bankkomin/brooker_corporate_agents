# Private Placement of New Shares by Listed Companies

## Summary

A **private placement (PP)** is a listed company's offer of newly issued shares to a
limited group of persons (เสนอขายหุ้นที่ออกใหม่ต่อบุคคลในวงจำกัด) rather than to the public.
The Thai SEC ([[thai-sec]]) regulates listed-company PPs through a dedicated stream of
Capital Market Supervisory Board notifications. This folder collects the regime as it
evolved: the **ทจ.72/2558** notification permitting listed-company PPs and its amendments
(ฉ.2 — ทจ.21/2559, ฉ.3 — ทจ.37/2559, ฉ.4 — ทจ.30/2561), the related ทจ.74/2558 (the
13th amendment to the new-share offering rules), and the PP rules culminating in the SEC's
**revised PP criteria effective 1 July 2566 (2023)**.

## How a Listed-Company PP Works

A listed company seeking to issue new shares by PP must obtain SEC approval and comply with
pricing, disclosure, and shareholder-approval rules. The regime distinguishes PP shares
priced at or near market from PP shares offered at a discount, which carry stricter
disclosure and approval conditions (see [[low-price-private-placement-disclosure]]). The
2566 (2023) revision recalibrated these conditions for listed-company PPs.

## Silent Period

PP shares offered at a **low price** are subject to a **silent period** — a resale
restriction barring the placement recipients from selling the PP shares for a defined
period after issuance. The folder's reference document (29 April 2015) sets out the silent
period applicable to low-price PP shares; the purpose is to prevent recipients from
immediately on-selling discounted stock for a quick gain at the expense of existing
shareholders.

## Why It Matters

PP is a primary route for a listed company to raise equity quickly from strategic or
institutional investors without a full public offering. Any Brooker-related listed-company
capital raise via PP must clear SEC approval, the market-price/discount pricing rules, the
applicable shareholder-approval thresholds, and — for discounted shares — the silent-period
resale lock.
