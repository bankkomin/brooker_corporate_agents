# Source: IASB IFRS 16 Leases — Project Summary (Jan 2016)

## Document Overview

**As of:** January 2016 (file timestamped 2016-11-03). The IASB *Project Summary and
Feedback Statement — IFRS 16 Leases*, a ~14-page explanatory publication issued alongside
the standard.

## Key Findings

- IFRS 16 was issued January 2016, effective **1 January 2019**, replacing IAS 17. Early
  application is allowed only if IFRS 15 is also applied.
- **Lessees:** single model — recognise a right-of-use asset and lease liability for all
  leases > 12 months (low-value-asset exemption); depreciation and interest shown
  separately. The operating/finance lease split is removed for lessees.
- **Lessors:** IAS 17 treatment substantially carried forward (operating/finance split kept).
- Background: IAS 17 left most leases "off balance sheet". In 2005 the US SEC raised
  transparency concerns; the IASB and FASB launched a joint project. Listed companies had
  disclosed ~**US$3 trillion** of off balance sheet lease commitments in 2014; long-term
  liabilities of heavy lease users were understated by 22%–45% by region.

## Relevance

Primary international source for the [[ifrs-16-leases]] concept; underpins Thailand's
TFRS 16. Relevant to any listed issuer assessing the balance-sheet and gearing impact of
its lease portfolio.

## Extracted Data

- Off balance sheet lease understatement of long-term liabilities by region: Africa/Middle
  East 27%, Asia/Pacific 32%, Europe 26%, Latin America 45%, North America 22%.
