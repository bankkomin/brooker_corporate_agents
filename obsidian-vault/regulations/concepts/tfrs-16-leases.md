# TFRS 16 — Leases

## Summary

**TFRS 16 — Leases** (มาตรฐานการรายงานทางการเงิน ฉบับที่ 16 เรื่อง สัญญาเช่า) is the Thai
Financial Reporting Standard on lease accounting, the Thai counterpart of **IFRS 16**.
It governs how an entity identifies a lease, and how lessees and lessors recognise,
measure, present and disclose leases. The extract in this batch is a draft of the standard
(source file modified 2 October 2022).

## Core Principle — Lessee Accounting

TFRS 16's central change is the **single lessee model**: a lessee recognises, for almost
all leases, a **right-of-use asset** and a corresponding **lease liability** on the
balance sheet — removing the old operating-lease / finance-lease distinction for lessees.
The standard provides **recognition exemptions** for short-term leases and leases of
low-value assets, which a lessee may elect to keep off balance sheet.

## Structure of the Standard

The standard addresses, in sequence:
- **Identifying a lease** and separating lease components from non-lease components.
- **Lease term** — including options to extend or terminate.
- **Lessee** — initial measurement of the right-of-use asset and lease liability,
  subsequent measurement, presentation and disclosure.
- **Lessor** — classification as finance or operating lease (the lessor distinction is
  retained), recognition, measurement and disclosure.
- **Sale and leaseback** — assessing whether a transfer qualifies as a sale, and the
  accounting that follows.

## Relevance

TFRS 16 affects how leased assets and lease obligations appear on a listed company's
balance sheet — relevant both to financial reporting and, indirectly, to transaction-size
tests under [[material-transactions-rpt-2026-reform]], where entering, amending or
terminating leases of business or assets can itself be a material transaction.
