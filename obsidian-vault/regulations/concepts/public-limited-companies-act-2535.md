# Public Limited Companies Act B.E. 2535 (1992)

## Summary

The **Public Limited Companies Act B.E. 2535** (พระราชบัญญัติบริษัทมหาชนจำกัด พ.ศ. 2535)
is the foundational corporate-law statute for Thai public limited companies — the legal
form every SET-listed company must take. Enacted 29 March 1992, in force 60 days after
publication, it replaced the Public Limited Companies Act B.E. 2521. It governs company
formation, shares and capital, directors and management, shareholder meetings, accounts,
dividends, capital increase/reduction, mergers, and dissolution.

## Amendments

The Act is consolidated with several amending statutes:

| Amendment | Year (B.E.) | Year (CE) |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| No. 2 | 2544 | 2001 |
| No. 3 | 2551 | 2008 |
| **No. 4** | **2565** | **2022** — published in the Government Gazette 23 May 2022; in force the day after publication |

Amendment No. 4 (2022) modernised the Act — including provisions enabling electronic
processes (e.g. additions to Section 6) — reflecting post-pandemic practice for company
administration and shareholder meetings.
