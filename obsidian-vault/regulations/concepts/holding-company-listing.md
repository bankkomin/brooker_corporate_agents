# Holding Company Listing Rules (SET)

## Summary

A **holding company (บริษัทที่ประกอบธุรกิจโดยการถือหุ้น / "Holding Company")** is a company
whose business is conducted through holding shares in operating subsidiaries rather than
directly operating a business. The Stock Exchange of Thailand ([[stock-exchange-of-thailand]])
maintains specific listing criteria for holding companies, organised around the concept of
a **"core company" (บริษัทแกน)** — the principal operating subsidiary whose business
qualifies the group for listing.

## Key Metrics

The SET disclosure rulebook (ข้อบังคับ บจ/ป 11-00) formally defines the terms "conducting
business by holding shares" (ประกอบธุรกิจโดยการถือหุ้น), "core company" (บริษัทแกน), and
"basic infrastructure" (สาธารณูปโภคพื้นฐาน), giving them the same meaning as in the SET rules
on listing ordinary or preferred shares. These definitions were *added* by Amendment No. 7
to that rulebook, dated 11 May 2015, effective **16 May 2015**.

SET circular **BorJor. (Wor) 190/2555** announced a revision to the listing criteria for
holding companies — improving the rules on how a holding company qualifies for listing
through its core company.

## Historical Context

The holding-company listing route has been used by Thai groups to list a parent over a
portfolio of operating subsidiaries. The 2012 circular (BorJor. 190/2555) and the 2015
definitional amendment progressively tightened how the "core company" test applies.
