# Source: SET Cash Company Possible-Delisting Note (Nov 2021)

## Document Overview

**As of:** November 2021 (file timestamped 2022-09-21). A SET reference note titled
*"Entering and clearing possible-delisting status — the case of a company and/or
subsidiaries holding all/substantially all assets as cash or short-term securities (Cash
Company)."* Presented as a structured topic/detail table.

## Key Findings

- Sets out the assessment trigger: a company holding cash/short-term securities as
  substantially all assets for **more than 6 months** after SET receives the
  auditor-reviewed statement of financial position.
- Filing deadline: statement of financial position due within **30 days** of the asset
  disposal.
- Pre-possible-delisting: SET posts **NP** and **C** signs and gives a **6-month** cure
  window.
- Possible-delisting runs in two **1-year** phases (Phase 1 remove the cause, NC + SP
  signs; Phase 2 regain trading qualification), with a one-time **1-year extension**
  available in Phase 2.
- Re-trading qualification thresholds: shareholders' equity ≥ **THB 50m** *or* core-business
  net profit ≥ **THB 10m**; relaxed free-float of **>15%** held by **>150** minor shareholders.

## Relevance

Primary source for the [[cash-company-delisting-risk]] concept. Directly relevant to any
disposal of substantially all operating assets by a listed entity.
