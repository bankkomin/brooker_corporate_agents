# Listing Criteria — SET & mai (เกณฑ์การรับหลักทรัพย์จดทะเบียน)

## Summary

This concept covers the quantitative and qualitative criteria a company must meet to list
its ordinary shares on the **Stock Exchange of Thailand (SET)** or the **Market for
Alternative Investment (mai)**. Two vintages are recorded: the **2004-era criteria** (from
the Listing Criteria reference) and the **revised IPO regulation effective 1 January
2016**, which raised the financial thresholds materially.

## Listing Criteria — 2004 Era (per Listing Criteria.doc)

| Qualification | mai | SET |
|---|---|---|
| Paid-up capital after IPO | ≥ THB 40M, < THB 200M | ≥ THB 200M |
| Shareholders' equity | ≥ THB 40M | ≥ THB 200M |
| Track record | ≥ 2 consecutive years before application | ≥ 2 consecutive years before application |
| Profit | Positive net profit in latest fiscal year (no minimum) | Positive net profit in latest fiscal year (no minimum) |
| Financial statements | Unqualified audited reports | Unqualified audited reports |
| Same management team | BOD + senior management (4 levels down) change < 50% for ≥ 1 year before application | Same |
| Small shareholders | ≥ 300 persons holding ≥ 20% of paid-up capital | ≥ 600 persons holding ≥ 20% of paid-up capital |

## Revised IPO Regulation — Effective 1 January 2016

The 2016 revision (per the IPO regulation spreadsheet) sharply raised the bar:

| Criterion | SET | mai |
|---|---|---|
| Par value | ≥ THB 0.50/share | ≥ THB 0.50/share |
| Paid-up capital after IPO | ≥ THB 300M | ≥ THB 50M (was THB 20M) |
| Shareholders' equity | ≥ THB 300M after IPO, ≥ 0 before | ≥ THB 50M after IPO (was THB 20M), ≥ 0 before |
| Net-profit criterion | Recent 2–3 yrs ≥ THB 50M; most recent year ≥ THB 30M; current-year cumulative > 0 | Most recent year ≥ THB 10M; current-year cumulative > 0 |
| Market-cap criterion (alternative) | ≥ THB 7,500M, with positive EBIT most recent year & cumulative | ≥ THB 1,000M, with positive EBIT most recent year & cumulative |
| Track record | 3 years regardless of market cap | 2 years regardless of market cap |
| IPO offering size | 15% of post-IPO paid-up capital (10% if paid-up ≥ THB 500M) | 15% of post-IPO paid-up capital |

## Going Public

The folder also holds SEC/SET "Going Public" guides (English 2004; Thai 2009) describing
the end-to-end IPO process. The 2004 English guide PDF extract is corrupted by
font-encoding issues and is not machine-readable; it is recorded here as a known process
reference.

## Why It Matters

For a holding company like Brooker, listing criteria define the gate any subsidiary or
new vehicle must pass to access public markets, and the 2016 thresholds set the modern
benchmark. The market-cap alternative is the route used by larger pre-profit companies.
