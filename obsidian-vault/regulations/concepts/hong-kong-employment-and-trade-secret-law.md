# Hong Kong Employment, Foreign Labour & Trade-Secret Law

## Summary

This article covers a small cross-border reference set on **Hong Kong (ฮ่องกง) law** —
Hong Kong SAR employment law, the regime for importing foreign workers, and trade-secret
protection. These are foreign-jurisdiction reference materials kept alongside the Thai
SEC/SET regulatory archive, dated 2013, useful where Brooker considers a Hong Kong
presence or workforce.

## Foreign Worker Importation

The Hong Kong government classifies foreign-worker admission into three categories. The
**professional employment** category comprises three schemes:

- **IANG** (Immigration Arrangements for Non-local Graduates) — non-local students who
  completed a full-time degree-level or higher programme in Hong Kong.
- **GEP** (General Employment Policy) — persons with knowledge, experience and special
  skills not available in the local workforce, **not** from Mainland China.
- **ASMTP** (Admission Scheme for Mainland Talents and Professionals) — the equivalent for
  skilled persons **from Mainland China**.

IANG, GEP and ASMTP admissions are **not subject to quota** and not restricted by
occupation, extending beyond business/finance professionals to arts, culture, sport and
culinary specialists. Workers from certain countries are not admissible (the source lists
Afghanistan, Albania, Cambodia, Cuba, Laos, the Republic of Korea, Nepal and Vietnam).

## Employment Law — Foreign Domestic Helpers

The Hong Kong employment-law extract focuses on the **Standard Employment Contract
(ID 407)** for **foreign domestic helpers**. On the helper's arrival the employer must,
among other duties: pay a travel allowance of **HKD 100**; explain the helper's duties and
the contract's accommodation/work-schedule terms; notify the wage payment date; set a
weekly rest day; and decide whether to provide food (if provided, it must be free).

## Trade-Secret Law

The third extract covers Hong Kong SAR **trade-secret (ความลับทางการค้า)** protection. The
extract text is garbled by font-encoding issues and only the document's table of contents
is recoverable — it addresses the definition of a trade secret, breach of confidence, and
related remedies under Hong Kong common law.

## Why It Matters

These are out-of-jurisdiction materials. They are relevant only if Brooker establishes a
Hong Kong entity, employs staff there, or needs Hong Kong trade-secret protection. They do
not affect Thai listed-company compliance.
