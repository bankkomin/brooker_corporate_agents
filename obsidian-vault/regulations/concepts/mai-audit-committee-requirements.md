# MAI Audit Committee and Independent Director Requirements

## Summary

Two 1999 SET notifications set the audit-committee and independent-director requirements
for companies listed on the **Market for Alternative Investment (MAI)**: the
*Notification Re: Audit Committee of Listed Companies in MAI (No.1), 1999* and the
*Independent Director Notification (No.1), 1999*. Both came into force on **9 July 1999**,
issued under the SET MAI listing regulations, to promote good corporate governance on the
secondary board.

## Audit Committee Composition

An audit committee of a MAI company (applicant or listed) must:

- Consist of company **directors**, appointed by the board or the shareholders' meeting.
- Have **no fewer than 3 persons**, of whom **at least 2** must meet the independence
  qualifications in Clause 6.
- After the company's ordinary shares have traded on MAI for **3 years**, **all** audit-
  committee members must meet the Clause 6 qualifications.

The company reports the appointment resolution and files the audit committee's name list
and scope of work with SET, and must submit a certified letter and résumé for each member
**within 3 days** of the appointing resolution.

## Independence Qualification (Clause 6)

A qualifying audit-committee member must, among other things, hold **no more than 5%** of
the paid-up capital of the company, its parent, subsidiary, affiliate or related company —
counting shares of close relatives. "Close relatives" covers relationships by blood,
marriage and legal registration; "related company" follows Section 258(3)–(7) of the
Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535.

## Relevance

Baseline governance rules for any group entity listed (or seeking listing) on MAI. These
1999 rules have since been supplemented by later SEC/SET governance requirements; treat
them as the historical foundation of the MAI audit-committee regime.

## Agent Notes

These are early-vintage rules (1999). The 5% holding cap and 3-member / 2-independent
structure are the durable core; current independence thresholds and director-tenure rules
should be checked against present SET corporate-governance requirements.
