# Source — SET Delisting of Listed Securities Regulation B.E. 2542

## Document Overview

The SET Board regulation "Delisting of Listed Securities B.E. 2542" (บจ/พ 01-00), issued
under Section 170 paragraph two (1) of the Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535 with the
consent of the SEC. Effective **16 December 1999**. The extract (modified 2017-03-07) is the
consolidated text incorporating amendments through No. 11 of B.E. 2558 (effective 16 May
2015). The folder includes both the Thai consolidated text and an English-language version.
This is the predecessor regime to the [[set-delisting-regulation-2564]].

## Key Findings

- **Two delisting routes (Clause 3):** (1) delisting of *shares* — governed by Chapters 1
  (company-requested / voluntary) and 2 (SET-initiated); (2) delisting of non-share listed
  securities, at the SET Board's discretion. When ordinary shares are delisted, preferred
  shares and convertible securities of the same company cease to be listed the same day.
- **Voluntary delisting procedure (Chapter 1, Clause 4):** A company seeking to delist its
  own shares must — appoint a financial advisor with independent-director consent to advise
  uninvolved shareholders; notify the SET of the board resolution within the prescribed
  time; cooperate with the FA; arrange a tender offeror for shares and convertible
  securities; and convene a shareholder meeting (notice ≥ 14 days) with disclosure of
  facts/reasons, independent-director opinion, FA opinion, the offer price, and Form 56-1
  company information.
- **Shareholder approval (Clause 5):** The delisting resolution requires votes of
  **≥ 3/4 of all issued and sold shares**, and **objecting shareholders must not exceed 10%**
  of issued shares.

## Relevance

Establishes the procedural backbone — FA opinion, tender offer, supermajority vote, 10%
objection cap — that voluntary delistings of Brooker portfolio companies follow. Superseded
for current matters by the B.E. 2564 regulation but still the reference for the legacy
framework.
