# Share-Based Payment — TFRS 2 (การจ่ายโดยใช้หุ้นเป็นเกณฑ์)

## Summary

**TFRS 2** (มาตรฐานการรายงานทางการเงิน ฉบับที่ 2 — *Share-Based Payment*) is the Thai
Financial Reporting Standard governing how an entity recognises **share-based payment**
transactions in its financial statements — payments to employees or others made in the
form of cash, other assets, or the entity's own equity instruments. TFRS 2 is the Thai
adoption of **IFRS 2** and is issued by the Federation of Accounting Professions
(สภาวิชาชีพบัญชี). This article covers the standard's scope and the substantive changes in
its recent revisions.

## Scope

TFRS 2 applies to **all share-based payment transactions**, whether or not the entity can
identify the goods or services received, including:

- **Equity-settled** share-based payment transactions (ชำระด้วยตราสารทุน)
- **Cash-settled** share-based payment transactions (ชำระด้วยเงินสด)
- Transactions where the agreement gives the entity or counterparty a **choice** of cash
  or equity settlement

It also applies to group situations where the share-based payment is settled by another
entity in the group (or by the group's shareholders) on behalf of the entity receiving the
goods or services.

## Key Revisions

The source manual explains the substantive changes from earlier editions. **TFRS 2
(revised 2561 / 2018)** changed the accounting versus TFRS 2 (revised 2560 / 2017) by:

- Adding a method for **vesting and non-vesting conditions** (เงื่อนไขการได้รับสิทธิ) in
  **cash-settled** share-based payments.
- Adding treatment for share-based payment with a **net-settlement feature** for
  withholding-tax obligations — where the arrangement permits or requires the entity to
  withhold equity instruments equal to the employee's income-tax liability.
- Adding treatment for a **modification that reclassifies** a share-based payment from
  cash-settled to equity-settled.

The source pack also includes the **TFRS 2 (revised 2565 / 2022)** standard text.

## Fair Value Caveat

TFRS 2 uses the term **"fair value" (มูลค่ายุติธรรม)** in a sense **different from** the
definition in TFRS 13 (*Fair Value Measurement*). When applying TFRS 2, an entity must
measure fair value according to TFRS 2's own requirements, not TFRS 13's.

## Why It Matters

For a Brooker-type holding company, TFRS 2 governs the accounting for any ESOP, employee
share schemes, or warrants issued as consideration for goods or services — a recurring
item in capital-structure and compensation planning. Mis-classifying a scheme as
equity- versus cash-settled changes the expense profile and the balance sheet.
