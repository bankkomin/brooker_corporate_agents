# Infrastructure Trust (Infra Trust) — SET Listing Regime

## Summary

An **Infrastructure Trust** (ทรัสต์เพื่อการลงทุนในโครงสร้างพื้นฐาน — *Infra Trust*) is a
trust-based fundraising vehicle that invests in infrastructure assets. To raise money
from the general public, an Infra Trust must obtain SEC Office approval and list its
**trust units** (หน่วยทรัสต์) on the Stock Exchange of Thailand.

## Eligible Infrastructure Assets

The trust's infrastructure business assets may include:

- Rail or pipeline transport systems
- Electricity and water supply
- Roads, expressways, or concession roads
- Airports
- Deep-sea ports
- Telecommunications / ICT infrastructure
- Alternative energy
- Water-management or irrigation systems
- Natural-disaster prevention systems (including warning and mitigation systems)
- Waste-management systems
- **Multi-infrastructure** combinations — where the combined businesses are
  interconnected/mutually beneficial (or serve the same/nearby community) **and**
  generate not less than **80% of total revenue** of the combined operations.

## Key Requirements

| Item | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Trust units | Fully paid; registered holder named; freely transferable except limits set by law and stated in the trust deed |
| Approval | SEC Office approval required |
| Trust manager | A trust manager (ผู้จัดการกองทรัสต์) approved by the SEC Office |
| Trustee | A duly approved trustee |
| Listing | Trust units listed on the SET |

## Why It Matters

The Infra Trust is a recognised structure for monetising or pooling infrastructure
assets with outside investor capital. It is one of several fund/trust structures
relevant to Brooker's capital-structuring options, and parallels the
[[infrastructure-company-listing]] route for operating-company listings.
