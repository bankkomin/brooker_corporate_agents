# Converting a Private Company to a Public Limited Company (การแปรสภาพเป็นบริษัทมหาชน)

## Summary

A **Public Limited Company** (บริษัทมหาชนจำกัด) is a company formed to offer shares to the
public, with shareholder liability limited to the unpaid amount on shares. It is governed
by the **Public Limited Company Act B.E. 2535 (1992)** and registered with the **Department
of Business Development (DBD)**. A private company may be **converted** into a public
limited company. This concept summarises the structural requirements, the conversion
mechanism, and the registration timelines, per the DBD reference dated December 2017.

## Structure of a Public Limited Company

Under the PLC Act B.E. 2535:

- **Shareholders** — at least **15 persons**.
- **Registered capital** — **no minimum amount**.
- **Shares** — each share must have the **same par value** and the share price must be
  **fully paid at one time**.
- **Directors** — at least **5 persons**, and **at least half** must have a registered
  address in Thailand.

## How Conversion Happens

A private company is converted to a public limited company when the **shareholders'
meeting passes an extraordinary resolution** under the Civil and Commercial Code. Two or
more public limited companies, or a public and a private company, may also **merge** into
a public limited company — each company's shareholders' meeting must approve by at least
three-quarters of the votes of attending shareholders entitled to vote (a merging private
company requires an extraordinary resolution).

After conversion the **newly appointed board must register the transformation within 14
days** of the meeting at which conversion was resolved.

## Key Registration Timelines

| Action | Deadline |
|--------|----------|
| Submit prospectus to the registrar | 15 days from submission to the SEC |
| Send meeting invitation/agenda to registrar before establishment meeting | at least 7 days before the meeting |
| Register company establishment | within 3 months of establishment |
| Register changes to paid-up capital, directors, authority, offices | within 14 days of the change |
| Register capital increase/decrease resolution | within 14 days of the resolution |
| Register capital decrease (paid-up) | within 14 days after the creditor-objection period |
| Register a merger | within 14 days of the meeting |
| Issue share certificates to shareholders | within 2 months of registration / full payment |

## Ongoing Duties of a Public Limited Company

- File the shareholder list within **1 month** of the annual meeting.
- File the annual report, financial statements and AGM report within **1 month** of the
  meeting approving the financials, and advertise in a newspaper for at least 1 day.
- Maintain shareholders', directors' and meeting registers at the head office.
- Have financial statements audited by a CPA and submitted to the shareholders' meeting.
- Newspaper-advertise meeting invitations, dividend payments, approved financial
  statements, and post-registration capital increases/decreases.

## Registration Fees (DBD, 2017)

Registering the memorandum of association or its modification: THB 1,000 per THB 1,000,000
of capital, capped at THB 25,000; company-establishment registration capped at
THB 250,000; **registering the transformation of a private company** capped at
**THB 10,000**; merger registration THB 5,000.

## Why It Matters

Conversion to a public limited company is the prerequisite legal step before a company can
make a public offering and seek a SET listing. The 15-shareholder / 5-director thresholds
and the 14-day post-resolution registration deadline are hard gating items for any
group-restructuring or pre-IPO timeline.
