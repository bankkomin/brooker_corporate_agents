# Soil & Groundwater Contamination Control in Factories — B.E. 2559

## Summary

Thai law requires certain higher-risk factory categories to test for **heavy-metal and
other contamination in soil and groundwater** within the factory premises, on a strict
basis. The regime rests on two B.E. 2559 (2016) instruments:

1. **Ministerial Regulation on Control of Soil and Groundwater Contamination within
   Factory Premises, B.E. 2559** (กฎกระทรวง ควบคุมการปนเปื้อนในดินและน้ำใต้ดินภายใน
   บริเวณโรงงาน พ.ศ. 2559).
2. **Ministry of Industry Notification setting the contamination criteria, soil and
   groundwater quality testing, data notification and reporting, B.E. 2559**.

## Key Points

### Factories within scope (higher-risk categories)

- Non-ferrous metal basic industries — smelting, mixing, refining, melting, casting,
  rolling, drawing or primary metal production (e.g. copper, zinc, lead, aluminium).
- Chemicals / chemical substances or chemical materials (other than fertiliser).
- Petroleum refineries.
- Paint, varnish, shellac, lacquer or related products.
- Electrical-equipment factories — lamps, light fittings, electrical insulators (not
  ceramic/glass), and accumulators/batteries (wet or dry) and their parts.
- Product-finishing / surface-treatment factories (category 100) — including metal
  plating and coating using heavy-metal chemicals.
- Combined waste-treatment factories (category 101).
- Other potentially at-risk categories — e.g. textile/yarn/fibre (non-asbestos) and
  pulp & paper factories.

### Contaminants to be tested

Two main groups: **Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs)** and **heavy metals** (e.g.
arsenic, cadmium, chromium, lead, mercury, nickel, zinc).

### Obligation

In-scope factories must **collect samples, analyse them per the prescribed criteria,
and prepare a soil-and-groundwater quality inspection report** to assess risk and
remediate where contamination exceeds the standard.
