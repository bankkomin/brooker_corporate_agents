# Thai SEC Utility Token Regulation (Ready-to-Use Tokens)

## Summary

In August 2024 the Thai SEC (ก.ล.ต.) overhauled how it regulates **ready-to-use
utility tokens** (utility token พร้อมใช้) — digital tokens already usable for their
stated purpose at the time of sale. The package of **seven Capital Market Supervisory
Board notifications** was published in the Royal Gazette and took effect **13 August
2024**, with the exchange-supervision uplift phasing in 90–180 days after each related
notification. The stated goal: adequate buyer protection without obstructing
technology adoption and the national digital-economy policy.

## Two Token Groups

The rule splits ready-to-use utility tokens by primary purpose:

- **Group 1** — tokens whose main purpose is **consumption** (อุปโภคบริโภค), or that
  act as a **certificate** or proxy for a right (e.g. loyalty / marketing-promotion
  tokens, in-game items). **Exempt from offering approval.**
- **Group 2** — all other ready-to-use utility tokens. If the issuer intends to
  **list** the token on a SEC-supervised digital asset exchange, it must obtain
  **SEC offering approval**, file a registration statement (แบบ filing) and draft
  prospectus, and sell **through a SEC-approved ICO portal**.

Across **all** utility tokens, the issuer must not offer **staking** (รับฝากสินทรัพย์
ดิจิทัลเพื่อให้สิทธิประโยชน์) except as specifically permitted, and the token must not
function as a **Means of Payment (MOP)** as defined by the Bank of Thailand.

## Secondary Market

Servicing Group 1 utility tokens (ready-to-use or not) is **not** treated as a digital
asset business — so digital asset exchanges, brokers and dealers are **prohibited** from
servicing them under their digital asset licence. A licensed operator wishing to service
Group 1 tokens as a separate line of business must use a **separate legal entity** and
must not present itself as a SEC-supervised digital asset operator.

## Token Listing & Delisting Criteria (กธ. 22/2567 Appendix 3)

Exchanges must set listing rules that **exclude**: meme tokens (no clear substance,
price driven by social trends), fan tokens, and tokens issued by the exchange itself or
its related parties (directors, executives, spouses, controlled entities, parent /
subsidiary / associate companies). Tokens sold at a **price below the public offering
price** must carry a **silent period of at least 6 months** from delivery. Delisting
triggers and ongoing issuer disclosure (revenue-impacting events, rehabilitation,
takeover, default, white-paper amendments — disclosed at least 15 days in advance) are
also mandated.

## Why It Matters

Brooker's digital asset strategy (see [[fireblocks]], [[binance-bnb-otc]]) is exposure-
focused rather than token-issuance, so this rule mainly bounds what tokens a Thai-
regulated venue may list and how any future Brooker-linked token offering would be
structured. The MOP and staking prohibitions are the key design constraints for any
token product.

Effective date is **13 Aug 2024**. Any proposal involving a Thai-listed token must check
Group 1 vs Group 2 classification, the silent-period rule for low-price tokens, and the
related-party listing prohibition.
