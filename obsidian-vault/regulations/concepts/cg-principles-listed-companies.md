# Corporate Governance Principles for Listed Companies (Thailand)

## Summary

The **Principles of Good Corporate Governance for Listed Companies** (หลักการกำกับดูแล
กิจการที่ดีสำหรับบริษัทจดทะเบียน), issued by the Stock Exchange of Thailand's CG
development centre, define corporate governance as the system of structures and
processes governing the relationship between the **board of directors, management and
shareholders**, intended to build competitiveness, sustainable growth, and long-term
value for shareholders while considering other stakeholders. The project archive holds
the **2006 (B.E. 2549)** edition and a body of accompanying SET/IOD practitioner
guides. (SET later issued a CG Code aligned with international standards; this folder
reflects the 2006 framework.)

## Key Points

- CG is presented as a value-creation tool — an effective, transparent, accountable
  management system that builds confidence among shareholders, investors and
  stakeholders, and is consistent with the Sufficiency Economy philosophy.
- SET pursues an ongoing program to help every listed company raise its CG to
  internationally comparable standards.
- The 2006 principles are organised into the standard five categories of listed-
  company governance: rights of shareholders; equitable treatment of shareholders;
  role of stakeholders; disclosure and transparency; and responsibilities of the
  board.

## Companion Practitioner Guides (same folder)

The "CG Thailand's Publications" folder bundles a large set of SET/IOD guidance
material that operationalises the principles:

- **Investor guide** to listed-company CG (คู่มือสำหรับนักลงทุน).
- **AGM Checklist** — guide to convening shareholder meetings.
- **Directors' Manuals 1–3** — (1) roles, duties and responsibilities of directors
  and the board; (2) guidelines for director practice; (3) protecting shareholder
  rights and convening shareholder meetings.
- **Good practice of the Audit Committee** — see [[audit-committee]].
- Good-practice guides for **director remuneration** and **director nomination**,
  plus supplementary notes on remuneration and nomination committees.
- An **Internal Control Checklist**, a **board self-assessment** form, the **2006
  Best Practice** document, an **IOD Handbook 2015**, and CG self-assessment forms.

## Historical Context

This 2006 framework is the foundation document for Thai listed-company governance
practice; it predates SET's later CG Code. The board self-assessment, internal-control
checklist and AGM checklist remain practical compliance tools for listed entities.
