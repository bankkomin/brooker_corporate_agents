# Transferable Subscription Rights (TSR)

## Summary

A **Transferable Subscription Right (TSR)** is an instrument a SET-listed company issues
to all its shareholders when it raises capital through a **rights offering (RO)**. Unlike
a plain rights offering, the subscription right itself is *tradable* on the Stock Exchange
of Thailand: a shareholder who does not wish to subscribe to the new shares can sell the
right to another investor rather than letting it lapse. Holders are issued TSRs in
proportion to their existing shareholding (e.g. 1 TSR per 10 shares held), and 1 TSR
subscribes for 1 new ordinary or preferred share.

TSR trading is governed by the SET notification *Trading of Transferable Subscription
Right (No. 1), 1999* (Bor.Sor./Saw. 01-08), in force from 31 March 1999.

## How It Works — Key Rules

- **Term:** the TSR term must not exceed **2 months**, running from the issue date (when
  the Thailand Securities Depository deposits TSRs into shareholder accounts) to the
  exercise date.
- **Trading period:** not less than **7 business days** on the Exchange.
- **Subscription period:** not less than **15 days** before the last subscription date.
- **Exercise ratio:** 1 TSR per 1 ordinary or preferred share.
- **Symbol:** the issuer's stock symbol followed by "-T#" (e.g. `XXX-T1`), max 8 characters.
- **Boards:** tradable on the main board (board lot of 100 TSRs; max 1,000,000 TSRs per
  bid/offer), the odd lot board and the big lot board.
- **Price limits:** ceiling/floor set as for warrants — change of up to 30% of the
  issuer's last ordinary-share price multiplied by the rights subscribed in one exercise.
- The Exchange suspends TSR trading for **3 days** before the share subscription and
  payment date to fix the eligible-holder list.

## Approvals and Filings

- The capital increase needs approval by **at least 3/4** of shareholders voting and
  present (in person or by proxy).
- A company may offer TSRs within **one year** of the shareholder resolution, without SEC
  approval and without using a financial advisor if it so chooses.
- Issuance does not require SEC permission, but the company must report TSR and share
  offering results (Form 53-5) to the SEC within **15 days** of the subscription period
  ending, and register the paid-up capital increase with the Ministry of Commerce within
  **14 days**.
- An issuer **must not** be in delisting, business reorganisation under bankruptcy law, or
  have its securities suspended at the time of issuing TSRs.

## Why It Matters

TSRs reduce the dilution and price-volatility impact of a capital increase: existing
shareholders are at least partly compensated through TSR sale proceeds, while new
investors can acquire shares below market. SET waives its listing fees for TSRs.
