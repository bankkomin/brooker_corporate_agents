# Building Control Act B.E. 2522 (1979)

## Summary

The **Building Control Act B.E. 2522** (พระราชบัญญัติควบคุมอาคาร พ.ศ. 2522) is
Thailand's principal statute governing the construction, alteration, demolition, and use
of buildings. Given 8 May 1979, it consolidated several earlier construction-control and
fire-zone laws into a single Act. It is **not** a capital-markets law, but is included
in the regulatory reference set because building compliance bears on the valuation,
permitting, and risk profile of real-estate and infrastructure assets.

## Scope

- The Act applies in localities designated by Royal Decree, and **automatically** —
  without a Royal Decree — wherever a comprehensive town plan (ผังเมืองรวม) is in force.
- For **high-rise buildings, extra-large buildings, places of public assembly, and
  theatres** (อาคารสูง อาคารขนาดใหญ่พิเศษ อาคารชุมนุมคน และโรงมหรสพ), the Act applies
  regardless of whether a Royal Decree covers the locality.
- It repealed the Building Construction Control Acts of B.E. 2479 and B.E. 2504, the
  Revolutionary Council Announcement No. 192 (1972), and the fire-zone construction
  Acts of B.E. 2476 and B.E. 2496.
- A companion document, *Standards for Control of Building Use* (มาตรฐานการควบคุม
  การใช้อาคาร), addresses building-use control standards.

## Why It Matters

Where Brooker holds, develops, or invests in property or infrastructure assets,
compliance with the Building Control Act (permits, occupancy classification, safety
standards) is a due-diligence and ongoing-risk item. Building-use category affects
permitted operations and insurance/liability exposure.
