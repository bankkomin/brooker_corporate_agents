# Maintenance of Listed-Company Status — SET Regulation B.E. 2544

## Summary

The SET Regulation on the **Maintenance of Listed-Company Status in the Exchange,
B.E. 2544** (การดำรงสถานะเป็นบริษัทจดทะเบียนในตลาดฯ พ.ศ. 2544) sets the **ongoing
qualifications and obligations** a company must continue to satisfy to remain a listed
company on the Stock Exchange of Thailand, after admission. The project archive holds
the consolidated text incorporating amendments through circa B.E. 2555 (2012).

## Key Points

- The regulation addresses continuing requirements on a listed company's board and
  management — including the conduct, qualifications and obligations of **directors,
  executives and controlling persons**, and arrangements where a listed company is a
  foreign company with a Secondary Listing and a separate Home Exchange.
- It complements the delisting regime: failure to maintain the required status can put
  a company into a possible-delisting cause.
