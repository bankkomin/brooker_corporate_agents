# SET Capital Reduction Procedure

## Summary

Capital reduction (การลดทุน) is a tool a listed company uses to right-size its capital —
most often to **eliminate accumulated losses**, **return capital to shareholders**, or
**restructure its capital base**. Because reducing capital directly affects shareholder
rights and the share price, SET imposes a defined disclosure-and-approval procedure. The
reference summary in this batch is dated 7 March 2025 (the underlying SET guide is dated
March 2024).

## Two Forms of Reduction

1. **Reducing par value** (การลดมูลค่าที่ตราไว้ต่อหุ้น) — lower the par per share.
2. **Reducing the number of shares** (การลดจำนวนหุ้น) — cancel shares.

## Procedure and Disclosure

- **Board resolution** — the company must disclose the board's capital-reduction
  resolution **immediately** via SETLink — by the resolution date, or at the latest before
  the first trading session of the next business day.
- **Disclosure content** includes: the resolution date; the shareholders' meeting schedule
  and record date / book-closing date; the memorandum-of-association amendment; the form
  of reduction; and (for a par reduction) par before and after, total capital reduced and
  purpose, or (for a share-count reduction) the affected shareholders, number of shares
  cancelled, record date, and the reduction ratio (old:new). A reduction returning capital
  to shareholders must additionally disclose the cash amount returned, the entitled-holder
  record date, and the payment date. Fractional-share treatment must be specified.
- **Shareholder approval** — requires a meeting resolution of **not less than 3/4** of the
  votes of shareholders present and eligible to vote.
- **Creditor objection** — the company must notify its creditors of the reduction
  resolution within **14 days** of the meeting, giving creditors **2 months** to object,
  and must advertise the resolution in a newspaper within 14 days. After the 2-month
  period, the company discloses the objection outcome via SETLink.

## Share Premium

A related Department of Business Development (DBD) ruling in this batch addresses **share
premium** (ส่วนเกินมูลค่าหุ้น) in the context of capital restructuring — relevant where a
company offsets accumulated losses against premium before or alongside a capital
reduction.

## Agent Notes

The two gating numbers — a **3/4 shareholder vote** and a **2-month creditor objection
window** — are the durable core. Any Brooker capital-reduction proposal must build the
2-month creditor window plus the 14-day notification and advertising steps into its
timeline.
