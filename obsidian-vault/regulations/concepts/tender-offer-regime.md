# Thai Tender Offer Regime (Securities Acquisition for Business Takeovers)

## Summary

A **tender offer** (คำเสนอซื้อหลักทรัพย์) is a formal, regulated offer to buy
securities of a Thai listed company. The governing instrument is **Notification
TorChor. 12/2554** (ที่ ทจ. 12/2554) "Re: Rules, Conditions and Procedures for the
Acquisition of Securities for Business Takeovers" (as amended). It is administered by
the Thai SEC ([[thai-sec]]) and its **Takeover Panel**, with filings made on **Form
247-4**. This article reflects the regime as compiled by counsel as of **4 August 2025**.

## When a Tender Offer Is Mandatory

A **mandatory tender offer** (MTO) is triggered when an acquirer crosses a control
threshold in a listed company — reaching or passing **25%, 50% or 75%** of total voting
rights obligates an offer for **all** remaining securities at the relevant trigger
point. A **voluntary tender offer** (VTO) may be launched without crossing a threshold.

## The Chain Principle

The **Chain Principle** (หลักการ Chain) extends the obligation: if an acquirer gains
significant control (≥50% of voting rights) of an **upstream holding company** that
itself holds a substantial stake in a listed business, the acquirer is deemed to have
acquired control of the **downstream** listed company and must make a tender offer for
its securities too. The Takeover Panel / SEC may grant **waivers** of the chain-triggered
obligation and of the obligation to pre-fund such an offer.

## Offer Price Determination

The offer price must be the **highest** of prescribed reference prices. Where control is
acquired indirectly, the rule uses the **"acquisition cost of controlling interest
through another entity"** method — back-calculating the per-share price implied by the
price paid for the holding company. Offerees typically bear a brokerage fee (e.g. 0.25%)
plus VAT, reducing the net price received.

## Worked Example — GULF / INTUCH / ADVANC (2021)

The GULF Energy voluntary tender offer for **ADVANC** (Form 247-4, 28 June 2021)
illustrates the Chain Principle. GULF intended to acquire **INTUCH** (which held 40.45%
of ADVANC); reaching ≥50% of INTUCH voting rights triggered a chain obligation toward
ADVANC. GULF launched both offers on the same business day. The ADVANC offer price of
**THB 120.93 per share** was derived from the Chain Principle calculation: INTUCH's
equity-method investment in ADVANC (69.78% of INTUCH total equity) applied to INTUCH's
THB 65.00 offer-price valuation, divided by ADVANC shares held by INTUCH. See
[[source-gulf-advanc-tender-offer]].

## Why It Matters

Any Brooker move that would cross a control threshold in a Thai listed company — or any
acquisition of a holding vehicle above a listed company — must be screened against the
MTO triggers and the Chain Principle, and budgeted at the regulated offer price.
