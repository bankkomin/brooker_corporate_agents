# Audit Committee of Listed Companies (คณะกรรมการตรวจสอบ)

## Summary

The **audit committee** (คณะกรรมการตรวจสอบ) is a board-level committee that Thai listed
companies must establish as a core mechanism of good corporate governance. The Thai SEC
(ก.ล.ต.) publishes an **Audit Committee Handbook** (คู่มือการปฏิบัติงานของคณะกรรมการ
ตรวจสอบ) setting out the committee's structure, qualifications, and duties. This concept
is built from the 2553 (2010) and 2557 (2014) editions of that handbook.

## Mandate and Legal Basis

The audit committee's existence and broad mandate derive from the **Securities and
Exchange Act B.E. 2535**. The SEC's Code of Governance frames it as one of the principal
mechanisms of corporate-governance oversight. Failure to appoint an audit committee within
the required time is itself a **ground for possible delisting** (see [[delisting-criteria]]).

## Structure and Composition

- **Appointment** of audit committee members, their **qualifications**, **term of office**,
  and grounds for **vacating office** are prescribed by SEC and SET rules.
- The committee has a designated **chairman** and a **secretary**.
- New audit committee members receive an onboarding information pack.

## Core Duties and Responsibilities

The handbook organises the committee's responsibilities into the following areas:

- **A. Internal control & risk management** — reviewing the adequacy of the internal
  control system and risk management.
- **B. Compliance with laws, rules & ethics** — reviewing compliance with regulations and
  laws, compliance with the code of ethics, the employee code of conduct, and the
  whistle-blowing (รับแจ้งเบาะแส) process.
- **C. Financial reporting** — reviewing financial reports, the relationship with the
  external auditor, and the auditor's management letter.
- **D. Relationship with management.**
- **E. Relationship with internal audit** — reviewing internal audit independence,
  approving the internal-audit plan, receiving and considering audit results, assessing
  the adequacy of internal-audit resources, and evaluating the internal-audit function and
  its head.

## Effectiveness Factors

The handbook lists factors that support an effective audit committee: a formal **audit
committee charter** (กฎบัตร), regular **committee self-evaluation**, adequate **resources**,
and ongoing **development and training**. Meeting practice — agenda planning, frequency,
quorum, document distribution, minutes — is also prescribed.

## Why It Matters

For a Brooker-type listed company, the audit committee is the governance body that signs
off internal control, financial reporting integrity, and related-party / connected-
transaction review. Its proper constitution is both a compliance obligation and a
delisting-risk safeguard.
