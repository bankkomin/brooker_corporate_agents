# Material Transaction (MT) & Related Party Transaction (RPT) Regime — 2026 Reform

## Summary

The Thai SEC overhauled the rules governing **Material Transactions** (รายการที่มี
นัยสำคัญ — acquisition/disposal of significant assets) and **Related Party Transactions**
(รายการที่เกี่ยวโยงกัน — connected transactions) of listed companies. The reform was
announced in SEC News No. 16/2569 (19 January 2026) and **takes effect 1 July 2026**
(1 กรกฎาคม 2569). It is delivered through three Capital Market Supervisory Board
notifications dated 19 December 2025: **ทจ. 45/2568** (MT), **ทจ. 46/2568** (RPT), and
amended **Form 56-1 One Report** (Section 1, item 1.1.5).

## What the Reform Does

The SEC frames the changes under its "Strong Issuer" (บริษัทผู้ออกหลักทรัพย์เข้มแข็ง)
programme, emphasising preventive regulation. Three thrusts:

1. **Clarity & alignment with current practice** — revised methods for calculating and
   aggregating transaction size, recalibrated duties by transaction-size band, and
   updated definitions.
2. **Stronger investor-protection mechanisms** —
   - Shareholders gain an explicit right to **vote against** a transaction when the
     Audit Committee or the Independent Financial Advisor (IFA / ที่ปรึกษาทางการเงิน
     อิสระ) opines that shareholders should not approve it.
   - Listed companies must **report progress** on transactions to investors.
   - The SEC may apply **substance over form** (เนื้อหาสาระที่แท้จริง) — treating a
     transaction as an MT/RPT where its real substance is comparable, to prevent
     circumvention.
3. **Reduced burden / added flexibility** — exemptions for certain intra-group MTs
   (between a listed parent and a subsidiary, or between subsidiaries), and the option
   to obtain advance **framework/principle approval** from shareholders, delegating
   execution within that mandate to the board (for time-sensitive cases such as bids
   or tenders where normal pre-disclosure could materially harm the company).

## Key Thresholds (MT)

Transaction value is the **highest** result from the calculation methods in Appendix 1,
using the latest audited/reviewed (consolidated) financial statements.

| Band | Trigger |
|------|---------|
| **≥10%** | For a company with negative net tangible assets or operating losses, where the transaction may negatively affect its financial position/performance |
| **≥25%** | For any other company |
| Aggregation | Related transactions or transactions under the same project within the prior **12 months** are counted as one (excluding any already approved by shareholders) |

**Shareholder approval** of an MT requires not less than **3/4 of votes** of shareholders
attending and entitled to vote, excluding interested shareholders. An **IFA opinion** is
required for MTs ≥25% (item 8(1) cases) and ≥50% (item 8(2) cases). Where the Audit
Committee or IFA advises against the transaction, opposing shareholders holding ≥10% of
voting rights present can block it.

## Why It Matters

Brooker's investment-company structure routinely involves significant asset
acquisitions/disposals and intra-group transactions. From 1 July 2026 the heightened
calculation, aggregation, IFA, and shareholder-veto rules — plus the SEC's
substance-over-form power — materially raise the disclosure and approval bar. Any
proposed transaction near the 10%/25%/50% bands must be screened against the new rules.
