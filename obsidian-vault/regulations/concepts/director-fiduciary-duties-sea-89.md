# Director & Executive Fiduciary Duties — SEA Sections 89/7–89/13 (หน้าที่กรรมการ)

## Summary

The **Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535**, as amended by **Act No. 4 B.E. 2551 (2008)**,
codifies the fiduciary duties of directors and executives of listed companies in
**Sections 89/7–89/13**. These provisions impose a **duty of care** and a **duty of
loyalty**, create a **statutory presumption** that certain self-dealing acts conflict with
the company's interest, and require **shareholder approval** for transactions between the
company (or its subsidiary) and connected persons. This concept summarises those sections
together with the key definitions used to apply them.

## Duty of Care (Section 89/8–89/9)

Directors and executives must perform their duties **with responsibility, due care, and
caution** (ด้วยความรับผิดชอบ ความระมัดระวัง). In assessing whether a director or executive
met this standard, the following factors are weighed:

1. the **position** the person held at the relevant time;
2. the **scope of responsibility** of that position, as set by law or delegated by the
   board; and
3. the person's **qualifications, knowledge, ability and experience**, and the **purpose
   of the appointment**.

## Duty of Loyalty (Section 89/10)

In performing duties with honesty and integrity (ความซื่อสัตย์สุจริต), directors and
executives must:

1. act in **good faith for the best interest of the company**;
2. act with a **proper and appropriate purpose**; and
3. **not act in a manner that significantly conflicts** with the company's interest.

## Presumed Conflicts of Interest (Section 89/11)

The following acts are **presumed** to significantly conflict with the company's interest
where they cause a director, executive or related person to receive an abnormal financial
benefit or cause the company loss:

1. transacting between the company / subsidiary and a director, executive or related
   person **otherwise than in accordance with Section 89/12 or 89/13**;
2. using the company's **non-public information**; and
3. using the company's **assets or business opportunities** in breach of rules or general
   practice set by the Capital Market Supervisory Board.

## Connected Transactions (Sections 89/12–89/13)

A director, executive or related person may transact with the company or its subsidiary
**only with shareholders' meeting approval**, unless the transaction falls within an
exemption under Section 89/12:

1. a **normal commercial-terms** transaction a reasonable person would make with a general
   counterparty, approved by the board or within board-approved principles;
2. a **loan under the staff-welfare scheme**;
3. a transaction where the counterparty is a subsidiary the company holds **≥ 90%** of, or
   a subsidiary in which the connected person's interest does not exceed limits set by the
   Capital Market Supervisory Board; or
4. a transaction of a type, or below a value, prescribed by the Capital Market Supervisory
   Board.

Section 89/13 empowers the Capital Market Supervisory Board to prescribe rules — on
disclosure and on the shareholder-vote count — for material connected transactions.

## Key Definitions (AC Handbook)

- **Associated company** (บริษัทร่วม) — a company in which the securities issuer and/or its
  subsidiaries hold **20% to 50%** of total voting shares, or in which they have power to
  participate in financial/operating policy decisions without controlling them.
- **Executive** (ผู้บริหาร) — a director, manager, or any of the **first four management-
  level officers** below the manager (and equivalents), including the head of accounting
  or finance at manager level or above.
- **Major shareholder** (ผู้ถือหุ้นรายใหญ่) — a holder of **more than 10%** of total voting
  shares (counting related persons' holdings).
- **Controlling person** (ผู้มีอำนาจควบคุม) — a person who, by circumstance, significantly
  influences a company's policy, management or operations — in particular one who holds
  directly or indirectly **more than 25%** of voting shares, or who can control the
  appointment/removal of directors.

## Why It Matters

These sections are the statutory backbone of director liability and connected-transaction
control for any Brooker listed entity. The 90% subsidiary exemption, the 25% controlling-
person threshold, and the 20–50% associated-company band determine when board-only
approval suffices and when a shareholder vote is mandatory.
