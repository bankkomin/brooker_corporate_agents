# Source — SET Acquisition/Disposal of Assets Regulation B.E. 2547

## Document Overview

The SET Board notification "Disclosure of Information and Conduct of Listed Companies in
the Acquisition or Disposal of Assets B.E. 2547" (บจ/ป 21-01), issued under Clause 9 of the
SET disclosure rules dated 15 September 1995. Effective **15 November 2004**, it superseded
the B.E. 2544 version. The folder also holds a SET-published consolidated copy
(SET_20140710). Multiple amendments are noted in-text (No. 2 of 2552, No. 3 of 2555,
No. 4 of 2556, No. 5 of 2560, No. 6 of 2561).

## Key Findings

- **Transaction classification (Clause 4)** — Asset acquisitions/disposals are reported by
  materiality as Type 1, 2, 3, or 4.
- **Four size tests (Clause 7)** — A company uses the **highest** value from: (1) asset
  value, (2) after-tax net operating profit, (3) total consideration, (4) value of
  securities issued as consideration — each as a percentage of the listed company's
  (or group's) figures.
- **Type 1 (Clause 13)** — Size 50% to below 100%: immediate disclosure plus shareholder
  meeting approval. For size 50%–below 75% a written shareholder resolution (≥ 3/4 of votes,
  excluding interested parties) may substitute for a meeting.
- **Type 2 (Clause 16)** — Size 15% to below 50%: immediate disclosure plus a notice to
  shareholders within 21 days.
- **Type 3 (Clause 18)** — Size below 15% with securities issued as consideration that
  will be listed: immediate disclosure with specified information.
- **Type 4 (Clause 20)** — Backdoor listing; covered in detail in
  [[backdoor-listing-type-4-transactions]].
- **Cash company (Chapter 4, Clause 26)** — If a listed company disposes of all/nearly all
  of its operating assets, leaving it holding only cash or short-term securities, the SET
  deems it unsuitable to remain listed; see [[cash-company-delisting]].

## Relevance

This is the foundational SET regime for material asset transactions (MT) of listed
companies and the source of the Type 1–4 framework still in use. It underlies later reforms
(see [[source-mt-acquisition-disclosure-seminar-2559]]).
