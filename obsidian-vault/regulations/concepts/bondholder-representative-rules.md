# Bondholder Representative — Qualifications & Duties (ผู้แทนผู้ถือหุ้นกู้)

## Summary

A **bondholder representative** (ผู้แทนผู้ถือหุ้นกู้) is the institution appointed to act on
behalf of debenture holders, monitoring the issuer's compliance with the terms of rights
(ข้อกำหนดสิทธิ) and enforcing those rights if needed. The Thai SEC Capital Market
Supervisory Board governs who may serve and how they must act, principally through
**Notification No. TorJor. 37/2552 (ทจ. 37/2552)** — the consolidated version — effective
**1 September 2009**, as amended through **TorJor. 22/2567 (ทจ. 22/2567)**.

## Qualifications of a Bondholder Representative

Under TorJor. 37/2552 (consolidated), a bondholder representative must be a financial
institution under Thai law, of one of these types:

- A **commercial bank** or **finance company** under the law on financial-institution
  business (ธนาคารพาณิชย์ / บริษัทเงินทุน)
- A **specialised financial institution** established by specific statute
- A **securities company** licensed for brokerage, dealing or underwriting of securities
  (and meeting additional capability conditions)

The notification is issued under Sections 16/6, 41(3), 46 and 49 of the Securities and
Exchange Act B.E. 2535 (as amended by Act No. 4, B.E. 2551).

## Conduct & Practice Guidelines

The SEC's **practice guideline of 17 May 2566 (2023)** sets out how a bondholder
representative should discharge its functions — covenant monitoring, communication with
bondholders, and the exercise of powers under the terms of rights. The representative's
role extends across all trust-type offerings, including real-estate investment trusts and
infrastructure investment trusts (กองทรัสต์).

## Why It Matters

For a debenture issuer, appointing a qualified bondholder representative is a precondition
of a compliant offering in most cases. The representative is the channel through which a
default or covenant breach is escalated, so its independence and capability directly affect
the issuer's reputation and refinancing ability.
