# Issuing Shares to Repay Debt & Debt-to-Equity Conversion

## Summary

Thai company law allows a company in **debt restructuring** to issue new shares to
creditors to settle debt — a **debt-to-equity (D-E) conversion** (การแปลงหนี้เป็นทุน).
The mechanism rests on **section 54/1 of the Public Limited Companies Act B.E. 2535**
(added by Amendment No. 2, B.E. 2544) and is detailed in a **Ministry of Commerce
Ministerial Regulation B.E. 2544 (2001)**, published in the Royal Gazette on
**3 November 2001** and given effect **23 November 2001**.

## What the Conversion Program Must Contain

To issue shares to repay debt, the board must send shareholders a **debt-to-equity
conversion program** together with the meeting notice. The program must include at
minimum:

- Policy, method, reasons, pros, cons and expected benefits of the conversion.
- A summary of the company's financial statements for the conversion year (assets,
  liabilities, shareholders' equity, total revenue and profit/loss).
- A summary of **audited** annual financial statements for the **three years** preceding
  the conversion year (or fewer if the company is younger).
- **Name, address and debt amount of each creditor** to whom new shares will be issued.
- Where a creditor is a **director, manager or deputy manager** holding **more than 1%**
  of the company's voting shares — or that person's spouse or minor child — the
  creditor's status must be clearly stated; the 1% count includes shares held by
  spouse and minor children.
- Name and debt amount of the **ten largest other creditors**.
- Type and number of new shares to be issued to each creditor.
- The new share price and the price-setting basis.

## Approval & Disclosure

The issue of new shares to creditors under the conversion program must be approved by
the shareholders' meeting with a vote of **not less than three-quarters (3/4)** of the
total votes of shareholders present and entitled to vote. Once approved, the company
must **post a summary of the program without delay** at its head and branch offices —
unless securities-law disclosure rules already require an equivalent or fuller capital-
increase disclosure for the listed company.

## Why It Matters

For Brooker — which carries a structured loan portfolio (see [[structured-loan-portfolio]])
— D-E conversion is the regulatory path to convert a distressed receivable into equity,
or to settle the company's own debt with shares, and it carries the heightened 3/4
super-majority and full creditor-disclosure burden.
