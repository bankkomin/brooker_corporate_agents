# Infrastructure Fund Rules (Thailand)

## Summary

An **Infrastructure Fund (กองทุนรวมโครงสร้างพื้นฐาน)** is a Thai mutual-fund vehicle that
invests in infrastructure assets or businesses (e.g. transport, energy, utilities,
telecommunications) and distributes the resulting income to unit-holders. The Thai SEC
([[thai-sec]]) regulates the establishment, management, borrowing, and disclosure of these
funds through a set of notifications.

## Key Metrics

The folder assembles the SEC notification framework for infrastructure funds:

- **TorNor. 1/2554** — rules, conditions and procedures for **establishing and managing**
  an infrastructure fund.
- **SorNor. 31/2554** — rules, conditions and procedures for the **appointment and
  performance of duty of the Infrastructure Fund Manager**.
- **BorJorRor 13-00** (B.E. 2556, revised 22 May 2014) — SET listing rulebook provisions
  for infrastructure-fund units.
- A SEC notification on **fund borrowing** (การกู้ยืมเงิน).
- SET circular **Bj59/16** — improvement of disclosure rules for property/infrastructure
  funds (notified by email 21 October 2016).

## Historical Context

The infrastructure-fund regime dates from 2011 (B.E. 2554), created to channel private
capital into Thai infrastructure. The framework was refined through 2013–2016 SET listing
rules and disclosure improvements. Infrastructure funds were later complemented by
Infrastructure Trusts (a separate REIT-style vehicle).
