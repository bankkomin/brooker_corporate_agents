# IFRS 16 / TFRS 16 Leases

## Summary

**IFRS 16 Leases** is the accounting standard issued by the International Accounting
Standards Board (IASB) in **January 2016**, effective for periods beginning on or after
**1 January 2019**. It replaces IAS 17 *Leases* and related interpretations. Thailand
adopts it as **TFRS 16** through the Federation of Accounting Professions (FAP), whose
stated policy is to keep Thai Financial Reporting Standards aligned with IFRS as issued
internationally.

## The Core Change

IFRS 16 eliminates, for **lessees**, the old distinction between operating leases and
finance leases. Under a single lessee model, a lessee must recognise — for all leases
with a term over 12 months, unless the underlying asset is of low value:

- a **right-of-use asset** and a corresponding **lease liability** on the balance sheet
  (leases are "capitalised" at the present value of lease payments); and
- **depreciation** of the lease asset separately from **interest** on the lease liability
  in the income statement.

This brings previously "off balance sheet" operating leases onto the balance sheet —
listed companies under IFRS or US GAAP had disclosed almost **US$3 trillion** of off
balance sheet lease commitments in 2014. **Lessor** accounting is substantially carried
forward from IAS 17 (operating vs. finance lease classification retained).

## Why It Matters

For a SET-listed company the standard increases reported assets and financial leverage,
and changes the timing and presentation of lease-related expense. The Thai SEC has
historically pressed for lease transparency — a concern the IASB traces back to a 2005 US
SEC statement. Companies with significant property or equipment leases must model the
balance-sheet and gearing impact.
