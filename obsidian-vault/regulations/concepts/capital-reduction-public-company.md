# Capital Reduction by a Public Limited Company (การลดทุน)

## Summary

Capital reduction (การลดทุน) by a public limited company is governed by **Sections 138–143
of the Public Limited Company Act B.E. 2535 (1992)**, as amended (notably by Act No. 2
B.E. 2544). A company may reduce its registered capital either by **lowering the par value
of each share** or by **reducing the number of shares**. This concept summarises the
quorum, the floor on how far capital may be reduced, and the creditor-protection process.

## How Much May Be Reduced

- As a general rule, capital **may not be reduced below one-quarter (25%)** of total
  capital.
- **Exception:** where the company has **accumulated losses** and has already offset them
  under Section 119 but still has remaining accumulated losses, it **may** reduce capital
  below the one-quarter floor.

## Shareholder Approval

A reduction of par value or number of shares requires a **shareholders' meeting resolution
passed by not less than three-quarters (75%)** of the total votes of shareholders
attending and entitled to vote. The company must then **register the resolution within
14 days** of the meeting.

A meeting may also resolve to reduce capital by **cancelling registered shares that were
unsold or never offered** (Section 140); the company registers that reduction within
**14 days** of the resolution.

## Creditor Protection

For a capital reduction other than the cancellation of unsold shares (Section 141):

- The company must **notify known creditors in writing within 14 days** of the
  shareholders' resolution, giving creditors **2 months** from receipt to file an
  objection, and must **advertise the resolution in a newspaper** within the same 14 days.
- If an objection is filed, the company **may not reduce capital** until the debt is paid
  or secured.
- After completing the Section 139 / 141 steps, the company registers the reduction
  within **14 days** of the expiry of the objection period (no objection) or within
  **14 days** of paying or securing the debt (objection filed).

After registering a paid-up capital change or a capital reduction, the company must
**notify shareholders in writing and advertise in at least one newspaper within 14 days**.

## Why It Matters

Capital reduction is a standard tool for cleaning up accumulated losses on a holding
company's balance sheet and for returning surplus capital to shareholders. The 75% super-
majority, the 25% floor (and its loss-offset exception), and the 2-month creditor-
objection window are the binding constraints on any Brooker capital-reduction plan.
