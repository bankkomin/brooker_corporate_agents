# Acquisition or Disposal of Assets (MT) — Materiality Rules

## Summary

When a listed company or its subsidiary acquires or disposes of assets of material
value or transaction size, it must disclose the transaction to investors, and where
the transaction is large enough to affect the company's financial position or
operating results, **shareholders must also participate in the decision**. The rules
flow from **Section 89/29 of the Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535** (as amended by
Act No. 4, effective **31 August 2008**), under which the Thai SEC empowered the
Capital Market Supervisory Board notification **ทจ. 20/2551** ("Rules on significant
acquisition/disposal-of-assets transactions"), which directs listed companies to
follow the criteria set by SET. This document reflects the **January 2020**
consolidated version. (These rules are commonly referred to as "MT".)

## Key Definitions

- **Asset** (สินทรัพย์) — anything tangible or intangible that a person or entity owns
  and that has transferable value.
  - *Tangible:* land, buildings, machinery, investments, warrants, etc.
  - *Intangible:* leasehold rights, concession rights, business licences, transfer or
    waiver of benefits, including waiver of claims against parties that harmed the
    company.
  - *Excluded:* (1) current operating assets (raw materials, trade receivables,
    inventory, cash, deposits); (2) liquidity investments (equity and debt
    instruments).
- **Acquisition or disposal** — buying/selling assets; agreeing/entering a contract to
  obtain or sell assets; acquiring or waiving rights to assets; transferring in/out
  long-term possession rights; or making/cancelling an investment.

## Key Metrics — The 4 Transaction-Size Tests

Transaction size is calculated as of the date the board passes the resolution. There
are **4 calculation criteria** (different criteria apply by asset type and
consideration method — cash vs. share issuance):

1. **Net Tangible Asset (NTA) test** —
   `(NTA of the investment × proportion acquired/disposed) × 100 / NTA of listed company`.
   NTA = total assets − intangible assets − total liabilities − non-controlling
   interests. Intangibles that generate the company's core revenue (concessions,
   mining licences) are not deducted.
2. **Net operating profit test** —
   `(net operating profit of the investment × proportion bought/sold) × 100 / net
   operating profit of listed company`.
3. **Total value of consideration test** —
   `value paid or received × 100 / total assets of listed company`. On a disposal,
   compare consideration value with book value and use the higher; if consideration
   is listed securities, compare market price with NTA and use the higher; if the
   disposal ends subsidiary/associate status, include loans, guarantees and other
   obligations.
4. **Value of shares issued to pay for assets test** —
   `shares issued to pay for assets × 100 / company's paid-up shares` (excluding the
   shares issued for the asset).

For companies preparing consolidated statements, use consolidated figures (latest
period).

## Procedure

1. Determine whether the transaction fits the definitions of "asset" and
   "acquisition/disposal".
2. Calculate transaction size under the applicable tests as of the board-resolution
   date.
3. Act according to the size band — e.g. no disclosure to SET, disclosure only, board
   approval required, or shareholder approval required.
4. If the counterparty is a connected person, also follow the connected-transaction
   (RPT) rules.
