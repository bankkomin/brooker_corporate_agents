# Source — SET Cash Company Possible-Delisting Reference (Dec 2019)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **December 2019** (file modified 2019-12-25; folder snapshot updated
17-Sep-2021). An English-language SET reference titled *"Grounds for Possible Delisting
and Elimination of the Grounds — All or most of listed company's assets are in the form
of cash or short-term securities (Cash Company)."* It is a structured subject/details
table plus a procedure timeline, Q&A, and a list of related regulations.

## Key Findings

- Cash Company status becomes a delisting ground once a company holds all/most assets in
  cash or short-term securities for **more than 6 months** after SET receives the post-
  disposition audited balance sheet.
- The reviewed statement of financial position is due **within 30 days** of disposition.
- Procedure: NP + C signs (6-month rectification) → NC + SP signs (1-year elimination of
  grounds) → Resume Stage (1-year qualification repossession), extendable once by ≤1 year.
- Resume Stage extension requires equity ≥ THB 50M or 1-year main-business net profit
  ≥ THB 10M, a continuous main business, a clear elimination plan, and clean management.
- A SET-listed Cash Company may apply to re-list on **mai** rather than SET.
- The illustrative timeline runs Feb 2018 (disposition) → Mar 2018 (SET receives reports)
  → Sep 2018 (possible-delisting announcement) → Sep 2019 / Sep 2020 (delisting if
  qualifications not repossessed).
