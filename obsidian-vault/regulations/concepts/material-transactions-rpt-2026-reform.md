# MT & RPT — 2026 Reform of Material Transactions and Connected Transactions

## Summary

On 19 December 2025 the Capital Market Supervisory Board (คณะกรรมการกำกับตลาดทุน)
issued two new notifications — **TorChor. 45/2568** on Material Transactions (รายการที่มี
นัยสำคัญ, "MT") and **TorChor. 46/2568** on Connected Transactions (รายการที่เกี่ยวโยงกัน,
"RPT"). They were announced publicly via SEC news release 16/2569 (19 January 2026) and
**take effect 1 July 2026** (1 กรกฎาคม 2569). The reform replaces the long-standing
acquisition/disposal-of-assets rules (TorChor. 20/2551 for MT; TorChor. 21/2551 and three
amendments for RPT).

The package is part of the SEC's "Strong Issuer" (บริษัทผู้ออกหลักทรัพย์เข้มแข็ง)
programme — preventive supervision through stricter rules — and aims to align Thai practice
with international standards and improve investor protection.

## Who It Applies To

Both notifications bind two types of company:
1. **Listed companies** (บริษัทจดทะเบียน) on SET/MAI — but **not** companies traded only
   on the SET LiveExchange board.
2. **Public companies** that have been licensed to make a public share offering and carry
   the Section 56 financial-reporting duty.

## The Three Pillars of the Reform

1. **Clearer, current rules** — revised transaction-size calculation and aggregation
   method, duties recalibrated by transaction-size band, and updated definitions.
2. **Stronger investor-protection mechanisms** — a new shareholder veto right where the
   audit committee or independent financial advisor (IFA) opposes a transaction; a
   mandatory progress-reporting duty; and an explicit **Substance Over Form** power
   letting the SEC require MT/RPT compliance where the true substance of a transaction is
   comparable to an MT/RPT.
3. **Reduced burden / more flexibility** — exemptions for in-group MT (parent–subsidiary,
   subsidiary–subsidiary), and a new ability to seek **advance ("framework") shareholder
   approval** delegating the board to act within an approved framework where normal
   pre-transaction disclosure would materially harm the company (e.g. competitive bids
   or tenders).

## Material Transaction (MT) Size Thresholds — from 1 Jul 2026

An MT covers acquisition/disposal of assets, transfer or waiver of rights and benefits,
leasing/lease amendment of business or assets, and financial assistance (loans,
guarantees, obligation-creating agreements). Transaction size uses the **highest** of
four calculation methods (Appendix 1): Net Tangible Asset ratio, Profitability ratio,
Total Consideration ratio, and Share-Issuance ratio.

| Case | Company financial status | Transaction size | EGM | IFA required | Veto right |
|------|--------------------------|------------------|-----|--------------|------------|
| 0 | Any | < 10% | No | No | — |
| 1 | **Adverse** financial status | ≥ 10% and < 25% | Yes (3/4 vote) | No | If audit committee says "No" |
| 2 | **Adverse** financial status | ≥ 25% | Yes (3/4 vote) | Yes | If AC or IFA says "No" |
| 3 | Non-adverse | ≥ 25% and < 50% | Yes (3/4 vote) | No | If audit committee says "No" |
| 4 | Non-adverse | ≥ 50% | Yes (3/4 vote) | Yes | If AC or IFA says "No" |

- **Adverse financial status** (สินทรัพย์สุทธิเป็นลบหรือมีผลขาดทุน) = negative Net Asset
  Value, or net loss in the most recent 12-month period.
- **Net Asset Value** = Total Assets − Total Liabilities − Minority Interest.
- **Veto rule:** where the AC/IFA opposes, the transaction is blocked if shareholders
  holding **≥ 10%** of votes present and eligible cast a "no" vote.

## Aggregation Rule

In computing MT size, a company must **aggregate related transactions, or transactions
under the same project, occurring within the 12 months before the transaction** —
treating them as a single transaction. Transactions already approved by a shareholders'
meeting are excluded from aggregation. The SEC may itself aggregate transactions it finds
were deliberately split to evade the rules.

## Disclosure and Progress Reporting

- Shareholder-meeting notice at least **14 days** in advance, disclosed via SET's
  information system; must include Appendix 2 details, the audit committee's opinion,
  and (where required) the IFA opinion (Appendix 3).
- Transaction disclosure (Appendix 4) via SET on the day the board resolves to table the
  agenda item.
- After approval, the company must publish a **progress report every 6 calendar months**
  (by 31 July and 31 January) until the transaction completes, plus an immediate report
  if it materially fails to proceed or is cancelled. Reports are also carried in the
  revised **Form 56-1 One Report** (Section 1, item 1.1.5).

## Brooker Relevance

Brooker Group (BTC) is a listed company and is directly bound. As of 31 Dec 2025 BTC's
total assets were ~THB 3,267M and NTA ~THB 2,494M, with a 12-month net loss of
~THB 853M — meaning BTC currently sits in the **"adverse financial status"** bracket,
where the lower 10%/25% MT thresholds apply rather than 25%/50%. Any acquisition,
disposal, financial assistance or asset lease must be size-tested on this stricter basis
from 1 July 2026. See also [[investment-company-classification]].

## Agent Notes

Treat 1 July 2026 as the hard switch-over date. A transitional rule (Clause 18 of
TorChor. 45/2568) keeps the old TorChor. 20/2551 rules for any MT whose board agenda
resolution predates the effective date. When testing a BTC transaction, first confirm
BTC's current financial status (adverse vs non-adverse) — it changes which threshold row
applies.
