# Corporate Governance Policy & Code of Ethics (Brooker)

## Summary

This concept covers the **corporate governance (CG) policy** and **code of ethics** adopted
by **The Brooker Group Public Company Limited**, as held in the SEC/SET regulatory archive.
These are the company's own governance instruments, drafted to comply with Thai
listed-company governance expectations. Available in English and Thai editions, dated 2007.

## The CG Policy

The Brooker Board's stated intention is to enhance operational and management efficiency
for the maximum benefit of shareholders and all stakeholders, with emphasis on protecting
stakeholder rights and treating stakeholders equitably. The policy commits the Board and
employees to four core CG principles:

1. **Accountability & Responsibility** — in decision-making and discharging duties to
   fullest capacity.
2. **Creation of Long-Term Value** — optimising shareholder value for long-term,
   sustainable growth.
3. **Ethics & Equitable Treatment** — clear business ethics; fair treatment of all
   shareholders and stakeholders.
4. **Disclosure & Transparency** — providing information to stakeholders; transparent,
   examinable operating systems.

The Board commits to performing duties diligently and to enhancing director roles and
responsibilities together with disclosure quality.

## Code of Ethics for Directors and Executives

A companion **Code of Ethics** (จรรยาบรรณกรรมการและผู้บริหาร) sets the conduct standards
for Brooker's directors and senior executives. It operationalises the "Ethics & Equitable
Treatment" principle of the CG policy.

## Why It Matters

These documents are Brooker's internal governance baseline. They sit beneath the Thai
listed-company governance regime (SEC Code of Governance, SET requirements) and define how
the Board and management are expected to behave. They are point-in-time (2007) and may
have been superseded by later board-approved revisions.
