# SET Listed-Company Governance Uplift (2024)

## Summary

The Stock Exchange of Thailand (SET) issued circular **บจ. (ว) 2/2567** (19 February
2024) announcing a comprehensive tightening of the rules governing listed companies —
from listing admission through ongoing supervision — to strengthen issuers' financial
health and disclosure. It revised or repealed **23 SET regulations/guidelines** in
total. Most measures took effect **25 March 2024 (25 มี.ค. 2567)**; the toughened
listing-admission criteria took effect **1 January 2025**.

## What Changed

### 1. Tougher listing-admission criteria (effective 1 January 2025)
Raised financial-position and operating-performance qualifications for companies
admitting to both **SET** and **mai**, with reduced size differentiation to accommodate
low-capital businesses — including a higher net-profit requirement to evidence sound
performance.

### 2. Stricter handling of at-risk listed companies (effective 25 March 2024)
For companies with financial-position, performance, or liquidity risk, or otherwise
off-criteria:

- New measures against companies with **no business**, **3 consecutive years of
  losses**, and companies that have **defaulted** on financial-institution debt or debt
  instruments.
- Revised treatment where the **auditor disclaims an opinion** on the financial
  statements.
- Revised treatment of companies off-criteria — including **Cash Company** status,
  deficient **Audit Committee** or **Free Float**, or failure to hold **Opportunity
  Day** as required.
- **Backdoor Listing** entrants and companies **resuming trading** after curing a
  potential-delisting cause are now scrutinised as strictly as **New Listings**.
- Alignment of SET rules with SEC Office rules; obsolete rules repealed or revised.

The package also expands the system of **warning signs/marks** alerting investors to a
company's status.

## Why It Matters

The uplift directly affects how a SET-listed issuer like Brooker is supervised: the Cash
Company / loss-making / free-float triggers, and the Opportunity Day obligation, are
ongoing-compliance items. Any backdoor-listing or trading-resumption route now faces
new-listing-grade review.
