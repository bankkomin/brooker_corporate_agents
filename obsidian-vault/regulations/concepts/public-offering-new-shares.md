# Public Offering of Newly Issued Shares — SEC Notification TorJor. 28/2551 (การเสนอขายหุ้นที่ออกใหม่)

## Summary

**SEC Notification of the Capital Market Supervisory Board No. TorJor. 28/2551 (ทจ.
28/2551)** — *Application for and Approval of the Offering of Newly Issued Shares*
(consolidated version, ฉบับประมวล) — sets the rules under which a company applies for and
obtains **SEC permission to offer newly issued shares to the public**. This is the regime
for an ordinary public offering of equity (PO) — including a company's continuing/follow-on
offerings to the public after an IPO.

## Legal Basis & Status

- Issued under **Sections 16/6 and 89/27** of the Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535,
  as amended by **Act No. 4 B.E. 2551 (2008)**, together with **Section 35** of the SEA
  B.E. 2535.
- **Effective 15 December 2008.**
- **Repealed and replaced** the earlier Notification TorJor. 14/2551 (dated 2 June 2008).
- Issued in **consolidated form** — the extract held is the version current as of the
  2015-12-14 file date, incorporating subsequent amendments.

## Scope & Defined Terms

The notification governs the application for, and the SEC's grant of, permission to offer
**newly issued shares** to the public. It adopts the standard SEA defined terms, including
**institutional investor**, **listed company**, **parent company**, **subsidiary**,
**fellow subsidiary**, **associated company**, **business-control power**, **related
person**, **executive**, **major shareholder**, **controlling person**, **person who may
have a conflict**, and **consolidated financial statements** — carrying the same meanings
as in the underlying notifications.

## Why It Matters

TorJor. 28/2551 is the gating SEC permission any Brooker listed entity must obtain before
a public equity raise of newly issued shares. It works alongside the SET listing-admission
rules (for an IPO) and the general-mandate / specific-purpose capital-increase mechanisms
that determine how the underlying new shares are authorised.
