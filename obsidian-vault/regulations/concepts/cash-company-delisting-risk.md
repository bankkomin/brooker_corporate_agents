# Cash Company — Possible-Delisting Process

## Summary

A **Cash Company** (บริษัทที่มีสินทรัพย์ทั้งหมด/เกือบทั้งหมดในรูปเงินสดหรือหลักทรัพย์
ระยะสั้น) is a SET-listed company that has sold all or substantially all of the assets
used in its normal business, so that the company and/or its subsidiaries hold cash or
short-term securities as substantially all of their assets. This article describes the
SET process for entering, and clearing, possible-delisting status as a Cash Company —
as documented in the SET reference note dated November 2021.

## The Process

**1. Reporting trigger.** After selling all/substantially all of its operating assets, a
company must file a **statement of financial position** (auditor-reviewed) to SET within
**30 days**. SET may temporarily halt trading until full, clear disclosure is made.

**2. Six-month cure window.** SET declares the company a **Cash Company**, posts the
**NP (Notice Pending)** sign and the **C (Caution)** sign, and gives the company **6
months** from the date SET received the financial-position statement to fix the Cash
Company status (i.e. acquire a qualifying operating business).

**3. Possible-delisting status.** If not cured within 6 months, the security enters the
possible-delisting process, with two one-year phases:

- **Phase 1 (remove the cause) — 1 year.** SET posts **NC (Non-Compliance)** and **SP
  (Suspension)**. The company must establish a clear, continuing **core business** —
  judged by factors such as commercial revenue, operating assets, and licences/concessions.
  Progress must be disclosed every quarter. If the cause is removed within the year, SET
  moves the security to Phase 2.
- **Phase 2 (regain trading qualification) — 1 year.** The company must apply for
  re-listing under SET / mai listing rules. A **one-time extension of up to 1 year** may
  be requested (filed 7 business days before the deadline) if the company can show it
  meets all of: (1) shareholders' equity ≥ **THB 50 million** *or* net profit ≥ **THB 10
  million** from the core business for 1 year; (2) a clear plan and progress to resume
  trading; (3) other qualifications complete (directors/executives free of prohibited
  characteristics, full disclosure compliance).

**4. Return to trading.** The company must meet SET/mai listing rules — **except** the
free-float rule, which is relaxed to **>15% of paid-up capital** held by **>150 minor
shareholders**. SET publishes the resumption 7 business days in advance.

**5. Delisting.** If the cause is not removed, or trading qualification not regained,
within the set time, SET will consider ordering delisting — typically allowing 7 business
days of trading beforehand, keeping the NC sign and the Cash Balance requirement.

## Why It Matters

A company that divests its operating business — for example to become an investment or
holding vehicle — can fall into Cash Company status and face the possible-delisting clock.
This is a structural risk to weigh before any large disposal of core operating assets.
