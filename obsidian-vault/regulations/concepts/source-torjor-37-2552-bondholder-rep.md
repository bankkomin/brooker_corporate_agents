# Source: Notification TorJor. 37/2552 — Bondholder Representative Qualifications

## Document Overview

**As-of:** consolidated version (ฉบับประมวล) of Capital Market Supervisory Board
**Notification No. TorJor. 37/2552** (ประกาศคณะกรรมการกำกับตลาดทุน ที่ ทจ. 37/2552),
"Qualifications of the Bondholder Representative and Acts Within the Bondholder
Representative's Authority," amended through **TorJor. 22/2567 (2024)**. Originally
**effective 1 September 2009**. Extract from a Feb 2025 regulatory pack.

## Key Findings

- A **bondholder representative** (ผู้แทนผู้ถือหุ้นกู้) must be a financial institution
  under Thai law — a commercial bank or finance company, a specialised statutory financial
  institution, or a licensed securities company (brokerage/dealing/underwriting).
- Defines "terms of rights" (ข้อกำหนดสิทธิ — the rights and duties of the issuer and
  bondholders), "private-placement debenture offering," and "trust fund" (กองทรัสต์,
  covering REITs and infrastructure trusts).
- Issued under Sections 16/6, 41(3), 46 and 49 of the Securities and Exchange Act
  B.E. 2535 (amended by Act No. 4, B.E. 2551). Amendment No. 4 (TorJor. 62/2559) took
  effect 1 February 2017.

## Relevance

The primary document behind the [[bondholder-representative-rules]] concept. It governs
who an issuer may appoint as the bondholders' agent — a precondition of most compliant
debenture offerings.
