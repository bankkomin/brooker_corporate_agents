# Financial Assistance Rules — Loans by Listed Companies (กฎการให้ความช่วยเหลือทางการเงิน)

## Summary

"Financial assistance" (การให้ความช่วยเหลือทางการเงิน) refers to a listed company
providing **loans, guarantees, or similar financial support** — typically to subsidiaries,
associates, joint ventures, or related parties. The Stock Exchange of Thailand and the
Thai SEC regulate when such assistance must be disclosed and approved, and what must be
disclosed if a borrower **defaults** on repayment. This concept covers the SET/SEC
financial-assistance framework and the related disclosure guideline.

## What the Rules Cover

The financial-assistance regime addresses:

- The provision of **loans** (กู้ยืมเงิน) and other financial support by a listed company.
- Disclosure obligations when financial assistance is given, and approval requirements
  where the assistance is material or involves connected parties.
- A specific **disclosure guideline for default cases** (กรณีการผิดนัดชำระ) — what a
  listed company must announce when a counterparty fails to repay financial assistance it
  has provided.

## Why It Matters

Inter-company and related-party lending is a routine activity for a holding company like
Brooker. Financial assistance can fall within the connected-transaction and acquisition/
disposition regimes, triggering board, audit-committee, or shareholder approval and
disclosure. A default on financial assistance given out is a material event requiring
prompt disclosure, and significant impairment of such loans can feed into delisting-risk
assessments.
