# Low-Price Private Placement — Disclosure & Silent Period

## Summary

When a Thai listed company increases capital by allocating new shares (or warrants) to
**specific persons** — a **private placement (PP)** — the SEC and SET impose disclosure
and approval rules, with extra requirements where the offering price is **below market
price**. The current framework was set by SEC Notifications **TorChor. 72/2558 &
73/2558** and a SET Board notification, communicated in SET circular **Bor.Jor.(Wor)
58/2015** dated **17 December 2015**, effective **1 December 2015**.

## Core Requirements

- A PP offering of shares or warrants must be **approved by the SEC before** the listed
  company starts offering. (Offerings at **market price** during the offering period are
  generally pre-approved.)
- The company must disclose the capital increase via the **SET disclosure system**
  together with the capital-increase report **on the same day** the board approves it,
  or by 9:00 a.m. the next business day at the latest.
- The notice calling the shareholders' meeting must contain the **minimum agenda and
  information** specified by the SEC (TorChor. 73/2558 and the SEC's PP Tool Kit).

## Information Disclosed to Shareholders

The capital-increase disclosure must cover: offering details (share count, investor
type, offering-price determination vs. market price, allocation method); objectives and
fund-utilisation plan (with project specifics, income opportunity, failure impact and
risks); the **impact on shareholders** (price dilution, control dilution, value
trade-off); and the **board's opinion** on the reasons, need, feasibility and adequacy
of the capital increase. For PP specifically, additional items are required:

- **Name and information of the PP investors** receiving the new shares.
- The **board's opinion** on the suitability of the offering price, the source of the
  price determination, and the board's ratification of investor potential.

## The Low-Price Trigger

Where the **offering price is lower than market price**, the disclosure must additionally
state the **shareholders' right to oppose** the low-price offering, plus the board's
opinion on whether the benefit from the investors outweighs the cost of selling below
market — assessed against the company's expenses and financial position and **consistent
with the share-based payment standard**. A low-price PP is one of the "special items"
that requires the **14-day** meeting notice (see [[shareholder-meeting-rules]]). Related
Thai SEC rules also impose a **silent period** restricting resale of low-price placed
securities — and the digital asset framework applies an analogous 6-month silent period
to low-price tokens (see [[utility-token-regulation]]).

## Why It Matters

Any Brooker capital raise via private placement must be screened for the SEC pre-approval
requirement, the immediate SETLink disclosure duty, and — if priced below market — the
opposition right, the 14-day notice, and the share-based-payment accounting overlay.
