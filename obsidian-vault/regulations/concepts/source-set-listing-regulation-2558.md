# Source — SET Listing Regulation for Ordinary/Preferred Shares B.E. 2558 (2015)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **16 May 2015** (effective date; B.E. 2558). Document code **Bor.Jor./Ror
01-00**. The **SET Regulation on Listing of Ordinary Shares or Preferred Shares as Listed
Securities B.E. 2558** — the consolidated SET rulebook governing admission of equity to
the Main Board. Issued by the SET Board of Governors under **Section 170, paragraph 2 (1)
of the Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535**, with SEC approval. It is a long
multi-chapter regulation (~430-page extract).

## Key Findings

- The regulation is structured into **Parts (ภาค)** and **Chapters (บทที่)** with
  **Sections (หมวด)** covering, among others:
  - **Chapter 2** — listing of ordinary/preferred shares of **Thai-incorporated companies**
    (security qualifications, applicant qualifications, application and SET review,
    financial advisers, restrictions on directors/major shareholders selling shares
    (silent period), and ongoing qualification-maintenance duties).
  - **Chapter 3** — listing of **foreign companies**, including **Primary Listing** and
    secondary-listing arrangements.
  - **Chapter 4** — additional/transitional provisions cross-referring qualification and
    application rules between Chapters 2 and 3.
- **Clause 58/1 / 122/1** impose a continuing duty on a listed company to **maintain the
  security qualifications** set out in Chapter 2 / Chapter 3 for as long as it stays
  listed.
- Certain clauses (e.g. 15 para. 3, 16, 23, 24, 31, 64, 82 para. 3, 83, 90, 91, 98, 129,
  139) are reserved to the **SET Board** itself and may not be delegated to the SET
  Manager.
- The regulation has been **amended multiple times** since 2015 (consolidated text notes
  superseded sub-sections).
