# New-Share Offering Approval — 2023 Amendments

## Summary

In 2023 (B.E. 2566) the Capital Market Supervisory Board issued three notifications
amending the rules on **applying for, and granting approval of, the offering of newly
issued shares (การขออนุญาตและการอนุญาตให้เสนอขายหุ้นที่ออกใหม่)**. The headline change is a
new restriction targeting **investment companies (investment company / บริษัทบริหารจัดการ
เงินลงทุน)** — applicants whose business is effectively investment management.

## Key Metrics

**TorChor. 45/2566** (Amendment No. 16 to TorChor. 39/2559 on new-share offerings):
- An applicant offering shares must **not** be a listed company currently flagged with a
  **"C" (Caution)** mark, or under an **"SP" (Trading Suspension)** order, on the ground
  that it is operating as an *investment company* — unless the SEC grants a waiver for
  necessary and reasonable cause.
- A new **Clause 18/1** sets the permitted business character of an applicant: it must not
  operate as an investment company, nor behave so as to give investors returns equivalent
  to investment-management activity (banks excepted).
- **Investment-company test:** investments in shares, derivatives, or digital assets of the
  types described, taken together, exceeding **40% of total assets** per the latest
  (consolidated) financial statements, constitute operating as an investment company.
  Carve-outs include investments in subsidiaries/associates, investments supporting the
  applicant's own business, and government bonds / MoF-guaranteed instruments.

**TorChor. 46/2566** (Amendment No. 20): amends the rules on offering shares issued by
**foreign companies** not listed on a foreign exchange — revising the business-character
and foreign-regulator-cooperation requirements (Part 1).

**TorChor. 47/2566** (Amendment No. 12): amends general rules on the offering of securities.

## Historical Context

These 2023 amendments are part of the SEC's effort to prevent companies from listing — or
raising new equity — primarily as investment vehicles, ensuring listed issuers have genuine
operating businesses. The 40%-of-total-assets investment-company threshold is the same
quantitative test the wider market uses; see the related CEO-side concept on the
investment-company rule for the Brooker Group's own internal application of this limit.
