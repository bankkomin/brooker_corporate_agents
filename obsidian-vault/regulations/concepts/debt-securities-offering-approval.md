# Approval to Offer Newly Issued Debt Securities (ทจ. 17/2561)

## Summary

Capital Market Supervisory Board Notification **No. ทจ. 17/2561** — *Application for and
Approval of the Offer for Sale of Newly Issued Debt Securities* — is the consolidating
notification governing how a company applies for, and obtains, permission to offer newly
issued debt instruments (ตราสารหนี้) to investors. It took effect **1 April 2018**.

## Legal Basis & Consolidation

The notification is issued under Sections 16/6 and 89/27 of the Securities and Exchange
Act B.E. 2535 (as amended by the No. 4 Act of B.E. 2551), together with Sections 35,
40(11), 41(3)–(4), 42(10) and 43(3) of the SEA.

It **consolidated and repealed at least eight earlier notifications** on the same
subject — the ทจ. 9/2552 debt-securities-offering notification and its amendments
Nos. 2–8 (ทจ. 29/2553, 16/2555, 36/2555, 46/2555, 12/2556, 18/2557, 30/2557) — replacing
a fragmented rule set with a single text.

## What It Governs

ทจ. 17/2561 sets the conditions and process by which an issuer seeks SEC approval to
offer newly issued debt securities, covering the application route and the conditions
the issuer and the offering must satisfy. It works alongside the rules on debenture
characteristics, on private-placement offerings, and on bondholder representatives.

## Why It Matters

Any Brooker debt-funding programme via newly issued debentures or bonds must run through
the ทจ. 17/2561 approval framework. Together with the private-placement and
debenture-characteristics rules, it defines the permissible debt-raising paths.
