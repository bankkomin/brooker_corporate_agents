# Source — SEC Public Consultation on Tokenized Carbon Credit Trading (Apr 2025)

## Document Overview

SEC news release No. 109/2568, **29 April 2025** — the SEC opened a public consultation on
amending its rules to let digital-asset exchanges, brokers and dealers offer **Tokenized
Carbon Credit** and related products. Source file modified 14 May 2025.

## Key Findings

- The SEC Board, at its **6 March 2025** meeting, approved in principle amendments
  allowing digital-asset business operators (digital-asset exchanges, brokers, dealers) to
  service three product types supporting Carbon Neutrality and Net Zero goals:
  **(1) Tokenized Carbon Credit, (2) Tokenized Renewable Energy Certificate (REC), and
  (3) Tokenized Carbon Allowance.**
- Stated objectives: widen investor choice, support a green economy, support Thailand's
  Carbon Neutrality / Net Zero targets, and promote Thailand as a carbon-credit trading
  hub.
- Proposed additional operating conditions for operators: (1) an effective system or
  standard for **selecting and delisting digital tokens**; and (2) an adequate system or
  standard for **disclosing information** about the tokens. Operators must comply with
  their other-business licensing conditions throughout the service period.
- The consultation ran until **12 May 2025**, with the consultation paper published on the
  SEC website and the central law portal.

## Relevance

A point-in-time regulatory development at the intersection of Thailand's digital-asset and
carbon markets. Relevant to Brooker's digital-asset division as a signal of an expanding
permitted-product set for digital-asset operators. As of the source date this was a
**consultation**, not a finalised rule.
