# SET 'C' (Caution) Sign

## Summary

The **"C" (Caution) sign** is a trading mark the Stock Exchange of Thailand posts against
a listed company's securities to warn investors that the company carries risk in its
financial position, financial statements or business nature, and to press the company to
fix the problem quickly. It was announced on 7 March 2018 (SET Release 18/2561) following
public consultation and SEC Board approval, and came into force on **2 July 2018** — the
first financial statements assessed being the quarter ending 30 June 2018.

## How It Works

When the "C" sign is posted, the security **must be traded through a Cash Balance
account** (the buyer must have cash deposited in full). SET announces the posting one
business day in advance. The listed company must clarify its situation and remediation
plan within **15 days** of the posting and every quarter thereafter. The sign stays in
place until the company has resolved the underlying cause — consistent with the general
principle for SET's other trading signs.

## Triggers for the 'C' Sign

The sign is posted when:

1. **Shareholders' equity falls significantly** — equity below **50% of paid-up capital**
   after deducting share discount (if any).
2. A financial-institution regulator (for a bank, securities company or insurer) issues a
   publicly disclosed order to fix the company's financial position or operations by
   suspending part of its activity or barring expansion, with significant impact.
3. The **SEC orders a restatement** of the financial statements or a **Special Audit**.
4. The **auditor disclaims an opinion** because scope was limited by the company or its
   directors/executives.
5. The **auditor issues an adverse opinion** that the financial statements are not correct.
6. The **bankruptcy court accepts** a business-reorganisation petition or a bankruptcy petition.
7. The company becomes a **Cash Company** — substantially all assets are in cash or
   short-term securities (see [[cash-company-delisting-risk]]).

## Why It Matters

The "C" sign is an early-warning signal short of the delisting-risk process. For an
investment-heavy company the equity-erosion trigger (#1) and the Cash Company trigger (#7)
are the most relevant — a forced asset sale or large losses can pull a company into the
"C" mark and the Cash Balance trading restriction.
