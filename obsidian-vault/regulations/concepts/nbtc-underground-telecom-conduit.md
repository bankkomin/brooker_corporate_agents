# NBTC Underground Telecom Conduit Guidelines (ท่อร้อยสายสื่อสารใต้ดิน)

## Summary

This concept covers the **NBTC** (National Broadcasting and Telecommunications Commission,
กสทช.) notification on **practice guidelines for using, investing in, and constructing
underground telecom cable conduits** (ท่อร้อยสายสื่อสารใต้ดิน), or using state-agency
infrastructure, for the provision of telecommunications services. It was published in the
Government Gazette on **2 June 2017** (Vol. 134, Special Part 148 Ngor).

## What the Notification Does

The notification sets practice guidelines so that telecom-licence holders' use, investment
in, and construction of underground cable conduits — or their use of state-agency
infrastructure — proceed in an orderly way. Its stated policy basis is to:

- promote **universal telecommunications service**;
- support **free and fair competition**;
- ensure **cost-effective use of resources**; and
- **avoid duplicative investment** in infrastructure.

It is issued under Sections 27 (6), (8) and (24), 29, 38, 39 and 40 of the **Act on
Organisation to Assign Radio Frequency and to Regulate Broadcasting and Telecommunications
Services B.E. 2553 (2010)**, together with Sections 39–44 of the **Telecommunications
Business Act B.E. 2544 (2001)**. The notification supersedes any conflicting earlier
notifications, regulations, rules or orders on the same matter.

## Why It Matters

Underground telecom conduit is a regulated shared-infrastructure asset class. For a
Brooker group entity evaluating a telecom-infrastructure or "dig-once" conduit investment,
the NBTC guidelines govern access rights, investment, and construction — and the anti-
duplication policy shapes whether independent conduit build-out is permissible.
