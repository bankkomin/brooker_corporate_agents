# Cash Company — Possible Delisting & Removal of Grounds (บริษัทที่มีสินทรัพย์เป็นเงินสด)

## Summary

A **Cash Company** is a listed company that has disposed of all or most of the assets
used in its ordinary course of business, so that all or most of its assets (on its own or
together with its subsidiaries) are held in the form of **cash or short-term securities**.
The Stock Exchange of Thailand (SET, ตลาดหลักทรัพย์) treats Cash Company status as a
**ground for possible delisting** because the entity no longer has an operating business.
This concept summarises the SET process from the SET reference dated **December 2019**.

## When the Cash Company Ground Applies

The ground arises when a listed company and/or its subsidiary has disposed of all or most
of its operating assets, leaving all or most of its assets in cash / short-term securities
for **more than 6 months** measured from the date SET receives the audited statement of
financial position after the disposition.

- The listed company must submit the **reviewed statement of financial position within 30
  days** of the asset disposition.
- SET may **suspend trading** until the company reports complete and clear information.

## SET's Procedure & Trading Signs

1. **Rectification window (6 months)** — From the date SET receives the statement of
   financial position, the company has **6 months** to cease being a Cash Company. During
   this window SET posts **NP (Notice Pending)** and **C (Caution)** signs; under the C
   sign, trading must be via a cash-balance account.
2. **Elimination of grounds for delisting — 1 year** — If still a Cash Company after the
   6 months, SET posts **NC (Non-Compliance)** and **SP (Suspension)** signs. The company
   has **1 year** from the possible-delisting announcement to eliminate the grounds
   (e.g. by acquiring a business). If it fails, SET may order delisting, allowing up to
   **30 days** of trading before removal.
3. **Resume Stage (1 year)** — Once grounds are eliminated, the company has a further
   **1 year** to repossess listing qualifications and resume normal trading. SET
   announces resumption **7 business days** in advance.

The Resume Stage may be **extended once, by no more than 1 year**, on a request filed
**7 business days** before the due date.

## Qualifications to Extend the Resume Stage

The company must show **all** of:

1. **Shareholders' equity** (after adjusting for the auditor's opinion) **≥ THB 50M**, OR
   net profit from the main business over 1 year **≥ THB 10M** (annual statements or the
   four latest quarters).
2. An apparent **main business operated on a continuous basis** (commercial revenue,
   operating assets, required licences/concessions).
3. A clear **procedure to eliminate the delisting grounds** with demonstrable progress.
4. Other qualifications — management and controlling parties free of prohibited
   characteristics under SEC notifications, and compliance with SET disclosure rules.

To finally resume trading the company must satisfy the listing qualifications under the
SET (or **mai**) Regulation on Listing of Ordinary or Preferred Shares, including the
minority-shareholder distribution requirement. A SET-listed Cash Company may apply to be
re-listed on **mai** instead of SET.

## Why It Matters

For a Brooker-type holding or investment company, large asset divestments that leave the
balance sheet predominantly in cash can trigger Cash Company status and the delisting
clock. Any major disposal programme must be paired with a credible reinvestment / new-
business plan within the 6-month rectification window to avoid the NC/SP cascade.
