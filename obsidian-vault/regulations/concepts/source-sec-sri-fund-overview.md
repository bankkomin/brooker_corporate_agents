# Source — SEC Overview Paper: SRI Funds

## Document Overview

- **As-of date:** Published by the Thai SEC on **12 January 2023** (เผยแพร่ 12 มกราคม 2566);
  source file last modified 2024-03-12.
- **Author:** Thai SEC ([[thai-sec]]) — Business Policy Department and Sustainability
  Promotion Department.
- **Type:** Public explanatory article — "Sustainable and Responsible Investing Fund (SRI
  Fund): มุ่งมั่นใส่ใจการลงทุนเพื่อผลตอบแทนที่ยั่งยืน" (also published at
  sec.or.th/TH/Template3/Articles/2566/120166.pdf).
- This is the **canonical primary document** for the SRI Fund archive; the accompanying
  `เว็บไซต์ของ กลต. (SRI Fund).docx` is merely a hyperlink to it.

## Key Findings

- **Why SRI Funds exist** — ESG investing has grown strongly, accelerated by COVID-19.
  ASEAN, including Thailand, is among the regions most exposed to climate risk (floods,
  droughts, warming). The SEC sees AMCs as a key mechanism to channel capital toward
  sustainable economic activity and help Thailand meet the UN SDGs (17 goals, three
  pillars: society, economy, environment).
- **The disclosure guideline** — issued **April 2022**: an SRI Fund is a fund focused on
  issuers that prioritise sustainability per international standards (SDGs, UN Global
  Compact); managing AMCs must disclose a uniform information set (policy, strategy,
  positive impact) so investors can compare funds and **greenwashing risk is reduced**.
- **2023 fee waiver** — application fees for establishing an SRI Fund, and for amending an
  existing scheme to become one, were waived for filings made in **2023**, as part of the
  government's "2023 New Year Gift" programme (Cabinet resolution, 20 Dec 2022).
- **Performance evidence cited** — at end-November 2022 the SETTHSI TRI index (Thai
  sustainable-business stocks) had outperformed SET TRI by **2.36% (1-year)** and **1.11%
  (3-year)** annualised; international studies (Pisani & Russo, 2021) found high-ESG-rated
  funds were more resilient through the COVID-19 crisis.
- **Population at issue** — at **31 December 2022 only 4 SRI Funds** had been SEC-approved;
  investors can check the live list and compare funds at sustainablefinance.sec.or.th.
- **PRI** — the paper promotes the UN-supported Principles for Responsible Investment (6
  principles; signatory categories Asset Owner / Investment Manager / Professional Service
  Provider). At publication only 3 Thai entities had signed.
- **Cross-border** — describes [[asean-srfs-standard]] (ASEAN Sustainable & Responsible
  Fund Standard) and the HK-TH MRF (Hong Kong–Thailand Mutual Recognition of Funds)
  negotiation that would let Thai AMCs distribute SRI Funds abroad.

## Relevance

This is the foundational reference for the [[sri-fund-regime]] concept. For Brooker it
matters where a portfolio company or fund counterparty markets an "SRI Fund" or "Thai ESG"
label — the SEC's disclosure standard defines what that label substantively requires.
