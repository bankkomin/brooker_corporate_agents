# Emergency Decree Amending the Civil & Commercial Code — Interest Rates (2021)

## Summary

The **Emergency Decree Amending the Civil and Commercial Code B.E. 2564 (2021)**,
published in the Royal Gazette on **10 April 2021** and effective the following day,
modernised Thailand's **default statutory interest rates**, which had stood unchanged at
**7.5% per annum** for decades. The decree was issued as urgent COVID-19 debt-relief
legislation.

## What Changed

- **Section 7 (statutory interest rate):** where interest is owed but no rate is fixed
  by contract or by an express provision of law, the rate is now **3% per annum** (down
  from 7.5%). The rate may be adjusted up or down by Royal Decree to match economic
  conditions; the Ministry of Finance is to review it **every three years**, targeting a
  level close to the average of commercial-bank deposit and lending rates.
- **Section 224 (default interest):** interest during a period of default is the
  Section 7 rate **plus 2% per annum** (i.e. effectively 5% where the base is 3%), unless
  a higher rate is lawfully justified. **Compounding of default interest is prohibited.**
- **New Section 224/1:** where a debtor pays a money debt in instalments and defaults on
  an instalment, the creditor may charge default interest **only on the principal of the
  defaulted instalment** — not the whole outstanding balance. Any contrary agreement is
  **void**.

## Transitional Application

The amended Sections 7 and 224 apply to interest **falling due on or after** the decree's
effective date; interest accrued for periods before that date is unaffected. Section
224/1 applies to default interest on instalments falling due on or after the effective
date.

## Why It Matters

Brooker's lending and structured-loan activity (see [[structured-loan-portfolio]]) is
directly affected: any loan agreement that is silent on interest now defaults to 3%, not
7.5%; default interest is capped at base + 2% with no compounding; and instalment-default
interest must be charged only on the defaulted instalment's principal. Loan documentation
and arrears calculations must reflect these limits.
