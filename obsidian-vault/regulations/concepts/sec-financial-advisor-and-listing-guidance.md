# SEC Financial-Advisor Supervision and Listing Guidance

## Summary

The SEC's `กลต. (SEC)` reference set in this batch covers the role of the **financial
advisor (FA)** in securities offerings and listing, and the SEC's supervision of FAs.
A licensed FA on the SEC-approved list must accompany a company through an IPO/listing
application; the SEC both publishes guidance to FAs and disciplines FAs for misconduct.

## Components

- **Pre-filing consultation guidance** (เวียน ม.ค. 2559) — the SEC encourages FAs to
  consult the SEC *before* filing a share-offering application, to resolve qualification
  and disclosure issues early and meet the timeframes of the **Licensing Facilitation Act
  B.E. 2558**. Summarised in [[source-sec-pre-filing-consultation-guide]].
- **IPO and Listing guide** — the *Going Public Guide 2018* on entering SET and MAI: the
  process and qualifications for a company to become listed.
- **SET Listed-Company Manual 2014** — the operational handbook for listed companies'
  ongoing obligations.
- **FA disciplinary action** — a 28 November 2561 SEC announcement penalising a financial
  advisor and its supervising controller, illustrating the SEC's enforcement of FA conduct
  standards.
- **FA list** — the SEC-approved list of FA firms (as of 8 September 2559 / 2016).

## Relevance

Whenever Brooker takes a company public, restructures via a listing, or appoints advisers
for an offering, an SEC-approved FA must be engaged and the SEC pre-filing-consultation
route is available to de-risk the application. The FA disciplinary precedent underscores
that the FA and its controller bear personal regulatory responsibility for the quality of
an application.

## Agent Notes

The Going Public Guide (2018) and the Listed-Company Manual (2014) are large reference
manuals — treat them as background handbooks, not point-in-time rules. The FA list (2016)
is stale; verify current FA approval status against the live SEC list.
