# Takeover & Mandatory Tender Offer Regime (Chapter 8 Part 2, SEA)

## Summary

The acquisition of securities for business takeover (การเข้าถือหลักทรัพย์เพื่อครอบงำกิจการ)
is governed by **Chapter 8, Part 2 of the Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535 (1992)**,
Sections 245–253, supplemented by Capital Market Supervisory Board (คณะกรรมการกำกับ
ตลาดทุน) notifications. It is Thailand's mandatory tender offer (MTO) framework: a person
crossing key ownership thresholds in a listed or public company must report and, at the
trigger point, make a general offer to all remaining shareholders.

## Key Thresholds

| Threshold | Trigger | Obligation |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Every **5%** of voting rights | Section 246 — each 5% increase or decrease in holdings | Report the change to the SEC Office each time (Form 246-2) |
| **25%** of voting rights | Section 247 — reaching 25% or more, alone or acting in concert | Constitutes a "business takeover"; mandatory tender offer for all securities of the target |
| **50%** and **75%** | Further chain-trigger points | Renewed MTO obligation on further acquisition past these points |

"Securities" (หลักทรัพย์) here covers shares, warrants and other convertible
instruments; "business" (กิจการ) means a SET-listed company, a company traded on a
securities centre, or a public limited company designated by the Capital Market
Supervisory Board.

## Why It Matters

Any Brooker acquisition that lifts a stake in a Thai listed company past 25% — or any
inbound investor crossing the line in a Brooker-controlled vehicle — triggers a costly
all-shares offer unless a whitewash waiver is obtained. The 5% reporting steps make stake
accumulation publicly visible well before the MTO point.
