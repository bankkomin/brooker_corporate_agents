# Migration from mai to SET (Market Transfer)

## Summary

A listed company may transfer its securities from the **Market for Alternative Investment
(mai)** to the **Stock Exchange of Thailand (SET)** — the change of "secondary market"
(การเปลี่ยนแปลงตลาดรองสำหรับการซื้อขายหลักทรัพย์). mai is SET's market for smaller and
growth companies; SET is the main board with larger size and free-float requirements.
A company that has grown to meet SET's listing criteria can apply to move up, retaining
its trading symbol while changing market, industry group and business sector designations.

## How It Works

The transfer is a market move, not a new listing of a different security: the company
keeps its ticker. SET reassigns the industry group and business sector and announces the
effective date in advance. The governing framework is SET's circular on the acceptance of
listed securities (หนังสือเวียนการรับหลักทรัพย์จดทะเบียน).

## Worked Example — JKN

**JKN Global Media PCL (JKN)** moved from **mai to SET**, effective **12 November 2020**.
JKN — a content-licensing and advertising-airtime business — stayed in the **Services**
industry group and was placed in the new business sector **Media & Publishing**. This
folder retains the JKN case as the 2022 sample of a mai-to-SET migration.

## Why It Matters

Moving to SET typically improves liquidity, index eligibility and institutional-investor
access, at the cost of meeting the main board's larger size and distribution thresholds.
The JKN case is held as a practical template for how the announcement and effective-date
mechanics work.
