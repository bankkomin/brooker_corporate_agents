# Source: Digital Asset Investment — IFA Supporting Information & Internal Control (2021)

## Document Overview

**As of 26 August 2021.** Two internal documents prepared for the **Independent
Financial Advisor (IFA)** in connection with Brooker's 2021 digital-asset
investment: a 44-slide deck of supporting research and a process memo on
exchange/wallet internal controls. Folder `Additional IFA (received From
K.Andrew date 27-Aug-21)`. These are **company-prepared materials**, not SEC
regulation — held in the regulations source set as the IFA-disclosure record.

## Key Findings

- **Mandate & timeline.** The Board (25 Feb 2021) approved a THB 1,500m digital
  asset investment plan and a Digital Asset Department, funded partly by a
  THB 568m rights offering. First BTC purchase 29 Mar 2021 (122.31 BTC for
  THB 205m); the Risk Committee later cut exposure to ~THB 1,200m amid volatility.
- **Strategy.** Diversified, top-down portfolio (≈60% big caps / 40% mid caps);
  buy-and-hold with no stop-losses; max THB 50m per DeFi project; rebalancing
  recycles profit into BTC/ETH/BNB and digital-dollar stablecoins.
- **Custody / venues.** Coinbase (US-SEC regulated, NASDAQ-listed) as primary
  on-ramp; Binance for breadth; **Fireblocks** self-custody wallet for DeFi
  (see [[fireblocks]], [[binance-bnb-otc]]). The deck explicitly advised the IFA
  **not** to disclose Fireblocks use for security reasons.
- **Internal control.** Two-person login on every exchange; CFO holds the 2FA
  key; Binance withdrawals need 3 keys (Email/2FA/Mobile); USD fiat moves only
  between same-name linked accounts (Brooker International Co., Ltd HSBC);
  USD 500,000 daily withdrawal limit; 48-hour crypto whitelist approval;
  Fireblocks requires all Project Owner + Admin sign-off; weekly statement
  monitoring; auditor view-only access.

## Relevance

Records how Brooker structured and controlled its 2021 digital-asset entry for
IFA review. Predates the 2024 SEC utility-token reforms (see
[[utility-token-regulation]]); useful as historical context for digital-asset
governance and the tax position (offshore via BICL HK).
