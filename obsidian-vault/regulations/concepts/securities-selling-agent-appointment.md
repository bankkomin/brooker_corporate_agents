# Appointment of Securities Selling Agents

## Summary

This topic covers the SEC framework governing **securities selling agents**
(ตัวแทนจำหน่ายหลักทรัพย์) — the rules, conditions and method by which a securities
business may appoint an agent to distribute securities, together with the related rules
on the **prohibited characteristics of capital-market personnel** and the **approval and
conduct standards for investor-contact persons** (ผู้ติดต่อกับผู้ลงทุน). The notifications
are issued by the SEC under the Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535.

## Key Components

- **TorThor. 54/2552** — Rules, conditions and method for appointing a securities selling
  agent. Sets the qualification requirements an appointed distribution agent must meet and
  the conditions under which a securities firm may delegate distribution.
- **TorThor-Nor-Khor. 37/2553** — **Prohibited characteristics** (ลักษณะต้องห้าม) of
  personnel in the capital-market business — a consolidated notification disqualifying
  individuals with specified adverse records from acting as capital-market personnel.
- **SorKhor. 15/2548** — Approval of **investor-contact persons** and the **standards of
  conduct** they must observe when dealing with investors.

## Relevance

Relevant to Brooker where the group, or a Brooker-managed fund or issuer, distributes
securities through appointed agents, or engages investor-contact staff — the personnel
prohibited-characteristics screen and the conduct standards apply to those individuals.
