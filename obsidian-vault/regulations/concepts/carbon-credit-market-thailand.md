# Carbon Credit Market — Thailand Context & Tax Support

## Summary

**Carbon credits (คาร์บอนเครดิต)** are tradable units representing verified reductions in
greenhouse-gas emissions. A business that reduces emissions below its target can have the
surplus certified as carbon credits and sell them; emitters above their target buy credits
to offset. The trading venue is a **carbon market (ตลาดคาร์บอน)**, often run as an
Emission Trading Scheme (ETS).

This folder is **market-context and tax material rather than capital-market regulation** —
it is the lightest-coverage topic in the batch.

## Key Metrics

From the March 2023 market commentary (Share2Trade):
- Carbon credits emerged as a strategic theme for Thai listed companies from 2022, with
  several announcing carbon-credit business plans.
- Global context cited: ~45 countries used carbon-pricing measures by 2021 with ~65 more
  in progress; global carbon-credit trading value reported around USD 851bn; the World
  Bank counted ~64 carbon markets. Price per credit (1 metric tonne) was projected to rise
  ~88% to roughly USD 67/tonne by 2030.
- A notable Thai example: Energy Absolute (EA) received a Letter of Authorization (LoA),
  approved by Cabinet on 31 January 2023, to transfer carbon credits from its electric-bus
  conversion project (2021–2030) to the Swiss KLiK Foundation.
- A short companion item describes **tax-support measures for carbon credits** (มาตรการ
  สนับสนุน cc ทางภาษี) — government incentives for carbon-credit activity.

## Historical Context

Thailand's carbon-credit interest accelerated through 2022–2023 alongside the global ESG
and net-zero agenda. Carbon credits are voluntary-market instruments distinct from
SEC-regulated securities; their relevance to the `regulations` area is as a business and
tax-incentive theme touching listed companies.
