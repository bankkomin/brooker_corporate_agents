# Source — TorChor. 46/2568: Rules on Connected Transactions

## Document Overview

Notification of the Capital Market Supervisory Board No. **TorChor. 46/2568** — "Rules on
Connected Transactions" (หลักเกณฑ์ในการทำรายการที่เกี่ยวโยงกัน), dated **19 December
2025**, issued under Sections 16/6(2)(1), 89/1, 89/12, 89/13 and 89/27 of the Securities
and Exchange Act B.E. 2535 (as amended No. 4). Effective **1 July 2026**. It repeals
four prior notifications: TorChor. 21/2551, 8/2552, 20/2553 and 79/2558.

## Key Findings

- **Scope (Clause 4):** listed companies and Section-56-reporting public companies.
- **Connected transaction defined (Clause 6):** any transaction between the company (or a
  subsidiary) and a **connected person** per Appendix 1 — acquisition/disposal of assets,
  leasing, giving/receiving services, giving/receiving financial assistance, new
  securities issuance, and the creation or waiver of rights to do so. The
  **substance-over-form** principle applies.
- **Key definitions:**
  - *Major shareholder* (ผู้ถือหุ้นรายใหญ่) — holding, directly or indirectly,
    **> 10%** of a juristic person's voting shares, counting shares of related persons.
  - *Related person* (ผู้ที่เกี่ยวข้อง) — spouse; minor children; ordinary partnerships
    where the person/spouse/child is a partner; limited partnerships where they are an
    unlimited partner, or limited partners holding **> 30%**; companies in which they (or
    those partnerships) hold **> 30%**; nested 30%-controlled companies; and juristic
    persons they can manage as representative.
  - *Financial assistance* — giving/receiving loans, guarantees, pledging assets as debt
    security, and similar conduct.
- **Exclusions (Clause 7):** Section 89/12(1)(2)(3)(a) transactions; new-securities
  issuance to connected persons via underwriting, pro-rata rights, or director/employee
  offerings; and transactions with an investee where the only connection is a seconded
  top executive.
- **Approval and size (Part 2 onward):** financial-assistance and other connected
  transactions are sized and routed to board or shareholder approval by value bands,
  with IFA opinions for larger transactions — mirroring the MT structure but applied to
  the connected-party dimension.

## Relevance

The RPT half of the [[material-transactions-rpt-2026-reform]] package. Critical for
Brooker because related-party financing (intra-group loans, guarantees) and any
transaction with directors, major shareholders or their affiliates must be tested under
these rules from 1 July 2026.
