# Labour Protection Act B.E. 2541 (1998)

## Summary

The **Labour Protection Act B.E. 2541** (พระราชบัญญัติคุ้มครองแรงงาน พ.ศ. ๒๕๔๑) is
Thailand's principal employment-protection statute, governing the relationship between
employers and employees — working hours, holidays, leave, wages, welfare, occupational
safety, severance pay and the Labour Inspector regime. Enacted **12 February 1998**, it
took effect 180 days after publication in the Government Gazette (i.e. from **19 August
1998**), repealing the earlier Revolutionary Council Announcement No. 103 (1972) regime.

## Scope and Exclusions

The Act does **not** apply to (1) central, provincial and local government, and (2) state
enterprises governed by the State Enterprise Labour Relations law — among other specific
exclusions set out in section 4.

## Why It Matters

The Act is the baseline for any Thai employer's HR and severance obligations. While the
broader `regulations` area focuses on Thai SEC / SET securities rules, the Labour
Protection Act is the cross-cutting employment-law reference held alongside them, relevant
to workforce restructuring, severance liabilities and compliance.
