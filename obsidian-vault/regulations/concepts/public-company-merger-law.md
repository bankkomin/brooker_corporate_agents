# Public Company Law & Business Amalgamation Framework

## Summary

This article covers the corporate-law foundations relevant to Brooker Group entities: the
legal characteristics of **public limited companies (บริษัทมหาชนจำกัด)** versus private
**limited companies (บริษัทจำกัด)**, and the framework for **business amalgamation /
mergers (การควบรวมกิจการ)**. The source folder ("Law") is a reference library of statute
extracts and explanatory materials rather than a single regulation.

## Key Metrics

**Two corporate forms:**
- *Private limited company (บริษัทจำกัด)* — formed under the Civil and Commercial Code,
  Book 3, Title 22; no public share offering; smaller shareholder base.
- *Public limited company (บริษัทมหาชนจำกัด)* — formed under the Public Limited Companies
  Act; may offer shares to the public and list on the SET; subject to enhanced disclosure
  and governance, and (once listed) to SEC/SET notifications.

**Mergers and amalgamations:** Thai law distinguishes *amalgamation* (การควบบริษัท — two
companies combining into a new juristic person) from *acquisitions*. The reference
materials trace M&A history through five "waves" — horizontal, vertical (backward/forward
integration), conglomerate, takeover, and the current cross-border wave — and note the
tax-planning considerations that precede any merger strategy. Civil and Commercial Code
Book 3, Title 22 governs the amalgamation procedure for limited companies.

A companion item in the folder is the **Treasury Stock manual** (share buy-back guidance,
August 2008), which sits alongside merger law as a corporate-restructuring tool.

## Historical Context

The "Law" folder materials are mostly dated 2008 and earlier — explanatory papers, Civil
and Commercial Code extracts, and case studies (e.g. Bank of Ayudhya / GECAL merger). They
serve as background reference; current listed-company M&A is governed by the more specific
SEC/SET notifications on acquisitions/disposals, tender offers, and the 2026 MT/RPT reform.
