# Distribution & Allotment of Newly Issued Shares and Warrants — ทจ. 40/2557

## Summary

Capital Market Supervisory Board notification **ทจ. 40/2557** governs how a company
issuing equity instruments distributes (จำหน่าย), takes subscriptions for, and
allots **newly issued shares and warrants** (ใบสำคัญแสดงสิทธิที่จะซื้อหุ้น) to the
public. It is issued under Sections 16/6, 89/27 and 35 of the
[[securities-and-exchange-act]] and **takes effect 16 December 2014**, replacing nine
earlier notifications (ทจ. 29/2551 and amendments). It was itself amended by ทจ.
15/2558 (effective 16 May 2015).

## Key Points

### Scope
Applies to companies issuing equity instruments that file an application and obtain
SEC approval to offer securities to the public (public offering) and must file a
registration statement and draft prospectus — but **not** to securities-swap offers
made to restructure shareholding.

### Distribution (Chapter 2)
- The issuer must conduct solicitation, recommendation or sale **through a securities
  company licensed for securities underwriting**, with limited exceptions (e.g. an
  issuer that is itself an underwriter offering warrants; a **preferential public
  offering** restricted to existing shareholders in proportion to their holding).
- The issuer must not set underwriter fees in a way that improperly induces the
  underwriter to push the securities regardless of investor suitability.
- In the **book-building** stage of an IPO, the issuer must ensure that demand and
  prices from **related persons** are excluded from price determination.

### Subscription (Chapter 3)
- A written/electronic subscription form is the minimum subscription method; the form
  must carry the subscriber's identification details and a risk warning.
- Subscription monies in excess of the expected offering proceeds must not be used and
  must be refunded to non-allotted subscribers **within 14 days** of the offer close.
- If the issuer halts the offer or cannot deliver, subscribers may cancel and be
  refunded within 14 days of the cancellation period closing.
- Late refunds bear interest of **not less than 7.5% per annum**.

### Allotment (Chapter 4)
- The issuer must clearly segregate the securities allotted to different groups and
  disclose this in the registration statement and prospectus.
- The issuer **must not allot shares to its own subsidiaries**.
- Where the issuer wishes to allot to **related persons** (บุคคลที่มีความสัมพันธ์ —
  directors, executives, major shareholders, controlling persons, the parent, and
  connected persons), it must segregate and disclose them; for an **IPO**, allotment
  to related persons is capped at **25% of (I − S)**, where *I* = shares approved for
  this offer and *S* = shares set aside for existing shareholders of the listed parent
  pro rata.
- For an IPO that places shares with **patrons** (ผู้มีอุปการคุณ — customers,
  suppliers, etc.), the issuer must report patron allotments of **≥ 1 million baht**
  to its board on **Form 35-IPO-1M**.

## Silent Period

The folder also holds SET circular **บจ. (ว) 17/2558** on the **Silent Period**
(ระยะเวลาห้ามขายหุ้น) — the post-offering lock-up barring strategic shareholders from
selling shares for a defined period after a share or warrant offering. This concept
covers the distribution/allotment notification; the silent-period rule is referenced
here and cross-cuts other regulations batches.
