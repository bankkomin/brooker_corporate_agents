## General Principle (Background)

Under Thai VAT rules, VAT applies to the **supply of goods or services**. A pure
**penalty / compensation for damage** that is not consideration for a supply is
generally **outside the VAT base**, whereas a payment that is in substance additional
consideration for a taxable supply can be VAT-able. The correct treatment depends on the
**substance of the payment**, not its "penalty" label — which is precisely the question
the source folder raises. This general statement is **background context only** and is
not drawn from the (unreadable) source documents.
