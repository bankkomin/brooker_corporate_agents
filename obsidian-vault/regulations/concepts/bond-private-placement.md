# Private Placement of Debt Instruments (หุ้นกู้ — เสนอขายในวงจำกัด)

## Summary

This concept covers the Thai SEC Capital Market Supervisory Board (คณะกรรมการกำกับ
ตลาดทุน) regime for **offering newly issued debt instruments to limited groups of
persons** (private placement, PP) and for offering **convertible debentures to persons of
specific characteristics**. The governing instrument is **Notification No. TorJor. 16/2565
(ทจ. 16/2565)**, effective **1 October 2022**.

## Scope — What Counts as a Debt Instrument

Under TorJor. 16/2565, "ตราสารหนี้" (debt instruments) eligible for private placement are
newly issued:

1. Debentures (หุ้นกู้)
2. Bonds (พันธบัตร)
3. Bills / promissory notes (ตั๋วเงิน)

Defined sub-types include **convertible debentures** (หุ้นกู้แปลงสภาพ — convertible into
newly issued shares of the issuer), **reserved shares** (หุ้นรองรับ — shares set aside to
support conversion), and **subordinated debentures** (หุ้นกู้ด้อยสิทธิ — unsecured
debentures ranking below ordinary creditors).

## Legal Basis

TorJor. 16/2565 is issued under Sections 16/6, 89/27, 35, 40(11), 41(3)–(4), 42(10) and
43(3) of the Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535 (as amended by Act No. 4, B.E. 2551).
It must be read together with the SEC notification defining standard terms for all
debt-instrument offerings, and with the **post-issuance terms in Appendix 3** (ภาคผนวก 3)
— continuing conditions the issuer must observe after the offering.

## Filing Requirements

Issuers must file a registration statement (แบบแสดงรายการข้อมูล) for debt-instrument
offerings under **Notification No. TorJor. 19/2565 (ทจ. 19/2565)**, which sets the form
and disclosure content for the debt-instrument registration statement and prospectus.

## Why It Matters

Private placement is the lower-cost, faster route for a listed company to raise debt
without a full public offering. Brooker-type issuers must confirm an offering genuinely
falls within the "limited persons" definition; otherwise the full public-offering regime,
with heavier disclosure, applies. Appendix 3 post-issuance covenants (e.g. financial-ratio
maintenance) bind the issuer for the life of the instrument.
