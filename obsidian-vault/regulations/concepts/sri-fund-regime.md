# SRI Fund Disclosure Regime — Sustainable & Responsible Investing Funds

## Summary

The **SRI Fund** (Sustainable and Responsible Investing Fund — กองทุนรวมเพื่อความยั่งยืน)
is a Thai SEC ([[thai-sec]]) regulatory classification for mutual funds that focus their
investment on issuers committed to sustainability per international norms (UN SDGs, UN
Global Compact). In **April 2022** the SEC issued a **disclosure guideline** that requires
any asset-management company (บริษัทหลักทรัพย์จัดการกองทุน — "บลจ." / AMC) managing an
SRI Fund to disclose a defined, uniform set of sustainability information so investors can
compare funds and so the market is protected from **greenwashing** (การฟอกเขียว).

The regime is a *labelling and disclosure* standard, not a product-approval gate: it
defines what a fund must publish to call itself an SRI Fund.

## What the Regime Requires

An AMC managing an SRI Fund must disclose, under a single common standard:

- **Sustainability-focused investment policy** — that the fund invests mainly in issuers
  prioritising sustainability per international standards (SDGs, UN Global Compact).
- **Investment strategy** — e.g. ESG Integration, best-in-class selection, index tracking
  of an ESG index (SET ESG / SETTHSI).
- **Positive social and environmental impact** the fund targets.
- Information sufficient for investors to **compare** one SRI Fund against another.

The standardised disclosure is the core anti-greenwashing mechanism: it prevents a fund
being marketed as sustainable when in substance it is not.

## How It Relates to Thai ESG Funds

Many funds in the archive carry **both** the SRI Fund label and the **Thai ESG**
tax-privileged status ([[thai-esg-fund]]). These are distinct: SRI Fund is a *disclosure
label*; Thai ESG is a *tax-incentive product class* (personal income tax deduction). A
single fund — or fund share class — can be both. The 16/47-fund archive consolidates the
SEC-approved list as it stood in early 2024.

## Cross-Border Extension

- **ASEAN SRFS** — SRI Funds disclosing fully per the [[asean-srfs-standard]] may be
  offered across ASEAN markets more easily.
- **HK-TH MRF** — the SEC was negotiating with Hong Kong's Securities and Futures
  Commission so Thai AMCs could offer SRI Funds in Hong Kong under the Hong Kong–Thailand
  Mutual Recognition of Funds scheme.

## Agent Notes

When a fund describes itself as an "SRI Fund", treat that as a SEC disclosure label
carrying mandatory standardised sustainability reporting — verify it against the SEC's
sustainablefinance.sec.or.th list. Do not conflate SRI Fund (disclosure label) with Thai
ESG (tax product). This archive is point-in-time (early 2024); the approved-fund count
grows over time.
