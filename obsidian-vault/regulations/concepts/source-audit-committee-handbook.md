# Source: SEC Audit Committee Handbook (2553 & 2557 editions)

## Document Overview

**As-of:** the Thai SEC's **Audit Committee Handbook** (คู่มือการปฏิบัติงานของคณะกรรมการ
ตรวจสอบ) — a practice manual for audit committees of supervised entities. Two editions are
held: **2553 (2010)** and **2557 (2014)**. The 2557 edition carries a foreword from the
Chairman of the SEC Audit Committee dated 28 February 2557.

## Key Findings

- The handbook is issued in line with the SEC **Code of Governance**, which directs that
  governance, ethics, conflict-of-interest, and stakeholder policies be set out in a
  manual to build understanding and support consistent practice.
- It covers: the **purpose** of establishing an audit committee; **structure and
  composition** (appointment, qualifications, term, vacating office, chairman, secretary,
  onboarding); **duties and responsibilities** in five areas — internal control & risk
  management, legal/ethics compliance, financial reporting, relationship with management,
  and relationship with internal audit; **meeting practice**; and **effectiveness
  factors** (charter, self-evaluation, resources, training).
- The audit committee's authority derives from the Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535.

## Relevance

The primary practice document behind the [[audit-committee]] concept. It is the reference
a Brooker-type listed company's audit committee uses to structure its charter and
workplan.
