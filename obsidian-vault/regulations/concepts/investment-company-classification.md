# SET Investment Company Classification Rule

## Summary

The Stock Exchange of Thailand revised its listing-admission and listed-company rules so
that listing applicants and listed companies on SET and MAI **must not operate as an
"Investment Company"** (บริษัทที่มีลักษณะการประกอบธุรกิจบริหารจัดการเงินลงทุน).
The rule, set out in the 31 May 2024 SET package and SEC practice guideline
**NorPor. 5/2566** (issued 15 December 2023), **takes effect from financial statements
ending on or after 1 July 2024**, with first assessment based on statements from 1 August
2024. This is a SET rule distinct from — though related to — the 40% threshold described
in the CEO knowledge base's [[investment-company-40-percent-rule]].

## What Counts as an Investment Company

A company is an Investment Company where its **investment in securities / derivatives /
digital assets exceeds 40% of total assets**, where those investments are made to earn
returns from appreciation, interest or dividends. The 40% calculation:

- **Includes** investments held by non-financial subsidiaries, and the company's
  investment in shares of affiliates that themselves carry such investments and are not
  financial institutions.
- **Includes** holdings above ~20% of an investee where the company has **no significant
  influence** over its financial and operating policy.

### Investments Excluded from the 40% Calculation

- **Low-risk liquidity-management investments** — government bonds, debt instruments fully
  and unconditionally principal-and-interest guaranteed by the Ministry of Finance, and
  units of money-market or fixed-income mutual funds. (State-enterprise bonds guaranteed
  by the MoF are **not** excluded.)
- **Investments in associates / joint ventures** where the company has decision-making
  participation — unless that associate is itself an Investment Company or holds
  investments > 40% of its total assets.
- **Synergy / value-chain investments** — investments that support, cooperate with or
  complete the company's own business along its value chain.
- **Investments in affiliates under the same parent**, or networked companies with a
  demonstrable cooperation policy.
- Debt instruments issued by an associate purely as financial assistance to that
  associate (counts as part of the associate investment); but buying an associate's bonds
  offered to the public/institutions for yield **is** counted.
- Speculative "study/learning" stakes taken to evaluate a business are **not** exempt.

## Consequences and Remediation Timeline

For a company that **newly** becomes an Investment Company:
1. SET posts the **"CC" (Non-Compliance)** sign — a **1-quarter** remediation window.
2. If unresolved after 1 quarter, SET replaces "CC" with **"SP" (Suspension)**.
3. If unresolved after **1 year** of "SP", SET considers **delisting** the securities.

For companies **already** Investment Companies at the rule's start (assessed on
statements ending before 1 August 2024), SET grants a **2-year grace period** from the
date the company first had a duty to file statements. The grace period ends early if the
company raises its investment ratio, or cuts it enough to no longer qualify.

## Brooker Relevance

SET formally notified **Brooker Group (BTC)** and Super Turtle (TURTLE) on 15 August 2024
that they qualified as Investment Companies. **BTC's grace period ends 14–15 August
2026.** If BTC has not cut its investment ratio below 40% by then, SET will escalate
through the CC → SP → delisting path. This is the binding regulatory deadline behind the
group's divestment and VCC-restructuring strategy. The new
[[material-transactions-rpt-2026-reform]] rules apply in parallel to any divestment
transactions BTC executes to remediate.
