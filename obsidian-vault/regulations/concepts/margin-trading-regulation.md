# Margin Trading of Securities (การซื้อขายหลักทรัพย์ประเภทมาร์จิ้น)

## Summary

**Margin trading** is the trading of securities in which a SET member (broker) lends a
client money to buy securities, or lends securities to a client for short selling. It is
governed by the **SET Regulation on Margin Securities Trading B.E. 2543 (2000)**, issued
under Section 170, paragraph 2 (8) and (9) of the **Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535**.
The 2543 regulation **took effect on 18 September 2000** and replaced the earlier 1998
(B.E. 2541) margin regulation.

## Key Definitions

- **Margin securities trading** (ซื้อขายหลักทรัพย์ประเภทมาร์จิ้น) — securities trading in
  which the member lends a client money to buy securities, or lends securities for short
  selling.
- **Short selling** (ขายชอร์ต) — selling securities that must be borrowed for delivery.
- **Client** (ลูกค้า) — a person who enters a money-loan contract with a member to buy
  securities, or a securities-borrowing contract for short selling (excluding institutional
  short-sale borrowers).
- **Margin account** (บัญชีมาร์จิ้น) — the account recording margin securities trades.
- **Initial margin rate** (อัตรามาร์จิ้นเริ่มต้น) — the initial margin rate set by the SEC
  Office's notification on lending money to / lending securities to non-institutional
  clients (definition substituted by the 2545/2002 amendment, effective 6 March 2002).
- **Client assets** (ทรัพย์สินของลูกค้า) — the net value of cash, securities and other
  collateral in the client's margin account, after deducting liabilities arising from
  margin-account purchases or sales.
- **Client excess assets** (ทรัพย์สินส่วนเกินของลูกค้า) — client assets above the level the
  client must maintain at the member's required rate.

## Why It Matters

These rules define how brokers extend leverage to investors and how short selling is
collateralised. For a Brooker group entity using margin facilities for portfolio
positions, the initial-margin and maintenance-margin framework, and the distinction
between client assets and excess assets, determine borrowing capacity and forced-sale
(margin-call) exposure.
