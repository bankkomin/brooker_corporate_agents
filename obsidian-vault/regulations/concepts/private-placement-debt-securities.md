# Private Placement of Debt Securities (PP) — ThorJor.16/2565

## Summary

This concept covers the SEC Capital Market Supervisory Board's framework for offering
**newly-issued debt securities to a limited number of persons (private placement, PP)**
and offering **convertible bonds to persons with specific characteristics**. The
governing instrument is **Capital Market Supervisory Board Notification ThorJor.16/2565**,
*Application and approval for the offer of newly-issued debt securities to persons in a
limited circle, and the offer of convertible bonds to persons with specific
characteristics* — effective **1 October 2022**. The companion notification
**ThorJor.19/2565**, *Filing of the registration statement for the offer of debt
securities* (also effective 1 October 2022), governs the disclosure filing and
consolidates eight earlier registration-statement notifications (ThorJor.10/2556 through
ThorJor.83/2558) which it repeals.

## Scope

"Debt securities" (ตราสารหนี้) under ThorJor.16/2565 means newly-issued (1) bonds
(หุ้นกู้), (2) debentures/government bonds (พันธบัตร) and (3) bills (ตั๋วเงิน).
"Convertible bonds" (หุ้นกู้แปลงสภาพ) are bonds whose rights may convert into the
issuer's newly-issued shares; "reserved shares" (หุ้นรองรับ) back that conversion.
The notification draws authority from sections 16/6, 89/27, 35, 40(11), 41, 42(10) and
43(3) of the Securities and Exchange Act B.E. 2535 (as amended).

## Key Features

- **General approval vs. case-by-case approval:** the rules distinguish offers approved
  generally from those requiring an application to the SEC Office.
- **Subordinated bonds** (หุ้นกู้ด้อยสิทธิ) — unsecured bonds whose holders rank below
  ordinary creditors — are defined and regulated.
- A **financial advisor** approved by the SEC and the **Thai Bond Market Association**
  (สมาคมตลาดตราสารหนี้ไทย) feature in the framework.
- Three appendices set out (1) an **exemption schedule** from the general criteria —
  e.g. for debt issued by Thai government agencies, foreign entities, and special-purpose
  vehicles; (2) **additional approval criteria and post-approval conditions**; and (3)
  **post-approval criteria for convertible bond offerings**.

## Why It Matters

The PP route lets issuers raise debt funding from a limited circle of (typically
institutional or high-net-worth) investors with lighter requirements than a public
offering, while ThorJor.19/2565 standardises the disclosure filing. For a corporate
issuer this is the principal regime to consult before structuring a non-public bond
raise.
