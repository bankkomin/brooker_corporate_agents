# Acting in Concert & Section 258 Securities Aggregation

## Summary

When the Thai SEC ([[thai-sec]]) tests whether a takeover-threshold has been crossed, it
does not count only the securities directly held by one person. **Section 258** of the
Securities and Exchange Act and the **acting-in-concert** (การกระทำร่วมกัน, "acting in
concert") concept aggregate the securities of related and coordinating persons as if held
by a single person. This determines when a mandatory tender offer is triggered (Section 247
governs acquisition of securities for business takeover).

## Section 258 Persons

Section 258 lists categories of persons whose securities are counted together with a given
person's (e.g. spouse, minor children, and controlled/related entities). The SEC publishes
the detailed list at sec.or.th. The aggregation prevents a buyer splitting a stake across
related parties to stay below disclosure or tender-offer thresholds.

## Acting in Concert

Beyond Section 258, the SEC also aggregates persons whose relationship or behaviour shows
they act jointly with another, judged by **intent** and **behaviour**:

- **Intent** — jointly exercising voting rights in the same direction, or letting another
  exercise one's votes, to jointly control the company.
- **Behaviour examples:**
  - An agreement (oral or written) to vote the same way on significant company matters.
  - A **standstill agreement** on maintaining or changing securities holdings.
  - Soliciting others to acquire or dispose of the company's securities at the same or a
    close time.
  - Sharing a funding source, or assisting another to obtain funding used to acquire the
    company's securities.
  - Acquiring/disposing of the company's securities together with persons with whom one has
    acted in concert in the past.
  - Habitually and continuously letting another exercise one's votes at shareholder
    meetings (excluding proxies given to an independent director, a custodian, or a proxy
    voting service).
  - Being a partner, director, or employee of a legal entity, with behaviour showing the
    securities are held for or jointly with the employer entity.
  - Gifting the company's securities to another, or trading them at a low price without
    reasonable cause.

## Why It Matters

Any Brooker acquisition of a Thai-listed company must aggregate Section 258 persons and
acting-in-concert parties before assessing tender-offer triggers. Co-investment agreements,
voting arrangements, and shared financing can all pull counterparties into a single
aggregated holding — an undisclosed concert party can convert a sub-threshold stake into a
mandatory tender offer.
