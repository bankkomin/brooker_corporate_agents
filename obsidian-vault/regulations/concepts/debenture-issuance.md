# Debenture & Convertible Debenture Issuance (หุ้นกู้ / หุ้นกู้แปลงสภาพ)

## Summary

A **debenture** (หุ้นกู้) is a debt instrument issued by a company to raise funds from
investors. A **convertible debenture** (หุ้นกู้แปลงสภาพ) is a debenture whose holders may
convert their rights into newly issued shares of the issuer. Thai SEC (ก.ล.ต.) governs the
issuance, offering and post-issuance conduct of both, layered on top of the Securities and
Exchange Act B.E. 2535. This article covers the issuer-side considerations a Thai listed
company faces when raising debt capital.

## Key Considerations for Convertible Debenture Offerings

The source material flags the following points an issuer must address:

- A convertible debenture is treated **the same as an ordinary debenture** for offering
  purposes — the same approval, filing, fee and credit-rating requirements apply.
- Conversion rights must be **exercisable within the life of the debenture** (i.e. the
  conversion window cannot outlast the instrument's tenor).
- **Reserved-share ceiling**: the number of shares reserved to support this offering, when
  aggregated with shares reserved for other convertible securities or warrants (excluding
  ESOP warrants), must **not exceed 50% of total issued shares**.
- If a convertible debenture is offered at **more than 10% below market price**, the issuer
  must disclose the investors' names and information, the board's opinion on price
  appropriateness, the price derivation and rationale, a board certification, and the
  rights of existing shareholders to object.
- Post-issuance covenants typically include maintaining a **D/E ratio** and a **cap on
  dividend payments** above a stated rate.

## Cost and Timing

- Issuance costs include the application/offering fee and the coupon (interest) rate.
- A **credit rating** must be obtained where the company has none — roughly **3–4 months**;
  a rating review takes around **2–3 weeks**.
- Favourable timing for a debenture issue: relatively low market interest rates, low
  debenture supply, and capital-market conditions less favourable to equity raising.

## Why It Matters

Debentures and convertible debentures are core capital-raising tools for a Brooker-type
listed holding company. The 50% reserved-share ceiling and the below-market-price
disclosure rules directly constrain how an issuer can structure a convertible deal, and the
credit-rating lead time must be built into any funding timeline.
