# Treasury Stock — Share Repurchase Rules (การซื้อหุ้นคืน)

## Summary

**Treasury stock** (หุ้นซื้อคืน) is a company's own shares bought back from the market and
held by the company. The **Public Limited Company Act B.E. 2535 (Section 66/1)** permits a
company to own its own shares in two cases: (1) where a shareholder **dissents** from a
shareholders' resolution amending the articles on voting or dividend rights, and (2) for
**financial management** (เพื่อบริหารทางการเงิน) when the company has accumulated profits
and excess liquidity. The detailed rules sit in the **Ministerial Regulation on Share
Repurchase, Disposal and Cancellation of Repurchased Shares B.E. 2544 (2001)** and SET
disclosure notifications (Bor.Jor./Por 11-04 and related circulars).

## Who May Repurchase & Approval

A listed company may repurchase its shares only if:

- Its **articles of association permit** share repurchase.
- If the repurchase is **≤ 10% of paid-up capital**, the **board of directors** may
  decide; if **> 10%**, the **shareholders' meeting** must approve and the repurchase must
  be completed **within 1 year**.

For a **financial-management** repurchase the company must additionally have:

- **Accumulated profit** in its separate (entity) financial statements — the repurchase
  amount may not exceed unappropriated retained earnings, which must be reserved until the
  treasury shares are fully resold or the related capital is reduced.
- **Excess liquidity** — judged by the ability to service debts over the next **6 months**
  from the repurchase start date; the repurchase must not impair debt service.
- Maintained **free float** — minority holding must not fall below **15%** of paid-up
  capital, and the company must have **at least 150 minority shareholders**.

## Methods, Pricing & Periods

| Method | Pricing | Period |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **Buy through SET (main board)** | Volume not over **10%** of paid-up shares; price not over **115%** of the prior 5-day average closing price | Buyback period **within 6 months** |
| **General offer** (with a financial advisor) | Price not specified (to be confirmed) | At least **10 days** but not more than **20 days** |

For **selling back** treasury shares:

- **Through SET** — sell-back price must be **not less than 85%** of the prior 5-day
  average closing price.
- **Public Offering (PO)** — no specified price rule; subject to SEC approval.

## Holding & Resale Deadline

- A **6-month grace period** runs from the last day of repurchase, during which the
  company may not resell.
- The **resale period is 2 years and 6 months** after the grace period.
- Therefore treasury shares must be resold (or the unsold balance cancelled by capital
  reduction) **within 3 years** of the last day of repurchase.

Any gain on a repurchase programme **cannot be booked as income** in profit or loss; the
difference between resale price and cost is recorded as an adjustment to the share-
repurchase reserve / premium or charged against retained earnings in equity.

## Why It Matters

Share repurchase is a core capital-management tool for a cash-rich, listed holding company
whose share price trades below intrinsic value — it returns surplus capital and can lift
per-share value. The retained-earnings cap, the 6-month liquidity test, and the 3-year
resale-or-cancel deadline are the binding constraints any Brooker buyback proposal must
clear.
