# Thai ESG Fund — Tax-Privileged Sustainable Fund Class

## Overview

**Thai ESG** (ชนิดไทยเพื่อความยั่งยืน / Thailand ESG Fund) is a tax-incentivised mutual
fund share-class category that Thai investors can use to claim a **personal income tax
deduction**. It is distinct from — but frequently combined with — the SEC's
[[sri-fund-regime|SRI Fund]] disclosure label: most funds in the SRI Fund archive carry a
"ชนิดไทยเพื่อความยั่งยืน" (Thai ESG) share class.

## Key Facts

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Category | Tax-privileged sustainable mutual fund share class |
| Tax benefit | Personal income tax deduction for unitholders (subject to holding-period and contribution-cap rules) |
| Typical mandate | ≥80% of NAV (annual average) in ESG-distinguished SET/mai-listed equities or sustainability-themed debt |
| Reference indices | SET ESG Total Return Index, SETTHSI TRI |
| Launch wave | Most Thai ESG funds in the archive were registered **18–19 December 2023** |
| Relationship to SRI Fund | A fund can hold both labels: Thai ESG = tax product; SRI Fund = SEC disclosure standard |

## Asset Managers Active in Thai ESG / SRI Funds

The archive's fund list spans most major Thai AMCs (บลจ.): Krung Thai (KTAM), Krungsri,
Kasikorn (K), KKP, KWI, TISCO, SCB Asset Management, Bualuang (BBLAM), Principal, UOB
Asset Management, LH Fund, ONE/วรรณ, Eastspring, MFC, and Asset Plus.
