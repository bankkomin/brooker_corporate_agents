# NBTC — National Broadcasting and Telecommunications Commission (กสทช.)

## Overview

The **NBTC** (National Broadcasting and Telecommunications Commission,
คณะกรรมการกิจการกระจายเสียง กิจการโทรทัศน์ และกิจการโทรคมนาคมแห่งชาติ; กสทช.) is Thailand's
independent regulator for **broadcasting, television and telecommunications**. It assigns
radio frequencies and regulates broadcasting and telecommunications services and operators.

## Key Facts

- **Statutory basis** — established under the **Act on Organisation to Assign Radio
  Frequency and to Regulate Broadcasting and Telecommunications Services B.E. 2553 (2010)**;
  also exercises powers under the **Telecommunications Business Act B.E. 2544 (2001)**.
- **Mandate** — frequency allocation; licensing and regulation of broadcasters and telecom
  operators; promotion of universal service and fair competition; oversight of telecom
  infrastructure (including shared infrastructure such as underground cable conduits).
- **Instruments** — issues notifications (ประกาศ กสทช.) published in the Government
  Gazette; later notifications supersede conflicting earlier ones.

## Relevance to Brooker

The NBTC is the gating regulator for any Brooker group activity touching telecom
infrastructure or services. Its 2017 underground-conduit notification (see related
concept) directly affects the feasibility of telecom-infrastructure investments under its
anti-duplication and shared-access policy.
