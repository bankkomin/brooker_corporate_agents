# Physical-AI Investment Thesis (SuperSeed Fund III)

## Summary

[[superseed-fund-iii|SuperSeed Fund III]] invests in companies building **AI for the
physical economy** — "physical AI, not digital theory." Each portfolio company applies AI
to a tangible industry (factories, warehouses, building sites, data centres, farms) and
delivers a measurable productivity, cost, or safety benefit to an operating business. The
thesis as presented to Brooker Group is **strategic, not passive**: a commitment that
brings frontier industrial technology directly into Thai operating companies, with Brooker
acting as the introduction layer to Thai customers.

## The Three Pillars

| Pillar | Claim |
|--------|-------|
| **Physical AI, not digital theory** | Every company applies AI to a tangible industry; each technology has been built and proven on the shop floor — not an abstract digital play. |
| **Quantifiable benefit** | Metric-anchored, customer-validated outcomes: energy savings up to 30%, manual effort cut 86–90%, cooling cost reductions on a data centre's largest energy line. |
| **Direct route to Thai businesses** | Each company is at commercial scale-up; Brooker provides the introduction layer to Thai manufacturers, freight forwarders, data-centre operators, and growers. |

## Sector Framework

The fund's eight companies map onto four sectors, each paired with a Thai market entry point:

| Sector | Companies | Thai entry point |
|--------|-----------|------------------|
| Buildings & Infrastructure | [[all3]] · [[hive-autonomy]] | Construction productivity / safety; logistics hubs & the Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC) |
| Manufacturing | [[thingtrax]] · [[ai-build]] | SME competitiveness vs Chinese cost pressure; moving up the value chain into advanced components |
| Energy & Logistics | [[octaipipe]] · [[freightsuite]] | New Thai data centres; regional logistics flows |
| Agriculture | [[messium]] · [[biographica]] | Rice / sugar / palm-oil yield; climate-adaptation for tropical crops |

## Why It Is Strategic for Brooker

Each company is at the point where **commercial scale-up is the priority**, so the
introduction layer Brooker provides is high-value to the portfolio company and to the Thai
customer. The strategic distinction from passive portfolio allocation is that the return is
**measurable benefit to Thai operating businesses, delivered through the Brooker
relationship** — not merely a financial holding.
