# SuperSeed Fund III — Strategic Technology Briefing for Brooker Group

**Counterparty / prepared by:** SuperSeed Ventures LLP
**Dated:** May 2026 (source file dated 2026-05-08)
**Classification:** Confidential — "not an offer of securities"
**Source:** `SuperSeed_Brooker_Portfolio_Brief.pdf` (3 pages)

> A briefing deck delivered by [[superseed-fund-iii|SuperSeed Ventures LLP]] to Brooker
> Group, pitching a **strategic, not passive** relationship around eight physical-AI
> portfolio companies with a direct route to Thai customers.

## Overview

[[superseed-fund-iii|SuperSeed Fund III]] invests in companies building **AI for the
physical economy**. The brief frames the opportunity for Brooker as bringing frontier
industrial technology directly into Thai operating companies, with Brooker acting as the
introduction layer to Thai manufacturers, freight forwarders, data-centre operators, and
growers. See [[physical-ai-investment-thesis]] for the full thesis.

## Sector Breakdown & Thai Entry Points

- **Buildings & Infrastructure** ([[all3]], [[hive-autonomy]]) — construction productivity
  and safety (Bangkok 2025 earthquake collapse, Rama 2 panel incidents); EEC logistics
  build-out.
- **Manufacturing** ([[thingtrax]], [[ai-build]]) — SME competitiveness vs Chinese cost
  pressure; moving up the value chain into advanced components / aerospace.
- **Energy & Logistics** ([[octaipipe]], [[freightsuite]]) — new Thai data centres;
  Thailand's position at the centre of regional logistics flows.
- **Agriculture** ([[messium]], [[biographica]]) — rice / sugar / palm-oil yield and
  fertiliser efficiency; climate-adaptation for tropical crops.

## Strategic Rationale ("Strategic, not passive")

The brief's closing argument: each company is commercially live and Brooker can introduce
it directly to specific Thai customer relationships. The strategic distinction from passive
portfolio allocation is **measurable benefit to Thai operating businesses, delivered
through the Brooker relationship**.
