# Red Flag Policy

## Summary

Securities whose unrealised loss exceeds **25%** of cost are classified as Red Flag and must have a documented reduction or exit plan. The Red Flag list is reviewed at every IC meeting under the Master Sheet section, and sub-thresholds are tracked at -50%, -75%, and -100%.

## Definition

A position is Red Flag when:

```
(MTM − Cost) / Cost  ≤  −25%
```

The deeper the drawdown, the higher the priority for the IC committee.

## Threshold Bands

| Band | Drawdown | Action |
|------|----------|--------|
| Watch | -25% to -50% | Monitor; flag in next meeting |
| Red Flag | -50% to -75% | Reduction plan required |
| Severe | -75% to -99% | Active workout; structured loan / restructuring considered |
| Total loss | -100% | Write-down; reclassify or recover |

## Why It Matters

Red Flag concentration drives the [[red-flag-portfolio-reduction]] running objective. Persistent Red Flag positions tie up capital, distort the [[investment-holding-limit]] calculation, and can force secondary liquidation under stress.

## Agent Notes

- The IC chair must list every Red Flag position with current drawdown % at the start of every meeting.
- Reduction plans for each Red Flag must be cross-referenced to the corresponding Brooker Portfolio narrative section.
- A Red Flag position that has been flagged for ≥3 consecutive meetings without a clear plan triggers a **High** escalation to CEO.
- Cross-check: any name that is both Red Flag AND >25% of investment ratio triggers **Critical** escalation.
