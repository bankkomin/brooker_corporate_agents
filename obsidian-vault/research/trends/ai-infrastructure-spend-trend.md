# AI Infrastructure Spend & Adoption (2023-2030 projection)

## Overview

A dated time series for AI infrastructure investment and paid-AI adoption, drawn from the
Messari State of AI report ([[source-state-of-ai-messari-2025]]) and corroborated by ARK
Big Ideas 2026 ([[source-ark-big-ideas-2026]]).

## Infrastructure Spend

| Metric | Value | As-of / forecast |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| Data-infrastructure spend forecast (Citigroup) | >$2.8 trillion through 2029 | Sep 2025 |
| Data-infrastructure projection (NVIDIA) | $3–4 trillion by 2030 | 2025 |
| OpenAI / NVIDIA / Oracle arrangement | $400 billion | Sep 2025 |
| Single training-run scale | 1–2 GW by 2028; >4 GW by 2030 | forecast |
| Data-center electricity use | ~415 TWh (~1.5% of global) in 2024; ~2% in 2025; potential doubling by 2030 | Oct 2025 |

## Paid AI Adoption

| Metric | Value | As-of |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Paid AI adoption rate | 5% | Jan 2023 |
| Paid AI adoption rate | 44% | Sep 2025 |
| 12-month retention | ~50% (2022) → 80% (2024) | 2024 |

## Context

ARK's Big Ideas 2026 frames this as part of an "unprecedented technology investment
cycle" — AI data centers, robotaxis, and AI software investment exceeding, as a share of
GDP, past waves such as railroads and electrification.
