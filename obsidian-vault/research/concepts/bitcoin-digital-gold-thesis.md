# Bitcoin as Digital Gold / Sound Money Thesis

## Summary

The single most recurring thesis across the external crypto research collection is that
Bitcoin is **sound money** — a scarce, apolitical store of value that is "better at being
gold than gold." The thesis was articulated most fully in institutional language during
2020–2021 (Stone Ridge, SkyBridge, Real Vision) as a response to unprecedented central
bank money printing, and it remains the analytical backbone of later signal/cycle reports
(Fidelity, Real Vision Pro Crypto).

## Core Argument

1. **Fixed supply.** Bitcoin's 21M cap and ~1%/year (declining) issuance make it the
   first store of value whose supply is *entirely unaffected by demand* — unlike gold,
   where a price spike pulls forward mining supply.
2. **The Difficulty Adjustment** keeps issuance on schedule regardless of mining power,
   making unexpected inflation impossible (Stone Ridge calls this Satoshi's most
   underappreciated breakthrough).
3. **Salability across time and space.** Bitcoin holds value across time (scarcity) and
   moves across borders faster than fiat with no credit risk — combining gold's monetary
   hardness with fiat's portability.
4. **Fiat debasement is the catalyst.** Near-zero rates and "money printer go brrr"
   place a premium on scarce assets; a 0% portfolio allocation is "the wrong number."
5. **Network-effect valuation.** Bitcoin should be valued like a network business
   (value grows with users), but cannot be antitrust-enforced or globally confiscated.

## Historical Context

The thesis matured during the COVID monetary expansion. It drove the first wave of
institutional treasury adoption (MicroStrategy/Saylor, Stone Ridge, Tesla) and the launch
of dedicated vehicles (SkyBridge Bitcoin Fund, NYDIG products). Goldman Sachs' 2021 report
debated — but did not endorse — Bitcoin's status versus gold as an inflation hedge.
