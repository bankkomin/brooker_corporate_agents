# DeFi Credit and On-Chain Lending

## Summary

DeFi credit is the thesis that lending and borrowing can be disintermediated onto
blockchains — spanning over-collateralized money markets (Aave, Compound, Venus),
uncollateralized real-world credit (Goldfinch), and new DAO financing structures
(convertible bonds, order-book lending markets). The promise is capital efficiency and
access for underbanked borrowers; the risk is that uncollateralized on-chain loans are
effectively high-yield bonds prone to default.

## Key Metrics

| Mechanism | Example | Detail |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| Uncollateralized RWA credit | Goldfinch | ~$100M served, ~98% utilization; microloan TAM ~$343B by 2027 |
| DAO convertible bonds | Porter Finance | Public-auction bonds; Ribbon raise $3M at ~7.1% YTM |
| Order-book lending | Atlendis | DAOs draw loans as needed at fixed rates, issued as NFTs |
| Over-collateralized money markets | Venus (BNB Chain) | Lending/borrowing primitive of a chain's DeFi stack |

## Historical Context

Delphi's 2022 reports ("Crypto Loans for Emerging Markets," "DAO Banking: Beyond
Tokens") argued tokens had been overused as a financing tool and that DAOs needed real
debt instruments. The BNB Chain DeFi study profiled lending as one of four core
verticals (alongside DEXes, asset management, liquid staking). Messari's 2024 theses
flagged "DeFi Lending" and RWA diversification as renewed growth areas after the
2022-23 credit crisis.
