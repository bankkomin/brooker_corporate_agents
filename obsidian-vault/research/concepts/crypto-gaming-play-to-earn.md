# Crypto Gaming and Play-to-Earn

## Summary

The crypto gaming thesis (Delphi Digital, 2022) acknowledges heavy gamer backlash
against crypto and concedes much of the critique is valid — early play-to-earn (P2E)
models over-financialized games and under-delivered on fun. The evolved thesis argues
blockchain's real contribution is true digital ownership of in-game assets and new
monetization models built *around* games rather than extractive token loops. Axie
Infinity (highlighted by CoinGecko in Q2 2021) was the breakout that vaulted crypto
gaming into mainstream consciousness and pioneered the P2E economy.

## Key Metrics

| Idea | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Play-to-earn | Players earn tradable tokens/NFTs; Axie Infinity the defining example |
| Core critique | Early P2E sacrificed game quality for token economics — "thesis drift" |
| Evolved model | Crypto monetizes *around* games (ownership, secondary markets, community) |
| Game studios + metaverse | Studios best-positioned for the metaverse — they understand storytelling |

## Historical Context

P2E surged in 2021 with Axie Infinity, then cooled in the 2022 bear market. The thesis
overlaps with the metaverse and NFT-culture narratives — gaming was widely seen as the
leading on-ramp to a consumer metaverse.
