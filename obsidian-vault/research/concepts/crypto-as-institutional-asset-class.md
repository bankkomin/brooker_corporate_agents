# Crypto as an Institutional Asset Class

## Summary

A recurring thesis across research from Grayscale (2019), Coinbase, Goldman Sachs, the
CFA Institute and Glassnode/CME is that digital assets — Bitcoin first, then Ether —
have crossed the threshold from a retail curiosity into a legitimate institutional
asset class. The argument rests on three pillars: (1) digital assets offer a return
stream with low correlation to traditional assets, a genuine diversification "free
lunch"; (2) a critical mass of credible institutions (MicroStrategy, Tudor, Druckenmiller,
BlackRock, Fidelity, CME) have allocated, validating the class; and (3) the maturing of
institutional-grade infrastructure — qualified custody, prime brokerage, regulated
futures, and ultimately spot ETFs — has removed the operational barriers.

## Historical Context

The thesis evolved in waves. Grayscale (2019) made the Modern Portfolio Theory case
before institutions arrived. Coinbase's 2020 review documented the actual arrival of
corporates and macro funds. Goldman's 2021 report captured the still-contested debate
(Roubini: "no income or utility"). By 2024 the debate was effectively settled by the
spot ETF complex and record inflows, as documented in the Glassnode/CME *Digital Asset
Insights*.
