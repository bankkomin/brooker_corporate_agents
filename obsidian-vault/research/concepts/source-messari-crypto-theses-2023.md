# Source Summary — Messari Crypto Theses for 2023

**As of: 3 January 2023** · Author: Ryan Selkis, Messari · ~200-hour annual report

## Document Overview

The sixth edition, written after an ~80% portfolio drawdown, the Terra collapse and the
FTX fraud. Shorter and more focused than 2022; concentrates on "themes of greatest — and
at times existential — importance."

## Key Findings

- Tone shift: "Winter is Here: It's Time to Build." Crypto is "(still) inevitable" but
  the report is written "in the pioneers' defense" against the frauds and tourists.
- "Surviving Winter, Redux" and "How Low Can We Go" — bear-market positioning.
- "Bear Market Building" — infrastructure (code security, decentralised hardware,
  custody, governance, scalability) is "in vogue."
- A full **CeFi** section: "Anatomy of a Crypto Credit Crisis," DCG & Genesis contagion
  risk, "Grayscale: Reflexivity up. Reflexivity down."
- "The Institutions are Coming" — but now expecting business models and board oversight.
- Privacy tech to be either promoted or "de facto banned" on national-security grounds.
- Predicts less NFT speculation, "fewer moon fumes," real DC policy progress.

## Relevance

The definitive post-mortem-era outlook; documents the 2022 credit crisis cascade and the
pivot from speculation to building. Cross-links to
[[algorithmic-stablecoin-death-spiral]], [[crypto-cycles-and-liquidity]],
[[institutional-crypto-adoption]].
