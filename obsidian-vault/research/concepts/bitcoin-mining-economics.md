# Bitcoin Mining Economics and Public Miner Solvency

## Summary

A concept linking two strands of the research collection: the *security model* of
Bitcoin mining (Difficulty Adjustment, location-independent energy use) and the *financial
fragility* of publicly-traded miners exposed during the 2022 bear market.

## Mining Economics

- Bitcoin's **Difficulty Adjustment** retargets mining difficulty to keep block time at
  ~10 minutes regardless of total hashrate — making issuance inflation-proof and the
  network secure even at low hashrate (Stone Ridge: Satoshi's key original breakthrough).
- Mining is "the only profitable use of energy that does not need to be near human
  settlement" — enabling monetisation of stranded/remote energy (hydro).
- Miner profitability is read via the **Puell Multiple** (daily issuance value ÷ 365-day
  average); cycle tops historically coincide with high readings.

## Public Miner Solvency Framework (Blockware, Feb 2023)

The 2022 downturn — leverage, higher rates, rising difficulty, energy costs, falling BTC —
caused widespread distress. Blockware's "Miner Score" ranks public miners on four ratios:

| Metric | Formula | Benchmark |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Breakeven BTC price | Cost of Mining Revenue ÷ BTC mined | Below spot = profitable |
| Current Ratio | Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities | ≥ 2.0 |
| Debt-to-Equity | Total Liabilities ÷ Shareholders' Equity | 0.5–1.5 ideal |
| Cash Ratio | Cash & Equivalents ÷ Current Liabilities | ~1.0 |

Core Scientific (CORZ) scored lowest (5.6) and filed Chapter 11; WULF, GREE, SDIG
restructured. By Q3 2022, after total OpEx, *no* public miner had a breakeven below BTC's
$19,426 close. CleanSpark (CLSK) was the most resilient.
