# The Metaverse Investment Thesis

## Summary

The metaverse thesis — at its 2021–2022 peak hype — framed immersive, persistent virtual
worlds as a >$1 trillion annual-revenue opportunity. JPMorgan's Onyx report and Real
Vision interviews distinguished a **Web2 metaverse** (Roblox, Fortnite, Second Life:
closed, platform-owned) from a **Web3 metaverse** (Decentraland, The Sandbox:
token-governed, user-owned, interoperable assets).

## Core Argument

- The metaverse is "a seamless convergence of physical and digital lives" — not one world
  but many. The defining Web3 property is **interoperability and true ownership** of
  avatars, items and currency, not just immersive graphics (per Sandbox's Sebastien
  Borget: "the essence can't be captured in a rebranding campaign").
- Spending on virtual goods (~$54B/year) already exceeds music spend; metaverse land
  secondary sales reached ~$2B by mid-2022.
- Brands (Nike, Gap, Adidas, Walmart, Warner Music) entered to reach digitally-native
  consumers; The Sandbox alone reported ~200 brand partnerships.
- Web3 gaming is the likely on-ramp: tokens/NFTs as in-game assets, DAO governance over
  game rules.

## Critique & Limits

The thesis was the most hype-exposed in the collection. NFT and gaming wallet activity
fell sharply through 2022 (BNB Chain GameFi UAWs down 41% QoQ in Q1 2023). a16z and
JPMorgan both stressed it remains "very early days." The term itself became diluted by
Meta's rebrand.
