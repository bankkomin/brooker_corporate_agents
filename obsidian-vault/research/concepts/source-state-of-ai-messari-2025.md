# Source Summary — Messari State of AI (December 2025)

**As of: 2 December 2025** (Messari Enterprise Report)

## Document Overview

Messari's enterprise research report on the state of artificial intelligence and its
intersection with crypto, authored by Chris Davis, Dylan Bane, Sam Ruskin and Mohamed
Allam. It is a primer on decentralized AI ("deAI") positioned within seven broader AI
industry trends.

## Key Findings

- **AI is everywhere.** AI is embedding into every major sector: 63% of K-12 teachers and
  49% of higher-ed instructors use GenAI tools; 66% of physicians used AI tools in 2024
  (up from 38% in 2023); 17% of finance teams already deploy generative agents, with 75%
  expecting agentic AI to be routine by 2028; the US Army signed a 10-year, up-to-$10B
  Palantir contract.
- **Sovereign AI race.** The US and China lead. The US "Winning the Race" AI Action Plan
  (23 July 2025) outlines 90+ federal actions; China issued its Global AI Governance
  Action Plan three days later.
- **Infrastructure spend surge.** Citigroup raised its data-infrastructure spend forecast
  to surpass $2.8T through 2029; NVIDIA projects $3–4T by 2030. Single training runs are
  projected at 1–2 GW by 2028, exceeding 4 GW by 2030. The OpenAI–NVIDIA–Oracle $400B
  arrangement has been characterized as "circular financing".
- **Adoption & revenue.** Paid AI adoption rose from 5% (Jan 2023) to 44% (Sep 2025);
  12-month retention climbed to 80% in 2024.
- **The deAI thesis.** Crypto is positioned to coordinate resources, verify systems,
  provide machine-native financial infrastructure, and create immutable identity/reputation
  records for AI agents. Seven trends drive a deAI revival: surging inference demand,
  frontier data needs, agentic economies, talent wars, and decentralized training.

## Relevance

Bridges the AI and crypto research areas. The deAI ecosystem map and "agents transacting
in stablecoins" thesis connect to the [[ark-disruptive-innovation-thesis]] convergence
