# Institutional Crypto Adoption

## Summary

A through-line of the research collection is the gradual move of digital assets from
retail speculation to institutional asset class. Reports from issuers (Grayscale,
SkyBridge), exchanges (Coinbase Institutional), treasury managers (Stone Ridge) and
educators (CFA Institute) chart the same arc: products, custody and regulation maturing
to let allocators participate.

## Adoption Markers

- **2019** — Grayscale frames a small Bitcoin allocation as a modern-portfolio
  diversifier; institutional products still nascent.
- **2020** — Stone Ridge holds BTC as treasury reserve since 2017; NYDIG affiliate
  custodies >$350M of insurer Bitcoin. SkyBridge launches a dedicated Bitcoin fund with a
  $272M position.
- **2021** — Coinbase Institutional's H1 review documents an inflection in institutional
  flows, custody demand and corporate treasury adoption. CFA Institute publishes a
  cryptoassets primer — a signal of mainstream professional acceptance.
- **2022–23** — the credit crisis and FTX fraud damage trust; Messari 2023 notes "the
  institutions are coming" but with demands for business models and board oversight.
- **2024–25** — spot Bitcoin ETFs and continued accumulation (per RV Pro Crypto).

## Why It Matters

The thesis ("demand > supply for crypto custody / for countries," Messari 2021) is the
demand-side counterpart to the [[bitcoin-digital-gold-thesis]] supply argument and the
basis for [[crypto-portfolio-allocation]] frameworks.
