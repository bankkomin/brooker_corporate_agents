# Source Summary — Coinbase Risk Factors (S-1 Prospectus, 2021)

**As of: 2021** (extract of the Risk Factors section of Coinbase's prospectus)

## Document Overview

An extract of the **Risk Factors** section from Coinbase Global, Inc.'s registration
prospectus for its Class A common stock. It catalogues the material risks of operating a
crypto-exchange business — a structured, issuer-disclosed taxonomy of crypto business
risk. See the recurring concept [[crypto-business-risk-factors]].

## Key Findings

- **Revenue volatility.** All of Coinbase's revenue sources depend on crypto assets and
  the broader cryptoeconomy; operating results fluctuate significantly quarter to quarter
  with market sentiment. Three-month crypto-asset volatility rose 73% from Q4 2019 to Q1
  2020, then fell 36% into Q2 2020.
- **Transaction-fee dependence.** Coinbase generates substantially all revenue from
  transaction fees (flat fee or % of value, plus a retail spread); declines in volume,
  price, or liquidity directly reduce revenue.
- **BTC/ETH concentration.** For FY2020, Bitcoin and Ethereum trading pairs drove over 56%
  of total trading volume; a deterioration in those markets would adversely affect the
  business. Specific risks include block-reward halvings, Ethereum's proof-of-stake
  migration, forks, and 51% attacks.
- **Breadth of factors** affecting crypto price/volume — regulatory and legislative
  change, liquidity and market-making, manipulative activity on unregulated platforms,
  miner/validator economics, security/hacks, banking access, interest rates, and
  macro/political conditions.

## Relevance

A clear-eyed, issuer-grade enumeration of the risks inherent in any crypto-trading or
crypto-exchange exposure — useful as a checklist when assessing counterparties (e.g.
exchanges used by a digital-asset treasury) and crypto-business investments.
