# Crypto Business & Counterparty Risk Factors

## Summary

A recurring theme across the Risk and Pitchbook research: a structured taxonomy of the
risks attached to crypto businesses, tokens, and counterparties. Two complementary
sources frame it — Coinbase's issuer-disclosed S-1 risk factors
([[source-coinbase-risk-factors]]) and the in-house VC investment-proposal "Red Flags"
list ([[source-vc-due-diligence-checklist]]).

## Risk Taxonomy

1. **Market / revenue volatility** — crypto-business revenues track asset prices and
   trading volume; results swing sharply with market sentiment.
2. **Concentration** — heavy dependence on Bitcoin and Ethereum (Coinbase: >56% of FY2020
   volume) leaves a business exposed to those two networks' health.
3. **Regulatory** — legislative/regulatory change, enforcement actions, and inconsistent
   cross-jurisdiction characterization of tokens.
4. **Security & operations** — hacks, outages, custody failure, key management.
5. **Tokenomics red flags** — FDV far above circulating market cap, tiny initial float
   with large future unlocks, vesting cliffs, runaway inflation (especially P2E tokens).
6. **Team / governance red flags** — founders with no skin in the game, side jobs,
   absent public profiles, opaque use of prior funds, "no competition / risk-free" claims.

## Why It Matters

Provides a checklist for assessing both crypto-business investments and crypto
counterparties used by a digital-asset treasury (exchanges, custodians). The
issuer-grade Coinbase taxonomy and the deal-screening Red Flags list together inform
investment due diligence and counterparty selection.
