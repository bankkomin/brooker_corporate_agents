# Source Summary — MicroStrategy Bitcoin Initiative Project Roadmap & Key Considerations (2020)

**As of: 2020 (document set dated 4 February 2021)**

## Document Overview

Two planning documents from MicroStrategy's corporate-Bitcoin program: a **Project
Roadmap** (a phased action-item checklist with owners) and a **Key Considerations**
memo (priority decision points for corporate BTC investment). Both are explicitly offered
as resources to help other corporates navigate a Bitcoin strategy; together they form the
front end of the [[corporate-bitcoin-treasury-playbook]].

## Key Findings

**Project Roadmap** — four phases:
- **Planning: Strategy & Team** — define the purpose of BTC investment, assemble a
  champion plus finance, legal, and IT working teams, and engage external legal/financial
  advisors with BTC experience.
- **Planning: Corporate Governance** — research, summarize prior Board authorizations,
  prepare regulatory chart (including **Investment Company Act analysis**), analyze
  director/officer conflict-of-interest issues.
- **Planning: Finance & IT** — accounting/tax/controls analysis, choice of investment
  vehicle entity, source-of-funds and on/off-shore decisions, IT/cyber risk, insurance,
  IR strategy, and direct-vs-fund investment choice.
- **Execution / Post-execution** — prepare Treasury Reserve Policy and BTC Trading Policy,
  negotiate brokerage/custodial agreements, accounting memo, SEC disclosures, Board /
  Investments Committee / Audit Committee approvals, announcement, and subsequent
  purchases.

**Key Considerations memo** — four areas:
- **Business Strategy & Governance** — rationale, risk-reward change vs cash, narrative
  impact on stakeholders, stock-price volatility, accounting/earnings impact.
- **Risk Management** — BTC investment management policy, authorized persons, vendor
  standards, Board/management approval thresholds, hedging, insurance, UCC treatment.
- **Disclosure Matters** — financial-statement impacts, critical accounting policies,
  MD&A, market-risk and risk-factor disclosures.
- **Execution** — custody/storage (engage a US "qualified custodian", multiple accounts,
  hot vs cold storage), broker partnership, fee negotiation, contractual protections.

## Relevance

The procedural playbook for standing up a corporate digital-asset treasury — a direct
reference for how Brooker would govern, document, and approve treasury digital-asset
activity (Engine 3 of the [[three-engine-model]]).
