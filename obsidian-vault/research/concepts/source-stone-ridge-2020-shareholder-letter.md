# Source Summary — Stone Ridge 2020 Shareholder Letter

**As of: December 2020** · Author: Ross L. Stevens, Founder & CEO, Stone Ridge

## Document Overview

The shareholder letter from Stone Ridge's 2020 mutual-fund annual report — a widely-cited
essay-style articulation of the sound-money case for Bitcoin, written by the founder of
NYDIG's parent.

## Key Findings

- **"What is money?"** Money is technology for moving wealth to the future; monies are
  plural and exist on a continuum of soundness subject to network effects.
- **Critique of central banking.** Unbacked "helicopter money" distorts price signals,
  misallocates capital, and creates booms/busts; the Fed is "the cause of severe economic
  downdrafts, not the cure."
- **Big Four Bitcoin aha-moments**: (1) salability across time — supply unaffected by
  demand; (2) salability across space — faster, no credit risk; (3) the Difficulty
  Adjustment — Satoshi's key original breakthrough making inflation impossible;
  (4) Bitcoin's energy use — the first location-independent profitable use of energy,
  enabling stranded-energy monetisation ("move the people to the power").
- **"Missing AMZN millionaires"**: investors repeatedly think they "missed it"; Bitcoin
  at 12 years old is compared to Amazon at year 12 (which then rose 62x).
- **The 10/10 portfolio**: risk management = diversification + humility; Bitcoin is one
  of ten ~10%-weight uncorrelated, anti-fad allocations.
