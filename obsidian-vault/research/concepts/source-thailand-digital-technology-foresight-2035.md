# Source Summary — Thailand Digital Technology Foresight 2035

**As of: report content circa 2019 (V12, file modified 13 June 2022)**

## Document Overview

A major **technology foresight study**, "Thailand Digital Technology Foresight 2035",
analyzing the megatrends and technologies that will shape Thailand's digital economy over
a 15–20 year horizon. The report progresses from 11 megatrends, through a long list of
13 technologies, to 7 shortlisted technologies, and an economic-impact assessment.

## Key Findings

- **11 megatrends.** Explosive data generation; the Fourth Industrial Revolution; growth
  of smart ecosystems; urbanization / megacities; rise of e-commerce; increasing
  connectedness / decreasing privacy; transformation of the workplace; shift in global
  economic power; aging society; scarcity of natural resources; climate change.
- **Data explosion.** Global data projected to grow from 33 zettabytes (2018) to 175
  zettabytes by 2025 (61% CAGR); the average connected person to interact with devices
  ~4,785 times/day in 2025.
- **7 shortlisted technologies** for Thailand: Internet of Things, Artificial
  Intelligence, Data Analytics, Next-Generation Telecom, Distributed Ledger Technology,
  Quantum Computing, and Automation.
- **Automation as a growth driver.** Thailand's robotics & automation market estimated at
  THB 200B in 2020, projected to THB 438B by 2025 (13.98% CAGR), THB 739B by 2030, and
  over THB 1 trillion by 2035 — see [[thailand-automation-market-trend]].
- **Adoption realism.** RPA adoption is early-stage; <35% of Thai companies expected to
  adopt RPA within 5 years; banking/finance and retail lead. RPA is expected to evolve
  into cognitive automation / Smart Process Automation by integrating AI/ML.

## Relevance

The macro-tech backdrop for Thailand — Brooker's home market. It frames which technologies
(AI, DLT, automation, IoT) will drive the Thai digital economy, informing both the
advisory pipeline (Engine 2 of the [[three-engine-model]]) and the country-level context
for Brooker's strategy. Complements the global [[ark-disruptive-innovation-thesis]].
