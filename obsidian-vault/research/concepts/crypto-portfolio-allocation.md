# Crypto Portfolio Allocation Frameworks

## Summary

Several reports translate the Bitcoin thesis into concrete portfolio-construction guidance
for allocators: how to size a position, how to model its risk, and why a 0% allocation is
itself an active (and, per the authors, wrong) decision.

## Frameworks in the Collection

- **Real Vision / Eight Isle white paper (Jan 2021)** — argues Bitcoin can be treated
  like any other asset within industry-standard risk models (Qontigo/Barra). Despite
  high standalone volatility, low correlation makes even a small allocation accretive to
  a globally diversified portfolio's risk-adjusted return.
- **Grayscale "The Modern Portfolio" (2019)** — positions a modest Bitcoin sleeve as a
  diversifier that improved historical Sharpe ratios (Grayscale Insights, Sep 2019).
- **Stone Ridge 10/10 portfolio** — Bitcoin as one of ten ~10%-weight, uncorrelated,
  anti-fad, persistent-risk-premium allocations; risk management = diversification +
  humility.

## Key Principles

- Size to volatility, not to conviction — small weights can be meaningful given
  asymmetric upside.
- Judge by *point-to-point* depreciation of fiat vs Bitcoin, not by interim volatility.
- Treat performance/risk on its own merits rather than via narrative.
