# Source Summary — Investment Proposal Question List & Red Flags (2022)

**As of: 26 April 2022**

## Document Overview

An internal **due-diligence checklist** for evaluating crypto / token investment
proposals — a structured question list across ten categories plus a "Red Flags" section.
A reusable diligence template.

## Key Findings

**Question categories (10):** Summary/Project Description, Investment Thesis, Investment
Proposal, Token Economics, Product Mechanism, Project Roadmap, Financials, Core Team,
Performance and Record, Competitor Landscape.

**Red flags enumerated:**
- **Tokenomics** — FDV (price × max supply) far above current market cap with inflated
  tokenomics signals coming sell-side pressure; tiny initial float with large future
  unlocks; very low initial market cap at TGE (cites DragonLand, Tryhards as cautionary
  P2E examples).
- **Funding** — raising too little (insufficient to build) or too much (unclear use of
  funds); early investors not joining a follow-on round; poor use of prior funds.
- **Team** — founders not investing their own money; too many founders / excessive team
  allocation; founders with other jobs; absent LinkedIn/Twitter profiles; comment-disabled
  social accounts.
- **General** — websites/whitepapers with many grammatical errors; "no competition /
  risk-free" claims; high overhead / thin margins; little data disclosed; "too good to be
  true"; pressure to act immediately; projects that skip due diligence.

## Relevance

A practical, reusable diligence framework for the Brooker investment process — directly
applicable to VCC fund-of-funds deal screening and any direct token/equity investment.
Pairs with the issuer-grade [[crypto-business-risk-factors]] taxonomy and the worked
[[source-bitdao-investment-proposal]] (whose optimistic valuation reasoning the red-flags
discipline is designed to check).
