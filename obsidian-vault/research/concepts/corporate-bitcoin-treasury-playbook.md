# Corporate Bitcoin Treasury Playbook

## Summary

A recurring theme across the Compliance research collection: a repeatable **playbook for a
publicly listed company to add Bitcoin (and other digital assets) to its treasury** while
managing legal, accounting, governance, and disclosure risk. The playbook is defined by
MicroStrategy's 2020 program documents and corroborated by Meitu's parallel Hong
Kong-listed crypto treasury program ([[source-meitu-crypto-purchases]]).

## The Playbook — Five Building Blocks

1. **Strategic rationale & governance** — define the purpose (treasury reserve asset,
   hedge against fiat debasement, technology-narrative signal), assemble a cross-functional
   team, and route decisions through the Board, Investments Committee, and Audit Committee.
   See [[source-microstrategy-bitcoin-roadmap]].
2. **Treasury Reserve Policy** — designate the digital asset as a treasury reserve asset,
   set Working Capital and Cash Reserve thresholds, require pre-approved transactions, and
   use only Permitted Custodians/Brokers/OTC counterparties. See
   [[source-microstrategy-treasury-reserve-policy]].
3. **Trading Policy** — an insider-trading-style control: Non-Trading Periods, no personal
   trading on material non-public information, no tipping, no market manipulation.
4. **Accounting & tax** — under US GAAP the asset is an indefinite-lived intangible (ASC
   350) with **non-reversible impairment**; sales recognized under ASC 610. See
   [[source-microstrategy-bitcoin-accounting]].
5. **Disclosure & custody** — SEC/exchange disclosures (HKEX Listing Rules for Meitu),
   "qualified custodian" engagement, segregated custody, and insurance.

## Why It Matters

This is the closest external precedent for how Brooker would govern its own digital-asset
treasury (Engine 3 of the [[three-engine-model]]). The playbook supplies ready templates
for treasury policy, trading policy, custody due diligence, and the accounting/disclosure
treatment a listed-company treasury function must adopt.
