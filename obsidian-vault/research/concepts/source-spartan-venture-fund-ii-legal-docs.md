# Source Summary — Spartan Venture Fund II LP Offering Documents (2022)

**As of: 17 February 2022** (PPM dated; final document set)

## Document Overview

The complete set of offering / governing documents for **Spartan Venture Fund II LP**, a
Cayman Islands exempted limited partnership: the **Private Placement Memorandum (PPM)**,
the **Amended and Restated Limited Partnership Agreement (ARLPA)**, and the **Subscription
Agreement**. This is the legal counterpart to the Gaming & Metaverse Fund pitchbook (see
[[source-spartan-fund-pitchbooks]]); see the fund entity [[spartan-venture-fund-ii]].

## Key Findings

- **Structure.** Cayman Islands exempted limited partnership; General Partner is Spartan
  Capital GP (Ugland House, Grand Cayman). No registration statement filed; offered by
  private placement to qualifying investors only; no intended stock-exchange listing.
- **Investment objective.** To seek capital appreciation by investing in the
  gaming/metaverse/blockchain venture theme (consistent with the pitchbook).
- **Principal terms** (from the PPM "Summary of Principal Terms"):
  - Minimum Commitment: **US$200,000**.
  - Term of the Fund: **six years** from the First Closing (plus extension provisions).
  - Management Fee: **2.0% per annum** of commitments during the Investment Period,
    stepping down thereafter; payable quarterly.
  - **Carried Interest** to the GP; **Transaction Fees** are offset against the
    Management Fee.
- **Document hierarchy.** The three documents together constitute the "Offering
  Documents"; where the PPM and the Partnership Agreement conflict, the Partnership
  Agreement prevails.
- **Risk disclosure.** Extensive "Certain Risk Factors and Potential Conflicts of
  Interest" — crypto-price volatility, dependence on the GP, illiquidity, no secondary
  market, and tax treatment of carried interest.

## Relevance

A full reference set for the legal architecture of a Cayman crypto/venture fund — the
PPM/LPA/Subscription triad, fee economics, and risk-disclosure conventions Brooker's own
VCC fund platform must replicate. See [[crypto-fund-structuring]].
