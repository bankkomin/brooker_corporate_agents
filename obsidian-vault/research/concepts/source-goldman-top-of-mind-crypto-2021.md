# Source Summary — Goldman Sachs: Crypto, A New Asset Class? (Top of Mind)

_As of 21 May 2021. Publisher: [[goldman-sachs]] Global Macro Research, Top of Mind Issue 98._

## Document Overview

A Goldman Sachs *Top of Mind* report debating whether cryptocurrencies can and should be
treated as an institutional asset class, built from interviews with proponents and
sceptics.

## Key Findings

- **Pro**: Michael Novogratz (Galaxy) — a critical mass of credible investors has
  "cemented" crypto as an asset class. Michael Sonnenshein (Grayscale) — the 2020
  rebound proved resilience.
- **Against**: Nouriel Roubini (NYU) — cryptos have "no income or utility" and no link
  to economic fundamentals.
- Goldman's Mathew McDermott: clients increasingly say "yes"; GS is launching crypto
  products.
- GS analysts contribute pieces on Bitcoin as a macro asset, digital stores of value,
  and crypto's role in balanced portfolios.

## Relevance

A snapshot of the still-contested 2021 TradFi debate — the
[[crypto-as-institutional-asset-class]] thesis at its inflection point.
