# Fund-Raising Advisory Economics & Instruments

## Summary

A theme drawn from the Contracts research collection: the **economics and instruments of
fund-raising advisory** — how Brooker is paid for advisory work, and the early-stage
instruments through which capital is raised.

## Brooker's Advisory Economics

From the standard advisory-mandate template ([[source-fund-raising-advisory-mandate]]):

| Engagement valuation | Success fee |
|----------------------|-------------|
| Under US$35M equity valuation | 5.00% of total equity / token amount |
| Over US$100M equity valuation | 3.00% of total equity / token amount |

Fees are **success-based** (payable on signing of the shareholder/partnership agreement),
exclude out-of-pocket and third-party fees, attract VAT, and the mandate is **exclusive**
for its 24-month term.

## Early-Stage Investment Instruments

The collection holds a worked **SAFE** (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) template
([[source-safe-agreement-template]]) — a standard early-stage instrument giving an
investor the right to future shares at a valuation cap, converting on an Equity Financing,
Liquidity Event, or Dissolution Event.

## Why It Matters

These documents define the contractual mechanics of Brooker's Advisory engine (Engine 2 of
the [[three-engine-model]]): the success-fee schedule sets the advisory revenue model, and
the SAFE template is a reference instrument for venture-round structuring.
