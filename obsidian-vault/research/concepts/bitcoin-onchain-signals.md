# Bitcoin On-Chain Signal Metrics

## Summary

Because public blockchains expose all transaction data, crypto generates an entirely new
class of valuation/sentiment signals unavailable in traditional markets. The collection
includes both a practitioner toolkit (Fidelity Signals Report) and a methodological
proposal to reframe on-chain accounting (ARK x Glassnode "Cointime Economics").

## Core Metrics

| Metric | What it measures |
|--------|------------------|
| Realized Price / Realized Cap | Average cost basis of all holders |
| MVRV Z-Score | Market value vs realized value — over/undervaluation |
| NUPL | Net Unrealized Profit/Loss — market sentiment zone |
| Puell Multiple | Miner revenue vs its 365-day average — cycle position |
| Reserve Risk | Long-term-holder confidence vs price |
| Hodler Net Position Change | Long-term holders accumulating vs distributing |
| Liquid vs Illiquid Supply | Share of supply held by long-term holders |
| Stock-to-Flow | Scarcity-based price model (flagged as weakening relevance) |

## Cointime Economics (ARK x Glassnode)

A proposed accounting framework that weights coins by **coinblocks** — the product of
coins held and blocks held — distinguishing actively-traded supply from dormant supply.
It introduces "Liveliness," "Vaultedness," and a refined cost-basis ("true market mean")
intended to be a more rigorous successor to realized-cap metrics.

## Relevance

These signals operationalise the [[crypto-cycles-and-liquidity]] thesis — letting
analysts read cycle position from data rather than narrative.
