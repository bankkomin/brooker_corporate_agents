# Source Summary — Messari Crypto Theses for 2022

**As of: December 2021** · Author: Ryan Selkis, Messari · ~250-hour annual report

## Document Overview

The fifth edition, written at the market top (rates near zero, crypto and S&P at all-time
highs). Covers top 10 narratives/themes, 10 people to watch, American crypto policy (a
notably large section), market infrastructure, DeFi, ETH & friends, NFTs and DAOs.

## Key Findings

- **#1 narrative: "The Collapse in Institutional Trust"** — a prescient framing given
  what followed in 2022.
- "Crypto/Web3 is inevitable"; "Bridges and Nifties and DAOs"; "Decoupling of Cryptos."
- "Permanent (Venture) Capital: In, Up and Down, Never Out" — the VC overhang theme.
- "Surviving Winter" and "How High Can We Fly" — already flagging downside.
- "Public Options: Coinbase Opens the Floodgates" — IPOs as an adoption channel.
- Extensive US policy chapters: FSOC/SEC dominance, Ripple vs SEC, crypto eurodollars and
  systemic risk, the case for DAO incorporation, "The American Web3 Council."
- Heavy NFT and DAO coverage, borrowing from Punk6529 and others.

## Relevance

Captures peak-cycle optimism alongside early-warning signals (institutional trust, VC
overhang, "surviving winter"). The bridge between the euphoric 2021 edition and the
sober 2023 edition. Cross-links to [[web3-ownership-thesis]], [[nft-creator-economy]],
[[dao-governance-and-treasuries]].
