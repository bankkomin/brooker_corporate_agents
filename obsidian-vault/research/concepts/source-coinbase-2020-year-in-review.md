# Source Summary — Coinbase 2020 Year in Review

_As of January 2021 (covering FY2020). Publisher: [[coinbase]] Institutional._

## Document Overview

Coinbase Institutional's annual review, subtitled "Crypto is an institutional asset
class" — a tour of the 2020 market from the perspective of its institutional practice.

## Key Findings

- Crypto asset class hit an all-time high of $781B aggregate market cap on 31 Dec 2020
  (+309% in 2020); Coinbase company-wide Assets on Platform ended >$90B.
- Standout new institutional buyers: MicroStrategy, One River, Tudor Investment, Square,
  MassMutual, Stanley Druckenmiller.
- Major banks (JP Morgan, Citi, Guggenheim) issued six-figure Bitcoin price targets;
  PayPal launched crypto retail brokerage.
- Covers Bitcoin, Ethereum, Coinbase Prime Broker, "onramps," derivatives, stablecoins
  (cryptodollars), DeFi, and Coinbase Ventures.

## Relevance

Documents the actual arrival of institutions in 2020 — the empirical core of the
[[crypto-as-institutional-asset-class]] thesis.
