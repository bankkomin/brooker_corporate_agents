# NFTs as a Culture-Driven Asset Class

## Summary

This thesis — articulated most fully by Animoca's Yat Siu and amplified by Real Vision's
Raoul Pal — holds that NFTs are not "JPEGs" but tokenized claims on culture, community,
and IP. Value accrues from network effects, story, craftsmanship and belonging rather
than the image itself (the "Birkin bag" analogy). Ownership creates care and defence of
an asset; renting does not. Generative art (Art Blocks) is positioned as a candidate
defining art movement of the 21st century, and community-owned IP (Jenkins the Valet /
The Writer's Room) as a new media model.

## Key Metrics

| Idea | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Culture as asset class | NFTs tokenize history, story, identity and community membership |
| Network-effect valuation | Virtual land value derives from proximity and interaction at scale |
| Ownership vs. renting | "If you own, you care" — ownership drives engagement and defence |
| Generative art | Art Blocks the leading curation platform; Chromie Squiggles at ATH floors (2022) |
| Community-owned IP | Bored Ape "Jenkins the Valet" → The Writer's Room media company |

## Historical Context

The thesis peaked with the 2021 NFT bull market (Axie Infinity, BAYC, profile-picture
collections) and persisted into the 2022 bear market, when generative art and NFT
funds/DAOs were framed as a flight to quality. It is closely tied to the metaverse and
play-to-earn narratives of the same period.
