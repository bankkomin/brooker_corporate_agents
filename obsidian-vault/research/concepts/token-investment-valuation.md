# Token Investment Valuation & Deal Screening

## Summary

A recurring theme across the Pitchbook research: how a crypto **token investment is sized,
valued, and screened**. Two sources frame it — the worked [[source-bitdao-investment-proposal]]
(an actual buy recommendation) and the [[source-vc-due-diligence-checklist]] (the
structured question list and red-flags discipline).

## The Valuation Approach (as observed)

- **Cashflow / earnings multiple.** Where a token captures protocol or exchange fee
  income, it is valued on a P/E-style multiple (BitDAO: 8x expected cashflow, P/E ~8.1).
- **Comparable FDV.** Fully diluted valuation is benchmarked against listed comparables
  (BitDAO benchmarked to FTX Token, Binance, Coinbase FDVs).
- **Return framing.** A posited multiple-on-listing is derived from the gap between entry
  valuation and comparable FDV (BitDAO: posited 2–3x within a year).

## The Screening Discipline

The diligence checklist tests ten dimensions (thesis, tokenomics, product mechanism,
roadmap, financials, team, track record, competitors). The **red-flags** discipline is the
counterweight to optimistic valuation reasoning:

- FDV far above circulating market cap → coming sell-side pressure.
- Tiny initial float / large future unlocks → runaway supply inflation.
- Founders with no skin in the game; opaque use of prior funds; "risk-free" claims.

## Why It Matters

A reusable framework for the Brooker investment process — both the valuation method and
the screening discipline apply to VCC fund-of-funds deal flow and any direct token/equity
investment. The BitDAO proposal also serves as a cautionary record: its valuation logic is
highly optimistic and pre-dates the 2022 crypto downturn.
