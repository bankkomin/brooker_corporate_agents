# Source Summary — a16z State of Crypto 2022

**As of: May 2022** (data as of 5/12/2022) · Andreessen Horowitz crypto · overview deck

## Document Overview

a16z crypto's inaugural annual overview report — a data-rich slide deck arguing the Web3
thesis. Sections: What is Web3 / Why Web3 Matters, Cycles of Innovation, Layer 1
Blockchains, Layer 2 Scaling, DeFi, Stablecoins, NFTs, Web3 Gaming, DAOs, Closing
Thoughts.

## Key Findings

- **Three eras of the internet**: Web1 (1990–2005, open protocols), Web2 (2005–2020,
  siloed platforms), Web3 (decentralised + modern functionality).
- **Cycles of innovation**: price → interest → new ideas → startups, a feedback loop
  producing consistent long-term growth in developers and projects.
- **Multi-chain reality**: Ethereum leads developers and block-space demand but Solana,
  Polygon, BNB Chain, Avalanche, Fantom compete on users/usage.
- **L2 scaling**: optimistic vs ZK rollups explained; EIP-4844 flagged as key.
- **DeFi**: grew from ~zero to >$100B TVL in <2 years; would be the 31st-largest US bank.
- **Stablecoins**: three designs; demand for a digital dollar but still a tiny fraction
  of global M1.
- **NFTs**: Web3 platforms have far lower take rates than Big Tech ("higher take rate
  than the mafia"); creators monetise directly.
- **DAOs**: >$10B treasury AUM, mostly in native governance tokens.
- Closing: still "very early days" — Ethereum addresses vs internet users on a log scale.
