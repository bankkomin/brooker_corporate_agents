# Source Summary — Messari Crypto Theses for 2021

**As of: 5 January 2021** · Author: Ryan Selkis ("TBI"), Messari · 134-page annual report

## Document Overview

The fourth edition of Messari founder Ryan Selkis's flagship year-ahead report — a
~134-page "201-level crypto course" written as a mental model for the year. Structured as
a narrative arc: top 10 trends, 10 people to watch, an investment framework, dedicated
Bitcoin and Ethereum chapters, then DeFi, stablecoins, synthetics, credit, infrastructure,
Web3/NFTs and policy.

## Key Findings

- Frames 2021 as the cusp of a new bull run; "narratives over fundamentals."
- Splits the market into "blue chips" valued on real vs relative value models.
- **Bitcoin** as "memetic, programmable gold" — demand exceeding supply for crypto
  custody and (provocatively) "for countries."
- **Ethereum** as the "parallel financial system" / everything marketplace; covers the
  eth2 PoS, sharding and rollup roadmap; argues "ETH is not money."
- **DeFi** "money legos" — Maker, on-chain credit, synthetics, exchange unbundling.
- **Stablecoins** "eating the (crypto) world."
- Predicts NFTs/Web3 may get ahead of themselves in 2021 but will be "incredibly big by
  2025."
- Closes on policy as both a warning flag and the author's pet idea — crypto disrupting
  the nation-state ("the network state").

## Relevance

The foundational annual-outlook document; establishes the recurring vocabulary
(blue chips, money legos, the network state) reused in later Messari reports and across
the research collection. Cross-links to [[bitcoin-digital-gold-thesis]],
