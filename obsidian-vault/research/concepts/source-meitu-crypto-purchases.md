# Source Summary — Meitu Cryptocurrency Investment Plan HKEX Announcements (2021)

**As of: 7 March – 8 April 2021** (three sequential HKEX announcements)

## Document Overview

Three Hong Kong Stock Exchange (HKEX) announcements by **Meitu, Inc.** (Stock Code 1357), a
Cayman-incorporated, HK-listed photo/community-app company. They disclose purchases of
Ether and Bitcoin under a board-approved **Cryptocurrency Investment Plan**. A
Hong Kong-listed counterpart to the [[corporate-bitcoin-treasury-playbook]].

## Key Findings

- **The plan.** A board-approved Cryptocurrency Investment Plan authorizing a **net
  purchase of up to US$100 million** of cryptocurrencies, financed from existing cash
  reserves (not IPO proceeds). "Net purchase" = aggregate market value bought minus value
  sold.
- **Purchase sequence:**
  - 5 March 2021 (voluntary announcement): 15,000 ETH (~$22.1M) + 379.12 BTC (~$17.9M).
  - 17 March 2021 (discloseable transaction): a further 16,000 ETH (~$28.4M) + 386.09 BTC
    (~$21.6M); cumulative net purchase ~$90M.
  - 8 April 2021 (discloseable transaction): a further 175.68 BTC (~$10M); cumulative net
    purchase ~$100M — the plan's cap reached.
- **Rationale.** The Board views blockchain as early-stage (analogous to mobile internet
  ~2005); crypto allocation diversifies away from cash (exposed to debasement) and signals
  the Group's intent to enter the blockchain industry. Ether is also held as a "gas
  reserve" for potential future dApps and as consideration for blockchain project
  investments.
- **Compliance.** Aggregated acquisitions exceeded the 5% percentage-ratio threshold (but
  stayed under 25%), triggering **discloseable-transaction** treatment under Chapter 14 of
  the HKEX Listing Rules. Crypto was held on "renowned trading platform(s)". Purchasing
  subsidiaries: Miracle Vision (Singapore) and Meitu HK.

## Relevance

A worked precedent for a publicly listed company running a digital-asset treasury program
under exchange listing rules — directly comparable to how Brooker (itself a listed entity)
would disclose and govern digital-asset treasury activity.
