# Source Summary — Brooker Hackathon Concepts (2022)

**As of: June–July 2022**

## Document Overview

Two internal **brainstorming documents** for a Brooker-hosted student hackathon: a generic
**contest framework** (eligibility, rounds, judging, prizes, terms) and a specific worked
**"Brook 01 Hackathon"** brief on market-neutral crypto trading strategies.

## Key Findings

**Contest framework**
- A multi-round student competition (First Round online tournament → Round of 16 → Round
  of 8 on-site 24-hour hackathon), hosted by The Brooker Group, open to university
  students of all nationalities.
- Judging across creativity/presentation (30), technical accuracy/practicality (40), and
  economic/social/environmental sustainability (30).

**Brook 01 Hackathon — Market-Neutral Trading Strategies**
- Theme: develop a profitable trading bot with very low price-volatility risk —
  e.g. **funding-rate arbitrage** or triangular three-pair arbitrage.
- Mechanism: collect perpetual-futures funding-rate interest by holding offsetting
  futures/spot positions; if funding is negative, long futures + sell spot; if positive,
  short futures + buy spot. Funding resets every 8 hours.
- Worked example: a 30-day XRP funding-rate-arbitrage trade returning ~1.48% (≈19.85%
  annualized) on ~440,991 beginning cash.
- Key risks identified: tail risk (price spikes / fat-finger liquidations), spread risk
  (futures/spot divergence), liquidity, trading fees.
- Prizes: 300,000 / 100,000 / 50,000 Baht; all code open-sourced (Web3 spirit).

## Relevance

A point-in-time record of Brooker's talent-sourcing and quant-strategy brainstorming. The
Brook 01 brief is a concise primer on the [[market-neutral-funding-rate-arbitrage]]
strategy — relevant to digital-asset treasury native-yield generation.
