# Source Summary — BitOoda Bitcoin Power Analysis (2020)

_As of 14 July 2020. Publisher: [[bitooda]]. Authors: Sam Doctor, Rishabb Suresh._

## Document Overview

A BitOoda research report measuring, locating and pricing global Bitcoin mining power
capacity and modelling miner profitability against future hashrate and capital
constraints.

## Key Findings

- Global mining power capacity ~9.6 GW (~50% China, ~14% US), growing ~10%/yr; ~65%
  utilized, powering ~2.8M mining rigs.
- Network hashrate ~121 EH/s; projected to reach 260 EH/s in 12 months and 360 EH/s in
  24 months via the S19 upgrade cycle.
- Median power price ~3c/kWh; median cash cost to mine 1 BTC ~$5,000.
- ~$6.3B capex to fully fund the upgrade cycle; ~$4.1B external funding gap.
- Each +10 EH/s of hashrate needs ~$1,000 of BTC price appreciation to stay
  revenue-neutral per MWh.

## Relevance

An early quantitative baseline for [[bitcoin-mining-economics]], pre-dating the 2021
China mining ban.
