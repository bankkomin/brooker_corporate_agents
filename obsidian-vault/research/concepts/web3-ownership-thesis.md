# Web3 / Internet-of-Ownership Thesis

## Summary

The "Web3" thesis — pushed hardest by a16z crypto — frames blockchains as the **third era
of the internet**: combining the open, community-governed ethos of Web1 (1990–2005) with
the rich functionality of Web2 (2005–2020), while fixing Web2's flaw — value capture by a
handful of platforms with "higher take rates than the mafia."

## Core Argument

- Web3 gives users **property rights**: the ability to own a piece of the internet
  (tokens, NFTs) rather than rent it from platforms.
- Token incentives **align network participants** toward growing the network they
  collectively own, replacing the extractive platform/user relationship.
- Lower take rates: Web3 platforms (e.g. NFT marketplaces) charge dramatically less than
  Big Tech, returning more to creators.
- DAOs extend community governance to "any kind of organization."
- The crypto market develops in **cycles** — a feedback loop of price → interest →
  new ideas → startups — producing consistent long-term growth in developers and projects.

## Critique

The thesis is promotional (a16z is a major token investor). The 2022 bear market, the
Terra collapse, and the FTX fraud tested it severely. Messari's 2022 Theses paired the
optimism with "The Collapse in Institutional Trust" as its #1 narrative. By 2023 the tone
shifted from "inevitable" to "winter is here, time to build."
