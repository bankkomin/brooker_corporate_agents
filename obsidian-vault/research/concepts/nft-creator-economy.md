# NFTs and the Creator Economy

## Summary

A large cluster of the research collection (Delphi "Crypto Insiders" / NFT Insights and
Real Vision interviews, 2021–2023) treats NFTs not as collectibles speculation but as
**creator-economy infrastructure**: programmable assets that let creators monetise
directly with fans, capture ongoing royalties, and build communities and brands from
zero.

## Core Themes

- **Direct monetisation.** NFTs let artists, musicians (3LAU/Justin Blau), authors
  (Ben Mezrich) and old media (Time magazine's TIMEPieces) sell directly to fans,
  bypassing labels, publishers and platforms — "a joint venture between artists and
  their fans" / "Universal Basic Equity."
- **Programmable royalties.** Smart-contract royalties give creators recurring revenue;
  Nike's NFT collections were shown leading all fashion brands in royalty income.
  (Royalty enforceability later became contested as marketplaces made fees optional.)
- **Brand & treasury from zero.** Nouns DAO demonstrated "hyperscaling a brand and
  treasury" — one NFT auctioned daily, proceeds into an on-chain treasury, CC0 IP.
- **Distribution-model design.** Free mints, allowlists, "free-to-own," and phygital
  (physical+digital) forging (Nike x RTFKT Clone X) emerged as deliberate go-to-market
  levers, not afterthoughts.
- **Cyclicality.** NFT trading volume is highly cyclical; mid-2022 onward saw "anemic"
  volumes and a "major Web3 recession," even as marketplaces (OpenSea) stayed profitable.

## Historical Context

NFTs broke out in 2021 (PFP projects, BAYC, art, gaming). 2022's bear market collapsed
speculative volume but the creator-infrastructure thesis persisted. Delphi's 2023 "Year
Ahead for NFTs" pivoted focus to utility, distribution and sustainable brands.
