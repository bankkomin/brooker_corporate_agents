# Source Summary — Crypto Funds in Hong Kong: An Overview (Tiang & Partners / PwC, 2022)

**As of: 6 January 2022**

## Document Overview

A practitioner guide, "Crypto Funds in Hong Kong — An Overview", produced by **Tiang &
Partners** (an independent Hong Kong law firm collaborating with PwC). It covers the key
considerations for setting up a crypto fund in Hong Kong: licensing, taxation, fund
structures, documentation, and service providers.

## Key Findings

- **Market context.** Global crypto-fund AUM grew ~90% year-on-year to an estimated
  US$3.8B in 2020; 21% of traditional hedge funds had already invested in digital assets.
- **Licensing — the Type 9 question.** A Hong Kong **Type 9 (Asset Management) licence**
  is required to manage a portfolio of "securities" or "futures contracts" ("SF Assets").
  Whether a digital token is an SF Asset depends on its features: ICO tokens conferring
  equity/ownership or debt-like rights are likely "securities" under the SFO; pure utility
  tokens are "virtual commodities" and are not.
- **Type 9 VA Licence regime.** Introduced October 2019, this expands the Type 9 licence
  for managers directly managing virtual assets. It is **part compulsory, part optional**:
  compulsory (Pro Forma terms & conditions imposed) for existing Type 9 managers whose
  virtual assets are ≥10% of portfolio gross asset value; managers keeping virtual assets
  under 10% need not opt in.
- **Scope of the guide** also covers taxation, typical fund structures, fund documentation
  (PPM, management/advisory agreements, subscription/redemption forms), and service
  providers — delivered as an integrated "one-stop shop" with PwC's tax/assurance teams.

## Relevance

A regulatory reference for structuring a crypto/digital-asset fund in Hong Kong — relevant
to Brooker's VCC fund-platform and digital-asset strategy where a Hong Kong vehicle or
