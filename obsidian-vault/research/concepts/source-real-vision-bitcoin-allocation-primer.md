# Source Summary — Real Vision: Bitcoin Asset Allocation Primer

**As of: January 2021** · Author: Joel Coverdale, CFA (Eight Isle) · Real Vision white paper

## Document Overview

A Real Vision white paper authored by risk-analytics specialist Joel Coverdale, aimed at
institutional allocators. It addresses Bitcoin's risk and return profile and how it fits
into an institutional portfolio using industry-standard risk models (Qontigo/Barra).

## Key Findings

- Bitcoin can and should be treated like other assets within standard risk frameworks —
  "it should not be treated that differently."
- Despite high standalone volatility, Bitcoin's characteristics provide "appealing
  benefits for a globally diversified investment strategy" (low correlation).
- Aims to add structure to the institutionalisation of Bitcoin and give allocators a
  vocabulary their trustees, consultants and treasurers understand.
- Real Vision's editorial framing (Raoul Pal): crypto's institutional adoption has been
  slower than necessary because the crypto community has not spoken the institutional
  language.

## Relevance

A primary source for [[crypto-portfolio-allocation]] — the worked argument for sizing a
Bitcoin position with standard risk models. Supports [[institutional-crypto-adoption]].
