# Source Summary — Delphi NFT Insights Weekly Updates (2022)

**As of: June–September 2022** · Delphi Digital (Teng Yan, Adrian Von Catangal et al.)

## Document Overview

A consolidated summary of seven Delphi "NFT Insights" weekly/retail updates from mid-2022
(8 Jun, 27 Jul, 3 Aug, 25 Aug, 31 Aug, 1 Sep, 21 Sep). Each covers NFT market state and
specific project developments. Grouped here as one source-summary as they are a recurring
weekly series rather than distinct reports.

## Key Findings

- **Bear-market market state**: NFT trading volumes were "anemic" / "flat" throughout
  summer 2022; August was the lowest-volume month of the year. Marketplaces (OpenSea)
  stayed profitable — ~$325M weekly sales, ~$1.1M/day OpenSea revenue (June).
- **Free-mint season**: a wave of "free mint" and "free-to-own" launch models (FLUF
  World's Futureverse, Memeland's Potatoz, y00ts, 10KTF).
- **Phygital**: Nike x RTFKT Clone X forging — combining physical and digital products.
- **Royalties**: Nike's NFT collections led all fashion brands in royalty income.
- **Project coverage**: Otherside/Otherdeed (Kodas/Voyager), Azuki, BAYC ecosystem.
- In Case You Missed It items track macro/regulatory events (e.g. DC AG suing Michael
  Saylor; Helium's proposed migration to Solana).

## Relevance

Granular, point-in-time evidence for [[nft-creator-economy]] (free-mint / phygital /
royalties) and a [[crypto-cycles-and-liquidity]] bear-market marker.

## Extracted Data

- OpenSea weekly sales (June 2022): ~$325M (~$45M/day); est. revenue ~$1.1M/day.
- August 2022: lowest NFT trading-volume month of the year.
