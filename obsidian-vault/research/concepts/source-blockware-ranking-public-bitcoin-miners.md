# Source Summary — Blockware: Ranking Public Bitcoin Miners

**As of: February 2023** (financial data from Q3 2022) · Blockware Solutions Intelligence

## Document Overview

A Blockware Solutions slide deck presenting a framework to quantify the liquidity,
solvency and profitability of publicly-traded Bitcoin miners after the 2022 distress.

## Key Findings

- 2022 spotlighted miner financial health: excessive leverage + higher rates + rising
  difficulty + energy costs + falling asset prices caused industry-wide distress.
- The **"Miner Score"** ranks miners on the average percentile across four metrics:
  Breakeven Price (Cost of Revenue), Current Ratio, Debt-to-Equity, and Cash Ratio.
- By Q3 2022, after total OpEx, **no public miner** had a breakeven below BTC's 9/30/22
  close of $19,426.
- Core Scientific (CORZ) scored lowest (5.6), filed Chapter 11 on 20 Dec 2022, was
  delisted (−100% in Q4). WULF and GREE announced debt restructurings; SDIG a
  debt-to-equity swap. CleanSpark (CLSK) was the most resilient (down only −35.9% in Q4).
- Blockware's editorial conclusion: it prefers mining BTC directly to buying miner
  equities, to eliminate counterparty/market/credit risk.
