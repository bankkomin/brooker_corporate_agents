# Crypto Market Cycles and the Liquidity Lens

## Summary

A recurring analytical frame across the research collection treats crypto prices as a
function of **adoption cycles and global liquidity**, layered on top of (not instead of)
fundamental valuation. Two complementary versions appear: a16z's "cycles of innovation"
(price → interest → ideas → startups → repeat) and Real Vision's macro-liquidity model
(Raoul Pal / Jamie Coutts), which ties crypto's direction to central-bank liquidity.

## Core Ideas

- **Fastest adoption in history.** Raoul Pal: crypto reached 150M users growing ~113%/yr
  vs the internet's 63%/yr at the same user count — implying the asset class scales toward
  the size of other major asset classes.
- **Cycles look chaotic but are ordered.** a16z shows developer, startup and social
  activity rising and falling with price in a self-reinforcing feedback loop, netting
  consistent long-term growth.
- **Liquidity cycle minded investing.** RV Pro Crypto frames drawdowns (e.g. Mt. Gox /
  government BTC sales in 2024) as absorbable supply events and buying opportunities for
  liquidity-cycle investors.
- **On-chain signals.** Fidelity's Signals Report formalises a metric toolkit by time
  horizon — MVRV Z-Score, NUPL, Puell Multiple, realized price, hodler net position,
  reserve risk — to read cycle position.
- **Supply overhang.** RV's supply-scoring model: Bitcoin's ~6% supply overhang is far
  below the ~29% altcoin average (pre-mine / insider vesting), a structural advantage.
