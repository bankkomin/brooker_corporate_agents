# Asian Finance 5-Year Rolling Business Plan (2025–2030)

## Summary

The 5-year rolling business plan for **[[asian-finance|Asian Finance]]** (Advance
Finance / [[adfin]]), Version 7, presented to its Board on **15 December 2025** and
marked "for internal discussion only". The plan repositions the institution as an
independent, low-fixed-cost, non-deposit lending platform focused on SME and retail
customers, targeting a required return of **at least 15%**.

## Goal

> "To become the best, stable, and efficient financial institution focusing on SME and
> retail in the country with a required return of at least 15%."

Pillars: modern risk framework and control, best-in-class asset quality, innovative
working methods, stable and consistent performance, highest efficiency, best response to
customers.

## Portfolio Transformation

The headline strategic shift:

- **Real estate exposure cut from ~45% (2025) to ~4% (2030)**.
- Overall loan portfolio **enlarged ~13× by 2030**.
- Five strategic levers: (A) ESG & sustainability focus, (B) capital recycle strategy,
  (C) diversification & working-capital emphasis, (D) networking approach, plus a focus
  on supply-chain customers in target industries to enhance lending yield with controlled risk.

## Funding Strategy

- Target an **"A category" credit rating** via an enhanced equity & hybrid capital
  structure.
- Enlarge capital through profit generation first, then create a quality cornerstone
  investor base for capital increases.
- Multiple capital-increase milestones charted across Dec 2025 – Mar 2026.

## 2026 Budget Scenario

The 2026 annual budget models the **Expected Credit Loss (ECL) between 1% and 4%**,
producing a profit range of roughly **Bt 0.71M to Bt 139.91M** depending on the ECL
outcome. NII (Net Interest Income) increases despite a 4% ECL assumption in Year 2026;
early-stage depreciation cost weighs on the profit line.
