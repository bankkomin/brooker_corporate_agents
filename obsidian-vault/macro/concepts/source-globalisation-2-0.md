# Source: Globalisation 2.0 (ARP Megatrend #7)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **25 May 2022** (extract `_Modified:` 2023-01-10). Author: Niels C. Jensen,
[[absolute-return-partners]]. The paper that *added* the seventh megatrend.

## Key Findings

- International trade has stopped rising as a share of global GDP; the turning point was
  the 2008 crisis, which fed the populism behind Brexit and Trump.
- G20 protectionist measures hit an all-time high (~5,560) 2008-2016; the Russia-Ukraine
  war added momentum. Framed as "Globalisation 2.0" — a reconfiguration, not an end.
- Trade may go *regional*: RCEP (10 ASEAN + 5 partners) entered force 1 Jan 2022,
  covering ~2.2bn people and ~30% of global GDP.
- Losers: carbon-intensive manufacturers (EU carbon scheme widening), Big Tech (EU
  regulatory campaign). Winners: green metals (lithium, copper).
- ARP *retired* the old "Currency Wars / QE" investment theme — judging QT and rising
  inflation had ended the 40-year risk-asset tailwind.
