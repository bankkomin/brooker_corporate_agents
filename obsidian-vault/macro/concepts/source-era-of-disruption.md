# Source: The Era of Disruption (ARP Megatrend #5)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **17 November 2022** (extract `_Modified:` 2023-01-10). Author: Niels C.
Jensen, [[absolute-return-partners]]. Update of megatrend #5 of [[the-seven-megatrends]].

## Key Findings

- Disruption is a *process*, and **technology is the tool, not the root cause** —
  changing customer behaviour is (Uber, Amazon examples; Thales Teixeira's work).
- IoT and LPWAN connections are scaling fast (>2bn projected by 2025) but slower than
  McKinsey's early estimates; blockchain "could do to banks what Amazon did to retail."
- Five investment themes: urbanisation, the digital revolution, electrification of
  everything, the death of fossil fuels, the rise of populism.
- Green-metal shortage is a binding constraint: proven reserves are ~20% (copper),
  ~10% (nickel, lithium), <5% (cobalt, graphite, vanadium) of what the green
  transition requires.
- Tesla flagged as a former favourite short (peaked Nov 2021 ~$407, then halved).

## Relevance

Megatrend #5 of [[the-seven-megatrends]]; complements the GMI [[the-exponential-age]]
