# The Seven Megatrends (Absolute Return Partners)

## Summary

The "Megatrends" are the structured long-horizon framework around which
[[absolute-return-partners]] (Niels C. Jensen) organises all of its research, product
development, asset allocation and portfolio construction. Megatrends are explicitly
*not* short-term: each paper is refreshed roughly every three years and carries a set
of associated *investment themes*.

## The List (as of 2022)

The list was restructured in early 2020 from eight trends to six (with
wealth-to-GDP reframed as the *aggregate result* of the others). A seventh —
Globalisation 2.0 — was added in May 2022.

## The Era of Disruption (#5)

ARP's disruption megatrend argues — citing Harvard Business Review and Thales Teixeira —
that **technology is not the root cause of disruption**; *changing customer behaviour*
is, with technology as the enabling tool (Uber, Amazon as examples). Associated themes:
urbanisation, the digital revolution, electrification of everything, the death of
fossil fuels, and the rise of populism. ARP flags banks as a long-term disruption
target (blockchain could do to banks what Amazon did to retail) and green-metal
shortages (copper, lithium, nickel, cobalt, graphite, vanadium) as a binding constraint
on the green transition.

## Climate Change (#6)

Megatrend #6. (The ingested `Climate Change.pdf` extract is image-only with no

the disruption and globalisation papers via electrification, the death of fossil fuels,
nuclear's renaissance (incl. Small Modular Reactors), and rising sea levels.
