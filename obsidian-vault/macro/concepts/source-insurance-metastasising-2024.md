# Source: Insurance — Metastasising — Macro Insiders Deep Dive (Jul 2024)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **28 July 2024** (extract `_Modified:` 2024-08-05). Author: Julian Brigden,
Macro Insiders Deep Dive ([[global-macro-investor]] / Real Vision).

## Key Findings

- A cautionary analysis of the US property-insurance market. Insurers are exiting key
  markets (Florida, Texas, California), leaving households underinsured or uninsured
  despite double-digit premium hikes.
- Legislation meant to protect homeowners from rate hikes has backfired — the market is
  less competitive and increasingly state-subsidised, socialising disaster risk onto
  taxpayers.
- The logical conclusion: either insurance costs rise until properly-capitalised new
  entrants return — forcing households to revise all-in housing costs and depressing
  property values (and collateral) — or insurance becomes impossible, raising the
  spectre of no recovery from neighbourhood disasters and breached mortgage covenants.
- Framed as an endogenous, "non-Goldilocks" problem that will exacerbate the next
  risk-off episode.

## Relevance

A specific, second-order risk to housing collateral and the financial system — a
"non-Goldilocks" outcome relevant to the [[business-cycle-cowbell]] risk picture.
