# Source: Mean Reversion of Wealth-to-GDP

## Document Overview

As-of date: **17 February 2021** (extract `_Modified:` 2023-01-10). Author: Niels C.
Jensen, ARP+ Research, [[absolute-return-partners]].

## Key Findings

- US wealth-to-GDP traded ~350-400% (mean ~380%) for decades, then rose to ~583% by
  Q3 2020 — ~50% above the long-term mean. It *must* mean-revert (economic-growth
  theory holds capital-to-output stable at ~3-4x).
- ARP's arithmetic: to revert, US wealth must fall from ~US$116.5tn toward ~US$80tn
  (~-31%); equities and property would take most of the hit.
- A bursting bubble could be the catalyst. ARP lists nine common bubble traits and
  flags the joint Tesla/Bitcoin run as "irrational exuberance."
- ARP judged Big Tech (FAAMG) earnings as broadly justifying their valuations; located
  bubble behaviour mainly in small, unprofitable tech. Leverage-light bubbles do less
  damage than leveraged ones.
