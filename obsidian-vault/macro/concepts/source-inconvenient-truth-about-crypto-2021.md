# Source: The Inconvenient Truth About Crypto Currencies — GMI (Jan 2021)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **20 January 2021** (extract `_Modified:` 2021-08-25). Author: Raoul Pal,
a Global Macro Investor / Macro Insiders bonus article ([[global-macro-investor]]).

## Key Findings

- Pal argues the crypto narrative is stuck in 2017-era "tribalism" and tries to bring a
  pragmatic, macro-investor lens to the digital-asset space.
- Critiques **Bitcoin Maximalism** — the belief that all non-Bitcoin assets are
  "scams" and the world ends in "hyperbitcoinization." Pal shares the optimism but not
  the libertarian/utopian framing.
- Applies a traditional asset-allocation lens to crypto: **liquidity preference, time
  preference, risk preference** — calculating risk-adjusted returns across the cycle.
- Notes crypto investors suffer recency bias from only ~12 years of price history.
- Ties crypto to the broader theme of societal discord from 40 years of relative wage
  deflation driven by demographics, globalisation, fiat money and technology.

## Relevance

An early statement of the disciplined, macro-investor approach to crypto that underpins
[[the-exponential-age]] — crypto as an asset class, not an ideology.
