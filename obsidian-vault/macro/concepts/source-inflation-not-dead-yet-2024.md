# Source: Inflation — Not Dead Yet — Macro Insiders In Focus (Jul 2024)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **16 July 2024** (extract `_Modified:` 2024-07-16). Author: Julian Brigden,
Macro Insiders In Focus ([[global-macro-investor]] / Real Vision).

## Key Findings

- Brigden argues the **sticky-inflation** side of the [[inflation-regime-debate]]:
  negative base effects are dropping out of the data, and rising goods prices could
  more than offset flat-to-falling services inflation.
- This is a "narrative breach" for a market positioned for sequential rate cuts — the
  June 2024 BofA Fund Manager Survey showed 84% expecting a soft/no landing; markets
  priced an 83% chance of at least 5x25bp cuts by the September 2025 FOMC.
- History: the odds of a "just right" Goldilocks balance are at best ~1:3 — you either
  under-tighten (inflation sticky) or over-tighten (recession).
- Loose financial conditions point to headline CPI ~200bp higher by October than a
  year earlier. Brigden is looking to add volatility trades into the summer.

## Relevance

The clearest sticky-side voice in the [[inflation-regime-debate]] — a direct
counterpoint to Raoul Pal's disinflation view within the same research family.
