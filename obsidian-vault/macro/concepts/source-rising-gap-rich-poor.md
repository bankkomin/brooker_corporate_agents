# Source: The Rising Gap between Rich and Poor (ARP Megatrend #3)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **16 April 2020** (extract `_Modified:` 2023-01-10). Author: Niels C.
Jensen, [[absolute-return-partners]]. Review paper for megatrend #3 of
[[the-seven-megatrends]].

## Key Findings

- The wealth and income gap has widened sharply since the 1980s (Reaganomics). The top
  0.1% now holds as much US wealth as the bottom 90% — last seen in the 1930s.
- The top 1% captured ~27% of all global income growth 1980-2016. Real wage growth
  since the 2008 crisis is near-zero across major OECD economies.
- The "cycle of discontent": inequality → populism → misallocated capital → low
  productivity → low GDP growth. Populist vote share in Europe ~25%.
- National income splits stably ~2/3 labour, ~1/3 capital; labour's share has fallen
  to 55-60% and must mean-revert — compressing corporate profits, favouring value.
- ARP's microeconomic GDP framing: growth re-accelerates only by pushing the demand
  curve out faster than supply, or by productive public spending.
