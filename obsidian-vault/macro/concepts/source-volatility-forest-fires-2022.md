# Source: Volatility and Forest Fires — Variant Perception (May 2022)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **26 May 2022** (extract `_Modified:` 2022-06-24). Publisher: Variant
Perception — *Understanding Volatility*.

## Key Findings

- Central-bank suppression of volatility is analogised to indiscriminately putting out
  forest fires: it produces denser "debris" and enables unusually large eventual
  wildfires — i.e. suppressed volatility builds latent systemic risk.
- A structural volatility framework with three medium-term drivers: (1) the
  credit/liquidity cycle, (2) the business cycle, (3) the volatility seller's yield.
- A tactical framework: the VP Correction Signal, implied-vs-realised correlations,
  cumulative advance-decline applied to volatility, fair-value vol models, gamma by
  strike/expiry.

## Relevance

A volatility-specific lens that complements the [[global-liquidity-cycle]] (credit /
liquidity cycle) and [[business-cycle-cowbell]] frameworks.
