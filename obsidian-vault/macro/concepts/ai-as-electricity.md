# AI as Electricity, AI as Magic

## Summary

An investment-lens thesis from Macro Insiders (Raoul Pal, Aug 2024) for thinking about
where value accrues in the AI cycle. The core metaphor: large language models are like
**electricity** — revolutionary but destined to be commodified into a race to the
bottom on price. The durable value sits in the **"magic"**: human context and
distribution. It is a practical sub-thesis of [[the-exponential-age]].

## The Three Layers

| Layer | Metaphor | Who wins | Economics |
|-------|----------|----------|-----------|
| LLMs / models | Electricity | Commodified | Race to the bottom on price |
| Tools / chips | Shovels in a gold rush | Nvidia, TSMC | Hard to dislodge incumbents |
| Applications | The "magic" | Tech giants (Apple, Google, Meta) | Human context + distribution |

## The Apple–OpenAI Deal

The thesis was crystallised by Apple's 2024 integration of generative AI ("Apple
Intelligence") and its partnership with OpenAI — a deal where *no money changed hands*.
Pal reads it as a "marriage of convenience": OpenAI gets distribution; Apple gets the
model. Sam Altman's relentless public presence is read as a strategy to maximise
OpenAI interactions and "monopolise the news" before LLM breakthroughs are commodified.

## The Investment Conclusion

What matters is not owning the model but owning **distribution and human context** —
the seamless user experiences (Apple's billions of daily iPhone checks), search
(Google), social (Meta) — plus the irreplaceable "shovels" (Nvidia, TSMC). Expect
industrial-policy "friend-shoring" attempts around the chip layer.
