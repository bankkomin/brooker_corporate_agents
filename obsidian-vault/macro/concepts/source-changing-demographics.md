# Source: Changing Demographics (ARP Megatrend #2)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **11 January 2021** (extract `_Modified:` 2023-01-10). Author: Niels C.
Jensen, [[absolute-return-partners]]. Review paper for megatrend #2 of
[[the-seven-megatrends]].

## Key Findings

- The OECD will lose ~150 million workers to retirement by 2050; 42 countries already
  had negative working-age population growth as of end-2019.
- Japan, South Korea, Italy, Spain, Germany are most affected; the US least (workforce
  still grows ~0.27% p.a.). Global growth (~0.67% p.a.) is almost entirely Africa.
- Ageing is structurally disinflationary (the Japan precedent), against the
  supply-curve counter-argument. Medical-care prices are the exception.
- Boomers own ~60% of US wealth (>US$74tn); their retirement turns an equity-market
  tailwind into a headwind (the "Spending Wave" / Arnott-Chaves work).
- Raising the retirement age postpones but cannot eliminate the problem; higher female
  participation and immigration are politically/structurally limited.
