# Rise of the East

## Summary

"Rise of the East" is megatrend #4 of [[absolute-return-partners]]'s
[[the-seven-megatrends]]. It argues that economic and overall power is shifting
structurally from West to East — led by China but extending across Asia — with
far-reaching consequences for growth, resources, and geopolitics.

## The Core Argument

- Chinese GDP grew tenfold in the 26 years to the 2008 crisis — faster than the West's
  industrial revolution. On a PPP-adjusted basis China was already the largest economy
  as of writing; on a nominal USD basis, base-case assumptions put the crossover ~11
  years out (early 2030s).
- ARP applies **game theory** (Nash, Harsanyi): overall power has four sources —
  *economic power*, *risk tolerance*, *threat power*, and *coalition power*. China is
  rising on all four while the West's risk tolerance and coalition cohesion decline.
- India and Indonesia rise too — "the Asian century is about to begin."

## The Food and Water Dimension

A rising Asian middle class drives a protein-and-resource demand shock: between now and
2050 humanity must produce more food than in the previous 10,000 years combined.
Agriculture consumes ~60% of global freshwater, so rising Asian living standards make
water scarcity a first-order risk.

## The Flip Side of the China Story

ARP tempers the bull case: China's growth is one-dimensional (capital investment ~45%
of GDP vs ~20% in the West), total debt-to-GDP has reached G7 levels (~250%), the
demographic outlook is poor, and the current account was never fully opened. China's
disregard for international rulings (South China Sea) weakens its coalition power.
