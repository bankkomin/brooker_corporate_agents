# Dollar Dynamics — The Wrecking Ball and the Doom Loop

## Summary

A recurring macro thesis on the US dollar that runs counter to the popular
"de-dollarization" narrative: because most global debt and trade are denominated in
dollars, a debased dollar tends to *rise*, not fall, and dollar strength is itself a
systemic risk. The dollar is "the Big Daddy of global assets" — the true global
denominator.

## The Counter-Intuitive Mechanism

- The popular myth that debasement weakens the dollar is "180 degrees wrong." In the
  GMI framing the **dollar lags the Fed balance sheet by ~18 months** and rises over
  time, because other major currencies (euro, yen, pound, yuan) are also debasing but
  lack the reserve-currency structural bid.
- When global GDP growth slows, the dollar *rises* as borrowers scramble to service
  dollar debts; when the cycle turns up and liquidity is abundant, the dollar corrects.
  It is "all a giant liquidity cycle." See [[global-liquidity-cycle]].

## The Wrecking Ball and the Doom Loop (2022)

In the 2022 GMI material, the "dollar wrecking ball" was in full swing — the DXY
heading toward 117-120, the second-largest move in three decades. The "dollar Doom
Loop": Fed tightening → scarcer dollar liquidity → foreign banks sell Treasuries →
rates up → dollar up → repeat. The thesis: "the dollar dies of its own strength, not
its weakness" — a final blow-off forces the world to splinter into regional currency
systems.

## The Eurodollar Dimension

Post-2008 Basel III forced banks to hold higher reserves, restricting the global supply
of dollars and making the world "entirely beholden to the Federal Reserve for
liquidity" — a key reason the global business cycle is near-perfectly correlated.
