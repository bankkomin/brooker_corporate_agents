# Source: The GMI Business Cycle Dominoes — Macro Insiders (Apr 2023)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **28 April 2023** (extract `_Modified:` 2023-05-02). Author: Raoul Pal,
Macro Insiders Deep Dive ([[global-macro-investor]] / Real Vision).

## Key Findings

- The "GMI dominoes": leading vs lagging indicators mapping the likely path of the US
  economy into Q2 2023 and beyond.
- Key lead/lag relationships: **China leads the cycle by ~16 months**; **CPI Shelter
  (the stickiest inflation component) lags by ~17 months**.
- The cycle trough is "coming relatively soon." Markets focus on lagging data
  (inflation, employment, wages), so the asset repricing has yet to gather steam.
- Recent positive activity surprises are attributed to idiosyncratic factors (benign
  weather, CPI revisions) that will flip — the case for bonds is improving.
- The Fed will cut into the lagging indicators — "late and aggressively."
