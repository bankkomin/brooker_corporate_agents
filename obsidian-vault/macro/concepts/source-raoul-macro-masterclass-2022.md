# Source: Raoul's Macro Masterclass (Apr 2022)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **25 April 2022** (recorded; published 2 May 2022; extract `_Modified:`
2022-08-29). Speaker: Raoul Pal, [[global-macro-investor]] / Real Vision — a ~86-minute
video transcript opening a multi-week interview series on global recession risk.

## Key Findings

- Pal lays out his out-of-consensus framework: the 2022 inflation spike is driven by
  supply shocks not seen since WWII / the 1970s, and is *transitory* — the persistent
  1970s inflation was a *demographic demand shock* (Boomers entering the workforce);
  today's demographics are the reverse. See [[inflation-regime-debate]].
- He frames a coming recession and lays out the [[business-cycle-cowbell]] logic — a
  growth slowdown forces the Fed to pivot.
- The series cross-checks his view against top managers (Dwight Anderson, Pierre
  Andurand, Cathie Wood).

## Relevance

A consolidated verbal statement of the GMI framework — the disinflation thesis, the
business cycle, and the bridge to [[the-exponential-age]].
