# The Retirement Crisis

## Summary

A recurring [[global-macro-investor]] secular theme: the developed-world retirement and
pension system is structurally under-funded, and currency debasement has made the
problem worse by routing asset gains to those who already own assets while wages
stagnate. It is the personal-finance consequence of [[demographics-as-destiny]],
[[rising-inequality]] and [[currency-debasement]].

## The Naked Numbers

- The median US Baby Boomer has ~US$150,000 in retirement savings.
- Gen X — now in their 50s, ~a decade from retirement — has saved a median of only
  ~US$40,000.
- The US retirement system is built on buying equities to fund retirement, yet the
  *purchasing power of wages in equities has fallen ~50% since 2008* — each year wages
  buy fewer assets, so under-saved cohorts cannot catch up.
- Defined-benefit pension plans are deeply under-funded: when DB pensions were designed,
  post-retirement life expectancy was under 10 years; it is now over 20.

## Why It Matters

- The 60/40 portfolio — the mainstay of the global pension system — suffered its
  largest fall in history in 2022.
- Retiring Boomers (who own ~60% of US wealth) shift from being net buyers of equities
  to net sellers, turning a multi-decade market tailwind into a headwind.
- The GMI argument: this desperation drives later adopters toward higher-risk assets —
  crypto framed as "the life raft" / "the greatest macro trade of all time" — and
  toward outright speculation (meme coins, lottery tickets).
