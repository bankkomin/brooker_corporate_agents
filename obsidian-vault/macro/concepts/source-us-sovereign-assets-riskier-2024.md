# Source: Are US Sovereign Assets Getting Riskier? — CrossBorder Capital (Jul 2024)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **July 2024** (extract `_Modified:` 2024-08-15). Publisher:
[[crossborder-capital]] — a *Global View* thematic note.

## Key Findings

- The late-June 2024 US Treasury sell-off coincided with the Biden-Trump TV debate —
  political risk being priced into bonds. But the bigger story is that term premia
  were *already* mean-reverting higher.
- US term premia were deeply negative (~-115bp), reverting toward a positive +34bp
  average (1990-2020). CrossBorder targets the 10-year yield testing ~5½% with the
  curve steepening.
- CBO/IMF projections: US public-debt/GDP testing ~230% over 25 years; annual public
  debt growth above 15% of GDP.
- The four-phase liquidity cycle determines how the term structure shifts; the US is
  in the **Rebound** phase, where the curve typically begins to steepen.
- World bond term premia are rising generally — fiscal imprudence and monetary
  inflation, not just US politics.
