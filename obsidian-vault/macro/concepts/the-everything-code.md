# The Everything Code

## Summary

"The Everything Code" is the all-encompassing macro thesis developed by Raoul Pal of
[[global-macro-investor]] and published in 2023, presented in three parts — *The
Renaissance* (the end-state), *The Set-Up* (how we got here), and *The Everything
Code* itself. Its central claim: asset prices are not driven by valuations or
corporate fundamentals but are an optical function of **G5 central bank balance
sheets** and the **debasement of fiat currency**. Pal claims ~97% of S&P 500
movements can be explained by combined G5 balance sheets. It is the recurring
backbone thesis of the GMI / Real Vision material in this knowledge area.

## The Magic Formula

The framework begins with a formula for long-run growth:

> **GDP Growth = Population Growth + Productivity Growth + Debt Growth**

- **Population growth** is collapsing (the demographic driver of GDP is "dead, forever").
- **Productivity growth** has faded since the 1960s, dragged down by an ageing,
  non-productive retiree cohort.
- **Debt growth** has therefore risen to plug the gap — total US debt > 250% of GDP,
  trend GDP growth down from ~5% to ~1.75% (a 65% fall).

## The Clockwork Economy and 2008

After 2008, all developed-world debt was refinanced into the cheap, short end of the
curve (3-5 years), producing a near-perfect ~3½-year "clockwork" business cycle in
which all GDP growth is consumed by interest payments. Pal argues 2008 was "Peak Fiat"
— the moment most non-US asset markets peaked in USD terms — and that interest rates
*must* stay at or below GDP growth or the system forces a debt deflation.

## Liquidity vs Debasement — The Key Insight

Pal's breakthrough was distinguishing **liquidity** from **debasement**. QE is not
merely a liquidity tool; it is the *purposeful monetisation of government interest
payments* and a debasement of the world's reserve currency. By devaluing the
denominator (fiat), asset prices rise optically. See [[currency-debasement]]. He
argues central banks act in a global "Accord" and that:

- P/E ratios are now monetary indicators, not valuations.
- Earnings rise with M2; asset prices rise with the larger central-bank balance sheet
  — the gap *is* P/E expansion.
- Only two assets escape the trap: technology equities and crypto — see
  [[the-exponential-age]].

## The Renaissance — End-State

The thesis resolves into "The Renaissance": a 10-15 year period of yield curve control
(à la Japan and post-WWII USA) and heavy fiscal stimulus, after which the productivity
miracle of [[the-exponential-age]] revives GDP. Pal frames digital-asset ownership as
the escape route from central-bank CBDC control.
