# Source: Last Stages of the Debt Supercycle (ARP Megatrend #1)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **19 October 2022** (extract `_Modified:` 2023-01-10). Author: Niels C.
Jensen, [[absolute-return-partners]]. ~3-year update of megatrend #1.

## Key Findings

- The developed world is in the late, "panic" stage of the debt supercycle. Total
  global debt exceeded US$300tn (2022); the IMF called debt "dangerously high."
- Late-stage markers: low yields, low risk premia, decelerating productivity, anaemic
  GDP, misallocated capital. Monetary policy stops working — "pushing on a string."
- No benign exit: debt is destroyed via default (disruptive, 2008) or inflation (less
  disruptive, c.1980). ARP's 2022 view: inflation is — conveniently — doing the job.
- Three investment themes: Out-of-Control Public Deficits; Regulatory Arbitrage
  (~90% of US SME lending is now non-bank); the Productivity Conundrum.
- Jensen openly flags his own 2019 forecast error (that rates would stay low) — noting
  *real* rates remain very low.
