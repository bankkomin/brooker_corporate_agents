# Creatures and Machines — The Accelerate/Decelerate Divide

## Summary

A societal-and-political thesis from Real Vision's *The Exponentialist* (David Mattin),
the social counterpart to [[the-exponential-age]]. Its claim: technology — above all
AI — is now the most powerful force shaping society, and the old conservative-vs-
progressive political axis is being replaced by a new divide between those who want to
**accelerate** the technology revolution and those who want to **decelerate** it.

## The Argument

- The conservative/progressive framework, born of the French Revolution, has been
  "scrambled" by permanent technological revolution — to "conserve" the status quo now
  means to preserve perpetual change.
- A new polarity is emerging: **accelerationists** (Marc Andreessen's *Techno-Optimist
  Manifesto*, the e/acc movement) vs **decelerationists** (degrowth, AI-doom). It cuts
  across old party lines and is visible across energy, finance/blockchain, and AI.
- The endgame is the deepest form of the divide: **augmented vs organic humans** — those
  who merge with machine intelligence via brain-machine interfaces (Kurzweil's
  singularity, Harari's *Homo Deus*) and those who refuse. Mattin invokes Isaiah
  Berlin's *value pluralism* and calls for a "post-liberalism" / "multi-speed
  modernity" that lets both groups coexist.

## The AI Consciousness Question

A linked Raoul Pal piece (*Do Android Robots Dream of Electric Sheep?*, 2024) pushes
further: as AI context windows reach billions of tokens and persistent memory is
introduced, the question of whether AI is — or convincingly simulates — *consciousness*
becomes unavoidable. Pal's provocation: if AI creates the illusion of consciousness,
does the distinction even matter?

## Why It Matters

For a macro/strategy reader, the value is in framing technology adoption as a coming
*political* faultline — relevant to regulation, the energy transition, AI policy, and
the social stability that underpins markets. It also surfaces a US/China split into
"two great systems of AI."
