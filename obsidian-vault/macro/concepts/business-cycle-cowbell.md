# The Business Cycle and "More Cowbell"

## Summary

The recurring [[global-macro-investor]] framework for trading the business cycle:
a debt-saturated economy "runs on stimulus," so every meaningful slowdown forces the
authorities to ease — QE, rate cuts and fiscal stimulus — which Raoul Pal labels
**"cowbell"** (from an online meme). Markets anticipate the cowbell Pavlovianly,
pricing the turn well before the stimulus arrives.

## The Key Indicators

- **The ISM** — the central business-cycle gauge. Since the 1960s, every time the ISM
  crossed below 50 the Fed cut rates (the 2016 pause aside). Regional Fed surveys
  (Philly, Chicago, Empire, Richmond) lead the ISM.
- **The China Credit Impulse** — "one of the most important charts in the world,"
  leading the global ISM by ~12-18 months and asset prices at various lags.
- **The GMI Financial Conditions Index** — combines rates, commodities and the dollar;
  leads by ~9 months.
- **Global M2** — when M2 turns, "markets change and the economy changes."

## The Conditions for "Cowbell"

GMI's October 2022 list of triggers that raise the probability of stimulus: (1)
inflation surprising lower, (2) unemployment rising, (3) ISM crossing 47, (4) the
dollar spiking, (5) yields decoupling, (6) equities collapsing, (7) systemic distress.
"Not all the boxes need to be ticked."

## The Phases

GMI labels the cycle phases — e.g. **"Macro Summer"** (inflation a non-issue, growth
and earnings picking up, liquidity spigots open) and the **"Banana Zone"** (the
liquidity-driven risk-on melt-up). These connect the business cycle to
[[currency-debasement]] (the cowbell *is* debasement) and the
[[global-liquidity-cycle]].

## Sentiment as a Contrarian Signal

The 2022 material leaned heavily on record-bearish sentiment (AAII, Investor
Intelligence, BofA Fund Manager Survey cash levels, put/call ratios, market internals)
as a contrarian buy signal — and noted inflation-driven bear markets average ~28-34%
drawdowns, not the 50% everyone anchors on.
