# Source: Energy / The Cost of the Crisis — Macro Insiders (Nov 2022)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **28 November 2022** (extract `_Modified:` 2022-12-02). Author: Julian
Brigden, Macro Insiders Deep Dive ([[global-macro-investor]] / Real Vision), with
energy specialist Alexander Stahel.

## Key Findings

- Europe's energy vulnerability traced to a decade of inaction after Russia's 2014
  Crimea seizure — failure to diversify gas supply/storage while pursuing carbon-free
  goals *and* cutting nuclear capacity (deliberate, plus French underinvestment).
- The 2022 full-scale invasion brought "all the chickens home to roost." Energy is a
  heterogeneous market — different products (nat gas, LNG, petrol, diesel, crude, jet
  fuel) and large regional disparities (US gas ~1/5 the European price).
- The piece quantifies the crisis cost as % of GDP and in trillions of USD, and
  identifies regional winners/losers.
- Macro conclusion: fragmented energy markets and infrastructure portend volatility —
  a harbinger of wider risk premia.

## Relevance

A geopolitics/energy deep-dive overlapping the [[globalisation-reversal]] megatrend
(energy security, fragmentation) and the volatility theme.
