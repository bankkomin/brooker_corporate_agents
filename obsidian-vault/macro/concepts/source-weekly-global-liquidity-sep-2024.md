# Source: Weekly Global Liquidity Update — CrossBorder Capital (9 Sep 2024)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **9 September 2024** (extract `_Modified:` 2024-09-11). Publisher:
[[crossborder-capital]] — the *Weekly Global Liquidity Update*.

## Key Findings

- Global liquidity growth gained traction through Q3 — the main driver again **rising
  collateral values (bonds)**, bolstered by encouraging US inflation data and signs of
  a slowing global (especially US) economy.
- Liquidity rose ~US$572bn in the week to **US$177.31tr**, a new all-time high (it
  ended 2023 at US$171.2tr, troughed ~US$169.2tr in late April, and has since expanded
  by ~US$7.65tr).
- Growth rates: ~18.8% (3-month annualised), ~7.2% (YoY).
- Central bank liquidity growth remains patchy (PBoC injecting on-and-off; the Fed
  holding back). CrossBorder reaffirms 2H 2024 better than 1H, with the cycle expanding
  toward a **late-2025 peak**.

## Relevance

A point-in-time data installment of the [[global-liquidity-cycle]]; reaffirms the
