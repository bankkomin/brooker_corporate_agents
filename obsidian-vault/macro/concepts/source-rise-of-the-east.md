# Source: Rise of the East (ARP Megatrend #4)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **13 January 2020** (extract `_Modified:` 2023-01-10; document dated
internally). Author: Niels C. Jensen, [[absolute-return-partners]]. The review paper
for megatrend #4 of [[the-seven-megatrends]].

## Key Findings

- Economic and overall power is shifting structurally from West to East, led by China.
  Chinese GDP grew tenfold in the 26 years to the 2008 crisis.
- Power has four game-theory sources (economic power, risk tolerance, threat power,
  coalition power); China is rising on all four, the West declining.
- China likely outgrows the US on a nominal USD basis ~11 years out (early 2030s,
  base case); already larger on a PPP basis. India and Indonesia rise too.
- A food-and-water crisis looms: humanity must produce more food by 2050 than in the
  prior 10,000 years; agriculture uses ~60% of freshwater.
- Flip side: China's growth is one-dimensional (capex ~45% of GDP), total debt ~250%
  of GDP, weak demographics, a closed current account.

## Relevance

The canonical statement of the [[rise-of-the-east]] thesis. Predates the
Russia-Ukraine war; complements [[globalisation-reversal]] and [[demographics-as-destiny]].
