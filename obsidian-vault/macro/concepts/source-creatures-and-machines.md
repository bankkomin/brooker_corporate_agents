# Source: Creatures and Machines — The Exponentialist (Jul 2024)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **July 2024** (extract `_Modified:` 2024-07-19). Author: David Mattin, Real
Vision's *The Exponentialist* monthly.

## Key Findings

- The conservative-vs-progressive political framework, born of the French Revolution,
  has been "scrambled" by permanent technological revolution — and is being replaced
  by **accelerate vs decelerate**.
- Accelerationists (Marc Andreessen's *Techno-Optimist Manifesto*, the e/acc movement)
  vs decelerationists (degrowth, AI-doom). The divide cuts across old party lines and
  appears across energy, finance/blockchain and AI.
- The endgame: **augmented vs organic humans** — those who merge with machine
  intelligence via brain-machine interfaces vs those who refuse. Mattin invokes Isaiah
  Berlin's value pluralism and calls for a "post-liberalism" / "multi-speed modernity."
- *Exponential News*: Goldman Sachs' sceptical *Gen AI: Too Much Spend, Too Little
  Benefit?*; Eton banning smartphones; OpenAI blocking Chinese users — pointing to two
  great AI systems (Global North vs China). Charts: AI inferencing ~100-1,000x more
  compute-intensive than email/search; solar capacity doubling every ~3 years; fusion
  funding going exponential.
