# Source: Global Liquidity — Cycles, Trends and Asset Allocation (CrossBorder, Sep 2024)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **September 2024** (extract `_Modified:` 2024-09-11). Publisher:
[[crossborder-capital]] — a flagship *Global View* thematic note.

## Key Findings

- The most complete statement of the [[global-liquidity-cycle]] framework: the cycle
  bottomed October 2022 and is slated to **peak in late 2025**.
- Three cycle drivers: the **Fed**, the **PBoC**, and the **MOVE index** (which sets
  repo collateral haircuts; the collateral multiplier was ~1.53x). The MOVE index is
  argued to be more important ex ante than the equity VIX.
- Markets are "vast debt-refinancing mechanisms" — ~3 of 4 deals are refinancings.
  Wholesale banks run a *collateral multiplier*, placing Treasuries at the centre of
  the monetary system.
- The long-run **[[monetary-inflation]]** trend: liquidity must rise ~6-8% p.a.,
  doubling every decade. US debt (~US$35tn, ~6-yr maturity) needs ~US$6tn rolled
  annually plus ~US$2tn new issuance. A stealth YCC is underway (bill issuance ~22% of
  debt, shorter tenors cut the 10-yr yield ~80-100bp).
- The stable debt/liquidity ratio (~2.5x stability threshold for Advanced Economies).
- **Conclusion**: the 60:40 portfolio is obsolete — replace ~20pp of bonds with gold,
  TIPS and potentially Bitcoin, plus pricing-power equities.
