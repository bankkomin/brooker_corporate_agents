# Source: Will the Bank of Japan Break the Market? — RV (Jun 2022)

## Document Overview

As-of date: **22 June 2022** (extract `_Modified:` 2022-06-23). An "Inside the Episode"
Real Vision transcript with Roger Hirst, reviewing Insider Talks between Julian Brigden
and Mike Taylor.

## Key Findings

- The Bank of Japan doubled down on bond buying to defend its 10-year yield cap. The
  yen should fall further, which could force China to allow yuan depreciation to stay
  competitive.
- If the BoJ's commitment wavers, rising Japanese yields would push global yields
  higher and USDJPY could gap lower.
- The ECB faces a parallel bind — tighter policy while needing to cap Italian yields.
- Commodities and consumers are showing the first signs of demand destruction.

## Relevance

An early case study in **yield curve control** and its global spillovers — relevant to
[[us-sovereign-debt-risk]] (YCC) and [[dollar-dynamics]] (the yen/yuan chain).
