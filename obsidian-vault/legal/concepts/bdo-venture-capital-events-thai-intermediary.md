# BDO Venture-Capital Events & the Thai Intermediary Collaboration Scheme

## Summary

The **Business Development Officer ("BDO") of [[brooker-group|BROOK]]** may engage in
communications with venture-capital managers of the funds that FOF invests in — by setting
up **physical events in Thailand** and organising for those VC managers to speak at the
events and introduce portfolio companies that seek to provide services in Thailand. The
Timblick & Partners opinion of 29 December 2025 concludes these activities present a
**relatively low risk** of being regarded as a restricted offering of securities under the
SEC Act — provided they comply with the [[reverse-solicitation-thai-sec]] limits **and**
the additional collaboration-scheme conditions below.

## Conditions for the Low-Risk Conclusion

The BDO's activities must satisfy the **same grounds and limitations** as the Head of
Investor Relations (see [[reverse-solicitation-thai-sec]]) **and, further** (Opinion para
4(g)):

1. **Thai securities-company intermediary.** The activities are engaged **through a Thai
   securities company** acting as collaborator or intermediary, which holds the relevant
   SEC licences to act as a Thai intermediary.
2. **Advance SEC registration.** Any Thai intermediary intending to invite an **offshore
   financial institution** (e.g. a product specialist or fund manager) must **register**
   with the SEC, in advance, the details of that offshore financial institution and the
   information of the relevant offshore personnel.
3. **Limited communication & qualifications.** The method of communication by the offshore
   financial institution must be **limited to the relevant permitted attendees**, and the
   offshore personnel involved must possess **all qualifications prescribed by the SEC**.

## The Regulatory Collaboration Scheme

These conditions reflect the **Notification of the Capital Market Supervisory Board No.
TorThor. 1/2560** re: Provision of Services to Clients regarding Investment in Capital
Market Products Denominated in Foreign Currency — as amended by **Notification No. TorThor.
41/2566** — which establishes a **collaboration scheme between Thai Intermediaries and
offshore financial institutions** (including the fund or investment manager as the offshore
securities issuer/offeror) for offering offshore foreign-currency-denominated securities to
investors based in Thailand.

## No PE Created

As with the Head of Investor Relations, the opinion concludes (Opinion para 4(h)) the
BDO's activities are of **purely preparatory or auxiliary character** and therefore
**shall not constitute a PE** under **Article 5(4e)** of the DTA; FOF's profits are then
not taxable in Thailand under Section 66 paragraph 2 and Section 76 bis of the Revenue Code
and Article 7 of the DTA. See [[permanent-establishment-dta-thailand-singapore]].

## Agent Notes

Unlike the IR pathway, the BDO pathway is **not self-executing**: it requires an actual
licensed Thai intermediary and advance SEC registration of the offshore institution and
personnel. Events organised without that intermediary/registration fall outside the
low-risk conclusion.
