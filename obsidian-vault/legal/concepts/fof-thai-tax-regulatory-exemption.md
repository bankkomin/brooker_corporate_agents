# FOF — Thai SEC Act & Corporate Income Tax Exemption

## Summary

The Timblick & Partners legal opinion of 29 December 2025 concludes that **Brook Limited
Partners Fund of Funds I ("FOF")** — a fund incorporated by [[brook-technology-capital-vcc]]
(BTC) in Singapore — is **not subject to the Thai Securities and Exchange Act 1992
("SEC Act") nor to Thai corporate income tax**, save for withholding tax (final tax)
deductible at source by the payer of income in Thailand. See
[[withholding-tax-foreign-fund-thailand]] for the WHT carve-out.

This is the core conclusion of the opinion. It is conditional on the factual assumptions
holding true and is subject to the qualifications recorded in
[[2025-12-29-thai-regulatory-tax-opinion]].

## The Grounds for Exemption

The opinion bases the conclusion on six grounds (Opinion para 4(a)):

| # | Ground |
|---|--------|
| 1 | FOF is a **foreign juristic person** and not incorporated in Thailand. |
| 2 | FOF has **no branch, office or other fixed place of business** in which business is wholly or partly carried on in Thailand. |
| 3 | FOF's **sole business** is to buy and sell securities of listed companies, including securities listed on the Stock Exchange of Thailand. |
| 4 | FOF carries on **no business in Thailand** and has no employee, agent or go-between of dependent status in Thailand carrying on business for/on behalf of FOF and thereby deriving income in or from Thailand. |
| 5 | FOF is **not, nor deemed to be, carrying on business in Thailand** under the SEC Act or the Thai Revenue Code. |
| 6 | **No overseas-sourced income** earned by FOF is remitted into Thailand. |

## Legal Basis

- **Section 76 bis, Revenue Code** — a foreign-incorporated company with an employee, agent
  or go-between carrying on business in Thailand is *deemed* to carry on business in
  Thailand; that person becomes its representative with a duty to file returns and pay tax.
  Grounds 4–5 are designed to fall outside this deeming rule.
- **Section 66 paragraph 2, Revenue Code** — taxes net profits of a foreign company on
  business carried on in Thailand.
- **SEC Act 1992** — governs the issuance and offering of securities in Thailand;
  "securities" under Section 4 includes shares and investment units.

The opinion also relies on **Thai Supreme Court Ruling No. 483/2546** and **Board of
Taxation Ruling No. 2/2526**.

## Key Conditions / Thresholds

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Place of business | No fixed place of business / PE in Thailand — see [[permanent-establishment-dta-thailand-singapore]] |
| Dependent agent | No dependent-status agent or go-between in Thailand acting for FOF |
| Income remittance | No overseas-sourced income remitted into Thailand |
| Onshore activity | Onshore activity must remain preparatory/auxiliary — see [[thai-resident-director-pe-risk]] |

## Agent Notes

This exemption is **not a binding ruling**. The opinion expressly states it is not an
advance tax ruling or regulatory confirmation, and the Revenue Department / SEC may take a
different view on audit. Any change in FOF's actual onshore conduct can change the outcome.
