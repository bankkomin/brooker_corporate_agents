# Withholding Tax on Foreign Funds — Section 70 Revenue Code

## Summary

The Timblick & Partners opinion of 29 December 2025 confirms that, although FOF is
otherwise exempt from the SEC Act and Thai corporate income tax (see
[[fof-thai-tax-regulatory-exemption]]), it **remains liable for Thai withholding tax (a
final tax) deducted at source** by the payer of income in Thailand. This is the **single
tax carve-out** to the exemption.

## Legal Basis — Section 70 Revenue Code

**Section 70** of the Revenue Code provides that a company incorporated under foreign laws
and **not carrying on business in Thailand**, but receiving assessable income under
**Section 40(4)(b) and (g)** — i.e. **dividends** and **gains derived from the transfer of
shares** — which is paid from or in Thailand, shall be liable to pay tax.

## The Withholding Mechanism

| Income type | WHT rate | Notes |
|-------------|----------|-------|
| Dividend (Section 40(4)(b)) | **10%** | Deducted at source by the payer |
| Capital gain on transfer of shares (Section 40(4)(g)) | **15%** | Deducted at source by the payer |

- The rates apply **unless otherwise exempt under a double tax treaty**.
- The **payer of income** must deduct the corporate income tax from the assessable income
  and **remit it to the local Amphur office**, together with a tax return in the
  Director-General's prescribed form, **within 7 days from the last day of the month** in
  which the income is paid.
- This is a **final tax** — it discharges the foreign fund's Thai tax liability on that
  income; FOF itself has no filing obligation for it.

## Why It Matters

Section 70 WHT operates **independently of PE analysis**. Even where FOF has no PE in
Thailand (see [[permanent-establishment-dta-thailand-singapore]]) and is not "carrying on
business" in Thailand, Thai-sourced dividends and share-transfer gains are still taxed at
source. The exemption in [[fof-thai-tax-regulatory-exemption]] is therefore expressly
stated as "save for withholding tax (final tax) deductible at source".
