# Thai-Resident Director — Regulatory & PE Risk

## Summary

One of BTC's two directors is also a director of [[brooker-group|BROOK]] and is **resident
in Thailand**. The Timblick & Partners opinion of 29 December 2025 assesses whether that
director's residency and activities cause FOF to be regarded as carrying on restricted
securities business, or carrying on business for Thai tax purposes. The opinion concludes
**residency alone does not** trigger either — but flags **day-to-day management** by that
director as the primary residual risk.

## Conclusion 1 — No Restricted Securities Activity (SEC Act)

The director's residency does **not** make FOF regarded as carrying out restricted
offering-of-securities activities under the SEC Act, on the basis that (Opinion para 4(b)):

- The Thai-resident director does **not** solicit or make offers in respect of any
  investment in FOF to persons in Thailand, exercise authority to negotiate/conclude
  contracts, make investment decisions, or perform other acts for/on behalf of FOF in
  Thailand.
- FOF has **delegated all investment management and administration** to
  [[timblick-and-partners|Ternary]] and other third-party administrators under the
  investment management agreement and administration agreement; FOF's directors therefore
  have no functions or duties in Thailand except overall supervision via attendance at
  board meetings held **offshore**.
- The directors of BTC have **independent status**: they are not appointed on an exclusive
  basis and no appointment restriction prohibits them from acting as director of other
  companies in Thailand or elsewhere.

## Conclusion 2 — No "Carrying on Business" for Tax (Section 76 bis)

The residency of the director who performs the activities described above does **not**
result in FOF being regarded as carrying on business in Thailand from a tax perspective,
and therefore FOF is **not** subject to the obligation to file returns and pay tax under
**Section 76 bis** of the Revenue Code (Opinion para 4(c)).

## The Residual Risk — Day-to-Day Management = PE

The opinion's strongest warning (Opinion para 4(d)): the **place of effective management**
may constitute a PE under **Article 5(2a)** of the DTA, and FOF's profits may then be
taxable in Thailand under **Section 66 paragraph 2** of the Revenue Code and **Article 7**
of the DTA — see [[permanent-establishment-dta-thailand-singapore]].

Day-to-day management by BTC's Thai-resident director for FOF in Thailand — including:

- exercising **signing authority over bank accounts**;
- entering into **engagements of third-party service providers**; or
- other activities involving **decision-making, contract negotiation/execution, or
  operational management** in Thailand

— could give rise to Thai regulatory or taxation implications. **The greater the extent of
onshore activity, the higher the risk.**

## Risk-Mitigation Measure

As an express risk-mitigation measure, the opinion recommends:

> Consideration should be given to **assigning signing authority to a person who is not
> resident in Thailand**, and ensuring that authority is **exercised outside Thailand**.
