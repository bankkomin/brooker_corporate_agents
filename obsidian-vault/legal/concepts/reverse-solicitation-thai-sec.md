# Reverse Solicitation — Thai SEC Treatment

## Summary

The **Head of Investor Relations of [[brooker-group|BROOK]]** may engage in **reverse
solicitation** activities in respect of FOF in Thailand and make referrals to
[[timblick-and-partners|Ternary]]. The Timblick & Partners opinion of 29 December 2025
concludes these activities present a **relatively low risk** of being regarded as a
restricted offering of securities under the SEC Act — provided strict conditions are met.

## What Reverse Solicitation Is — and the SEC's Position

The SEC **does not formally recognise reverse solicitation as a specific regulatory
exemption** under the SEC Act. Thai market participants nonetheless treat it as a
**practical exemption based on market practice** / a risk-mitigation mechanism. Critically:

> There remains a **burden of proof on the fund** to demonstrate that the investors'
> request was genuinely **unsolicited and made at their own exclusive initiative**.

The opinion is therefore subject to the risk that the SEC may, on its supervisory or
enforcement discretion, take a different view of the actual conduct.

## Conditions for the Low-Risk Conclusion

The low-risk conclusion holds only if the Head of Investor Relations observes all of the
following limits (Opinion para 4(f)):

| Area | Permitted | Prohibited |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Marketing | Strictly **informational / educational** meetings and group presentations, initiated solely for informational purposes; general factual information on the fund, market trends, industry developments, regulatory updates, investment philosophy | Marketing presentations, roadshows, seminars, suitcasing, outboarding (in-person, virtual or hybrid) that actively promote investment in FOF |
| Content | General factual content only | Referencing specific investment terms, fees, returns, or fund performance data |
| Distribution | **Passive**, non-targeted distribution of general literature/brochures (e.g. newsletters), strictly informational, **solely in a foreign language**, no call to action | Fund-initiated outreach — cold calls, targeted emails, one-on-one meetings; distributing pitchbooks, subscription forms; QR codes / links / contact details directing to investment-terms material |
| Subscriptions | Receiving basic prospective-investor information **solely to enable** a sale/subscription to be conducted, negotiated and completed **outside Thailand** | Soliciting, accepting or processing any subscription, payment, commitment, or execution of transaction documents in Thailand |

## No PE Created

The opinion concludes (Opinion para 4(h)) that the activities of the Head of Investor
Relations are of **purely preparatory or auxiliary character** and therefore **shall not
constitute a PE** under **Article 5(4e)** of the DTA; FOF's profits are then not taxable in
Thailand under Section 66 paragraph 2 and Section 76 bis of the Revenue Code and Article 7
of the DTA. See [[permanent-establishment-dta-thailand-singapore]].

## No Recognised Procedure

The opinion notes there are **no specific criteria or procedural steps** recognised in Thai
market practice for establishing a reverse solicitation arrangement. Measures borrowed from
other jurisdictions include: requiring Thai investors to initiate contact by emailing/
calling a designated **offshore** contact (e.g. the offshore administrator); conducting all
subsequent communications offshore; the offshore representative handling all documentation
and the subscription process offshore; keeping an **audit trail** of communications; and
obtaining a **written acknowledgement** from investors that they initiated contact without
prior solicitation — with **no Thai-based representative** involved in the process.
