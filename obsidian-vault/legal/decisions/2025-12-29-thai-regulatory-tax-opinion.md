# Legal Opinion — Thai Regulatory & Tax Advice (29 Dec 2025)

## Summary

[[timblick-and-partners|Timblick & Partners Limited]] delivered a formal **legal opinion**
to [[brooker-group|Brooker Group PCL]], dated **29 December 2025**, addressing whether the
presence and activities of certain BROOK personnel in Thailand expose **Brook Limited
Partners Fund of Funds I ("FOF")** — a Singapore fund of [[brook-technology-capital-vcc]] —
to Thai securities law (SEC Act) and Thai taxation. This article records the opinion as a
legal-advice decision record; the substance is decomposed into the linked concept articles.

## Instruction & Scope

Brooker instructed the firm to opine on the application of Thai securities and tax laws
arising from foreign-entity personnel present in Thailand. Three factual scenarios were
assessed:

1. One of BTC's two directors is also a director of BROOK and **resident in Thailand**.
2. The **Head of Investor Relations** of BROOK may engage in **reverse solicitation** for
   FOF in Thailand and make referrals to Ternary.
3. The **Business Development Officer** of BROOK may engage with VC managers of FOF's
   underlying funds by setting up **physical events in Thailand**.

Scope was **limited** to whether this presence/activity causes FOF to be regarded as
carrying on restricted business in Thailand. It excluded the overall fund structure, any
unrelated legal/regulatory/tax issues, non-Thailand matters, and any due diligence.

## Conclusions

| Topic | Conclusion | Article |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| FOF & SEC Act / corporate income tax | **Not subject**, save final WHT at source | [[fof-thai-tax-regulatory-exemption]] |
| Withholding tax | Section 70 WHT applies (10% dividend / 15% share-transfer gain) | [[withholding-tax-foreign-fund-thailand]] |
| Thai-resident director — SEC Act | Residency does **not** create restricted securities activity | [[thai-resident-director-pe-risk]] |
| Thai-resident director — Section 76 bis | Does **not** cause FOF to be "carrying on business" for tax | [[thai-resident-director-pe-risk]] |
| Place of effective management | **Material PE risk** if day-to-day management is onshore | [[permanent-establishment-dta-thailand-singapore]] |
| Head of Investor Relations | **Low risk** if strict reverse-solicitation limits observed | [[reverse-solicitation-thai-sec]] |
| Business Development Officer | **Low risk** if conducted via a licensed Thai intermediary with SEC registration | [[bdo-venture-capital-events-thai-intermediary]] |
| IR & BDO activities & PE | Preparatory/auxiliary — **no PE** under Article 5(4e) DTA | [[permanent-establishment-dta-thailand-singapore]] |

## Key Risk-Mitigation Recommendation

Assign **bank-account signing authority** for FOF to a person **not resident in Thailand**
and ensure it is **exercised outside Thailand**, to reduce place-of-effective-management /
PE exposure. See [[thai-resident-director-pe-risk]].

## Assumptions & Qualifications

The opinion relies (without investigation) on the **accuracy and completeness** of the
factual representations made to the firm, on the absence of any non-Thai law implication,
and on there being no undisclosed amendments. It is given under Thai law in force at its
date, relying on email correspondence up to 8 December 2025 only.

It is **expressly qualified**:

- It is **not a binding ruling, advance tax ruling or regulatory confirmation** from the
  Revenue Department or SEC; there is no assurance either authority will agree on audit.
- The Revenue Department and SEC have **broad discretion**, may apply a **low threshold**,
  and consider **substance over form**; any variation in actual conduct may change the
  outcome.
- The SEC does **not formally recognise reverse solicitation** as an exemption; the burden
  of proof remains on the fund.
