# Khao Yai Strategic Retreat — 21 Feb 2026

**Date:** 21 February 2026, 10:00 · Khao Yai, Thailand
**Context:** Second full strategic retreat in ~5 years.
**Source:** `BOD Khao Yai Retreat Pack 2026.docx`
**Deck:** `Brook Strategic Retreat Khao Yai 2026.pptx` (36 slides)

## Attendees

Board of Directors and Management, Brooker Group PCL. The individual attendee list is not
recorded in the retreat pack; the formal resolutions are attributed to the "Board of
Directors, Brooker Group Public Company Limited."

## Purpose

Align Board and Management on (1) a focused strategy, (2) a disciplined capital and
liquidity doctrine for a more chaotic global environment, (3) a regulatory-safe operating
model, and (4) a relationship/counterparty plan that increases execution speed and
resilience.

**Expected outputs:** new Vision/Mission; adopt the Three-Engine model; adopt the Sell-Down
and Melt-Up plans; adopt the Funding Diversification plan; approve prediction-market AI
trading after Q1; approve the new org chart, chartered committees, and Decision Rights
Matrix; approve the Key Partners & Counterparties map; approve the 12-month OKRs; approve
the equity story & KPI packs.

## Session 1 — Opening: The North Star (2026–2028)

Defined Brooker's "why" and the 36-month future state: **Southeast Asia's Institutional
Innovation Machine** — the gateway for corporates, family offices, and global accredited
investors to access the next regime of technology investment products. The firm "no longer
holds assets — it monetizes its knowledge, network, and access into fee platforms."

Three pillars of success: **Integrity** (regulatory safe harbor), **Sovereignty** (the
"Stay Liquid" moat), **Cashflow** (the fee-income flywheel). The session also fixed
Brooker's positioning — it is *not* a speculative trading house, passive investment
vehicle, macro hedge fund, technology incubator, or diversified conglomerate; it *is* a
capital structuring platform converting innovation exposure into recurring fee income.
Every future initiative must increase recurring fee income, strengthen treasury
optionality, or enhance institutional positioning — or it will not be pursued.

→ Full detail: [[north-star-2028]].

## Session 2 — 5-Year Retrospective: The Brutal Truth

A "radical honesty" review of 2021–2025. The period was defined by "Innovation
Volatility" — the LUNA/FTX contagion, "Regulation by Enforcement," the AI talent/capital
drain, and a weak Thai economy. Strategic missteps: over-diversification into
low-conviction assets (IREC/carbon, IP-NFTs, Robinhood), the "paper gains" trap (BNB/ICO
yields never sold due to the investment-company grace period), bad loans on unenforceable
collateral, and a lack of compounding. What held firm: Bitcoin resilience, blue-chip
concentration (BNB reached ~$1,300, ~4x), stablecoin utility, and a capable R&D team.

→ Full detail: [[five-year-retrospective-2021-2025]].

## Session 3 — Macro & Innovation Regime Alignment

Set the macro backdrop for 2026–2030: **compulsory monetary debasement** driven by a ~$40
trillion debt-maturity wall, and **physical scarcity** driven by the ~$85 trillion AI
infrastructure buildout. Global M2 at a record $137 trillion; 320+ rate cuts in 24
months; US QT terminated 1 Dec 2025. 2026 is a lateral "Strategic Accumulation Window,"
not an appreciation year. Macro scenario odds: **DA Melt-Up 40% · Sideways Accumulation
35% · Liquidity Contraction 25%.**

→ Full detail: [[macro-innovation-regime-2026]].

## Session 4 — Selection of the 3 Priority Engines

Adopted the **Three-Engine model** — two Operating Engines (VCC Platform, Advisory) for
recurring fee/service income and one Strategic Capital Engine (Digital Asset Treasury) for
alpha and native yield; also framed as PRODUCT, SERVICE, VALUE. Detailed the VCC vehicles
(VC Fund of Funds, 14% Yield Fund of Funds) with fee economics, the Advisory service
suite, and the DAT yield mechanisms. The "Institutional Flywheel": DAT seeds VCC, VCC
feeds Advisory deal flow, Advisory surfaces new products, VCC co-investments accelerate
DAT. Capital-allocation guardrail: non-core investments must not exceed 10% of total
assets.

→ Full detail: [[three-engine-model]]. Resolution: [[2026-02-21-three-engine-model-adoption]].

## Session 5 — R&D: Prediction Markets

First-mover, innovative profit generation through AI-driven prediction-market arbitrage —
low-drawdown algorithmic trading on venues such as Polymarket and Forecast. Approved for
deployment up to **THB 10M after 31 March 2026**, with a mandatory circuit breaker at a
15% drawdown.

→ Resolution: [[2026-02-21-prediction-market-ai-trading]] (R-04).

## Session 6 — Branding the Narrative: The "Vessel" Framework

Installed the **Vessel** — a repeatable system that converts Brooker's polymathic
curiosity into high-signal market outcomes, solving the firm's biggest risk:
fragmentation ("shiny object syndrome"). Positioned Brooker as a **Sovereign Generalist**;
the conversion sequence Brand → Content → Product; a three-part flywheel of Insight →
Distribution → Monetization; 7 Signature Theses; a 12-week installation plan; 4 System
Products; and governance rules (the Attachment Rule, the 90-day Hard Kill criteria).

→ Full detail: [[brooker-vessel-framework]].

## Session 7 — The "Stay Liquid" Doctrine

Established **Liquidity Sovereignty**: in a chaotic regime, survival depends on
friction-free liquidity, not book value. A dual-track profile — on-chain sovereignty
(permissionless reserves) and institutional stability (THB banking with SCB/BBL).
Approved a symbolic **100 BTC Sovereignty Buffer** as permissionless collateral; the
on-chain funding stack (Aave, Compound, Sky, Morpho/Spark); a four-level Liquidity Ladder;
a credit-rating strategy (target BBB+); and multi-sig custody governance.

→ Full detail: [[stay-liquid-doctrine]]. Resolution: [[2026-02-21-capital-sovereignty-resolution]] (R-03).

## Session 8 — Regulatory Design & Structural Roadmap

Solved the **40% Investment-Company rule**: Brooker is currently non-compliant at ~50%
(THB 1,728M of THB 3,476M total assets). The Singapore **VCC** is the structural "vent" —
moving assets from the direct balance sheet into managed fund structures converts
Investment Assets into Fund NAV with outside capital. The Melt-Up Divestment Plan
(3 phases) targets a managed **35% ratio**; the remaining 60%+ goes to operations capex,
loans, active investments, and private credit (e.g. film receivables, ~9% target, zero
defaults in 55 films). Compliance protocol: the "Safe Harbor" — technical-services
documentation, accredited-investor gatekeeping.

→ Full detail: [[investment-company-40-percent-rule]]. Resolution: [[2026-02-21-melt-up-divestment-plan]] (R-02).

## Session 9 — Operating Model & Decision Rights

Institutionalised the **Brooker Operating System**: a governance-led model replacing
reliance on key individuals. Chartered **nine committees** — five Board-level (IC, RC, AC,
NRC, **CGSC**) and four management (EXCOM, LC, CAC, OPC) — each with a full mandate and
key functions. The **Corporate Governance & Sustainability Committee (CGSC)** carries the
ESG / sustainability / anti-corruption mandate. Also adopted: the Decision Rights Matrix,
the next-stage org chart and talent bench, the execution cadence (weekly/monthly/quarterly/
annual), and modernised incentives (risk-adjusted metrics, clawback provisions, ESOP, the
"Elon Moon Shot").

→ Full detail: [[2026-02-21-committee-governance-structure]] (R-05) and [[esg-sustainability-governance]].

## Session 10 — Risk Pre-Mortem: "Project Failure"

A **pre-mortem**: the Board imagined a failed firm on 21 Feb 2028 and worked backward to
find blind spots. Six failure modes were identified, each with a mitigation:

1. Regulatory re-classification trap → independent VCC investment committees
2. Liquidity & margin spiral → hard 25% DTV cap; 6 months offline THB cash
3. Custody & counterparty breach → "No Re-hypothecation"; third-party audits
4. The "HODL" trap → non-discretionary Board-triggered sell orders
5. Talent & execution gap → specialist headhunter; shadow-leadership program
6. Structured loan blowup → rapid restructuring; balance-sheet strengthening

Board "defensive wall" prompts: the asset-loss "Kill Switch," the custody audit, and
willingness to vote "No" on a melt-up extension.

→ Full detail: [[risk-pre-mortem-feb-2028]] and the six [[failure-mode-regulatory-reclassification|failure-mode]] articles.

## Session 11 — The 12-Month OKRs & Budget

Established **five 2026 OKRs**: Revenue Transformation, Structural & Regulatory
Re-alignment, Institutional-Grade Treasury, Talent Bench & Org Design, and Market
Narrative & IR. Approved the 2026 budget (~THB 100M total): Talent Acquisition THB 10M,
Strategic Seeding USD 21M (rebalance investment), Operational Infrastructure THB 90M. The
operating budget totals THB 95.7M (±10%). Four dashboard "North Star" metrics: NAV per
share, AUM growth, cost of funds vs. ROE, engagement score.

→ Full detail: [[2026-02-21-2026-okrs]] (R-07) and [[2026-budget]].
