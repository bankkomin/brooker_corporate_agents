# Failure Mode 1 — The Regulatory Re-Classification Trap

Failure mode 1 of 6 from the [[risk-pre-mortem-feb-2028|Khao Yai Risk Pre-Mortem]].

## What Happened (Hypothetical, Feb 2028)

Despite Brooker's VCC structures, the Thai SEC ruled that the firm remained an
"Investment Company" *in substance*.

## The Blind Spot

Management focused on the **legal form** of the VCCs but failed to maintain **operational
substance**. It was deemed to be exercising too much direct control over fund assets,
triggering the **"look-through" principle**.

## The Consequence

A forced liquidation of assets at the bottom of a market cycle to meet the
[[investment-company-40-percent-rule|40% rule]], producing massive realized losses and a
shareholder revolt.

## Pre-Mortem Mitigation

- Ensure **clear, independent investment committees for each VCC**.
- Strictly document the **"Manager vs. Owner"** relationship.
- Use **outside money** to grow, and focus on **foreign overseas accredited investors** so
  the VCC NAV is genuinely external capital, not Brooker's balance sheet in disguise.
