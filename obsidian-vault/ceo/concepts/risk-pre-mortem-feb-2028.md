# Risk Pre-Mortem — Hypothetical Project Failure (Feb 2028)

## Summary

Session 10 of the Khao Yai retreat was a **pre-mortem exercise**: the Board imagined it is
21 February 2028 — two years on — and that the Three-Engine strategy has *failed*. Share
price at an all-time low, AUM fled the VCC platforms, the DA Treasury decimated. Working
backward from that failure, the retreat identified **six "silent killers"** in the current
plan and a mitigation for each. This article is the parent index; each failure mode has
its own article.

## The Exercise — Prospective Hindsight

The premise: "Why did we fail?" Reasoning backward from a concrete, vivid failure surfaces
blind spots that forward-looking optimism hides. The output is not a forecast but a
**Survival Manual** — failure in 2028 is judged most likely to come from *disciplined
greed, regulatory slippage, or liquidity arrogance*, not bad luck.

## Board Discussion Prompts — "The Defensive Wall"

The session closed with three unresolved questions for the Board:

- **The "Kill Switch":** at what level of asset loss (x%) does the Board automatically
  take over all management decision rights?
- **The Custody Audit:** are we 100% certain the "Sovereignty Buffer" is held in a way
  that is immune to counterparty bankruptcy?
- **The "No" Vote:** is the Board prepared to vote "No" on a melt-up extension if
  management asks to delay the monetization triggers?

## Why It Matters

The pre-mortem converts optimism into hard guardrails. Several retreat resolutions are
direct mitigations — the non-discretionary sell triggers ([[2026-02-21-melt-up-divestment-plan]]),
the DTV cap ([[stay-liquid-doctrine]]), and the committee structure
([[2026-02-21-committee-governance-structure]]) all exist to defuse a specific failure
mode below.
