# The Three-Engine Strategic Model

## Summary

The Three-Engine Model is Brooker Group's primary operating architecture, adopted at the
Khao Yai retreat (see [[2026-02-21-three-engine-model-adoption]]). It reframes the firm
from an opportunistic, balance-sheet-driven holding company into a systematic, repeatable
revenue machine built on two **Operating Engines** (recurring fees) and one **Strategic
Capital Engine** (yield + capital gains). The engines are also described as PRODUCT,
SERVICE, and VALUE engines, and must feed each other rather than operate as silos.

## Definition

| Engine | Type | Description |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Engine 1 — VCC Platform | Product | Asset-management product factory. VC Fund of Funds and the 14% Yield Fund of Funds, generating recurring management + performance fees. See [[brook-limited-partners-fof]], [[yield-fund-of-funds]]. |
| Engine 2 — Advisory | Service | Corporate & Family Office "Business & Tech Integration" advisory: stablecoin settlement, RWA/tokenization, DA treasury setup, AI implementation. High-margin (>40%) service income. |
| Engine 3 — Digital Asset Treasury | Value | Strategic treasury managed as a disciplined, high-alpha portfolio generating native yield (staking, options, launchpool, on-chain lending) and melt-up capital gains. |

## How It Works — The Institutional Flywheel

The engines are designed to compound into each other:

- **DAT → VCC (the Anchor):** the $11.2M GP commitment from treasury seeds the
  [[brook-limited-partners-fof]], providing social proof for external LPs.
- **VCC → Advisory (the Deal Flow):** investing in 20–30 top global VC funds gives a
  front-row seat to 500+ portfolio companies, feeding the Advisory pipeline.
- **Advisory → VCC (the Alpha):** advisory client problems surface new product ideas.
- **VCC → DAT (the Tail Flow):** co-investment opportunities accelerate the treasury.

## Why It Matters

The model converts balance-sheet volatility into a "Fee Machine," targeting an
earnings-multiple valuation rather than a discount-to-NAV. It is the structural answer to
the [[investment-company-40-percent-rule]] and the route to the [[north-star-2028]]
targets.

## Key Metrics / Thresholds

| Engine | Capital allocation range | Target return | Risk profile |
|--------|--------------------------|---------------|--------------|
| Engine 1 — VCC Platform | 15% (GP stakes + seed) | 15–20% IRR | Moderate |
| Engine 2 — Advisory & Loan | 10–30% of capital | 40%+ margin | Low |
| Engine 3 — DA Treasury | 20% of total assets | ~10% native yield | High |
| Liquidity Buffer | Min. 6 months operating burn | Capital preservation | Very Low |

Capital allocation guardrail: non-core investments must not exceed 10% of total assets.
