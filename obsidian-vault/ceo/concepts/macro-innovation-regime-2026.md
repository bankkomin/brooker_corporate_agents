# Macro & Innovation Regime 2026–2030 — Compulsory Debasement

## Summary

Session 3 of the Khao Yai retreat set the macroeconomic backdrop for the strategy: the
2026–2030 period will be defined by **compulsory monetary debasement** and the **physical
scarcity** necessitated by the AI buildout. Through a process of elimination, software
abundance and fiat holdings are de-emphasised in favour of physical scarcity, digital
scarcity, and systematic yield. This is the forward-looking macro thesis underpinning the
[[three-engine-model]] and the [[2026-02-21-melt-up-divestment-plan]]; it operationalises
the theses in [[brooker-worldview-2026]].

## The Architecture of Global Liquidity — The Quinquennial Cycle

Global liquidity runs a 5–6 year cycle. The current cycle began at the 2022 nadir and has
reached significant maturation.

- **Inflection point of monetary velocity:** liquidity momentum is decelerating — not
  merely from restrictive policy, but because a robust real economy (the AI capex cycle)
  is extracting monetary surplus from financial markets into capital-intensive industrial
  inputs.
- **Sequential rotation of assets:** from the "Technological Phase" (Nasdaq equities) →
  "Financial Services Phase" (banks, widened margins) → the current terminal
  "Commodities & Natural Resources Phase" — explaining gold/silver outperformance vs.
  stagnating software equities.
- **The Federal Reserve "pantomime":** policy-rate focus obscures the true drivers — the
  Repo market and US Treasury short-term bill issuance determine actual liquidity.
- **FY2026 — Strategic Accumulation Window:** the fourth year of the cycle is lateral /
  volatile. 2026 is *not* a year of significant appreciation; 20–25% drawdowns are entry
  points, not exits. Patience over high-frequency gains.

## Compulsory Debasement — The Obligation Maturity Horizon

- Between 2026–2029 the global financial system faces a **~$40 trillion debt-maturity
  wall** — obligations issued at zero rates in 2020 must be refinanced under harsher
  conditions.
- Sovereigns cannot tax their way out, so central banks will be **compelled to monetize
  debt** — printing currency to buy government securities and prevent systemic default.
- This creates a **Doctrine of Perpetual Liquidity**: permanent monetary inflation, with
  fiat purchasing power under continuous structural erosion. Gold and Bitcoin become a
  **fiduciary hedge**, not a speculation. Amplified by "Treasury QE" — funding government
  spending by issuing short-term bills directly to private banks.

## The Divergence of Scarcity and Abundance — "The Everything Code"

A structural divergence: traditional software (abundance) devalues while physical
intelligence infrastructure (scarcity) appreciates. Energy is converted into intelligence.

- **AI as a deflationary catalyst:** intelligence becomes an abundant, low-cost commodity,
  compressing the value of human-intermediated services and legacy SaaS. Agentic AI
  enables total customization, making generic subscription "bloat" obsolete.
- **The $85 trillion infrastructure reconfiguration:** embedding intelligence into all
  mechanical/industrial assets (autonomous vehicles, humanoids, power grids) needs ~$85T
  over 15 years. Physical components face scarcity — power, semiconductors, industrial
  metals. Silver is the "synapse" of modern machinery.
- **The scarcity haven:** inexorable demand for energy, copper, and silver — assets with a
  "Proof of Work" characteristic that algorithms cannot bypass.

## Geopolitical Dynamics

- **US vs. China:** an "existential" AI arms race — neither side can afford to lose,
  driving continued massive spending regardless of debt levels.
- **Resource wars:** tensions over Taiwan, Venezuela, Greenland are about securing
  minerals and power for super-intelligence.
- **The power problem:** the US has ample gas but a shortage of transformers and turbines
  for new data centres.
- **China:** the PBoC injected ~$1.1tn to mitigate property and debt crises; Chinese
  participants use **gold** as a monetary proxy, anchoring its global price.
- **Japan:** the BoJ is in a "Fiscal Interest Rate Trap" — debt >250% of GDP means it
  cannot tighten, ensuring a structurally weak Yen and the continuation of the **Yen carry
  trade** as cheap global liquidity.

## Key Events That Could Shift the Regime

Prolonged major-power war · global energy supply shock · US–China decoupling / tariff
escalation · central-bank regime shift (real-rates discontinuity) · sovereign or banking
crisis in a major economy · supply-chain realignment from sanctions · large productivity
inflection · breakdown in global financial plumbing · major political upheaval ·
climate / resource shock.

## Why It Matters

This regime view is the "why now" of the entire retreat. Compulsory debasement justifies
holding the Digital Asset Treasury; the lateral 2026 accumulation window justifies a
yield-first posture; the melt-up scenario justifies the non-discretionary sell triggers.
