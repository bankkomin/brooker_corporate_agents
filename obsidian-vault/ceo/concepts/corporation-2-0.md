# Corporation 2.0 — The Company Becomes a Network

## Summary

Corporation 2.0 is Worldview #10 of the [[brooker-worldview-2026]], expanded into its own
operating thesis. It claims the 20th-century corporation — designed for industrial labour,
centralised management, national markets, and bank-centred finance — is being replaced by
a next-generation firm that is AI-augmented, globally networked, media-native,
capital-fluid, programmable, and community-integrated. The corporation evolves from a
**hierarchy** into a **coordination system**.

## Definition

| Old Corporation | Corporation 2.0 |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| Employees → Departments → Managers → Meetings → Reports → Decisions | Humans + AI Agents + Community + Capital Networks + Media + Smart Contracts + Global Liquidity |
| Slow, hierarchical, geographic, human-bottlenecked | Fast, adaptive, borderless, programmable |

Companies evolve from organisations into operating systems.

## How It Works

Corporation 2.0 is the umbrella thesis for AI agents, tokenized incentives, treasury
systems, digital workforces, VCC ecosystems, creator-driven brands, community capital
formation, on-chain governance, and distribution-first businesses. It explains why old
companies struggle, why startups scale faster, and why media and communities matter.

## Why It Matters

For Brooker, Corporation 2.0 is not just commentary — it is the design brief for the
[[brooker-operating-system]] and a core part of the firm's advisory pitch ("Brooker
business consulting will help advise and integrate with the best tech stack"). It also
frames Brooker's own transformation from a static container of assets into a "Sovereign
Innovation Operating System."
