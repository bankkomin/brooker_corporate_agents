# ESG & Sustainability Governance — The CGSC Mandate

## Summary

Brooker's ESG and sustainability oversight is vested in the **Corporate Governance and
Sustainability Committee (CGSC)** — one of the five Board-level committees established at
the Khao Yai retreat (Session 9, [[2026-02-21-committee-governance-structure|R-05]]). The
CGSC carries the firm's corporate-governance, ethics, anti-corruption, and
sustainability/ESG mandate. This article documents that mandate in full, since it is the
firm's only standing ESG governance structure.

> **Two distinct things — do not conflate.** The CGSC governs *how Brooker itself is run*
> (corporate ESG governance). It is separate from the firm's **carbon-credit business
> investments** (IREC, Laos Carbon), which the [[2026-02-21-strategic-stop-list|Stop List]]
> places in "harvest-only" mode. Winding down carbon *investments* does not reduce the
> firm's *corporate sustainability governance* obligations as a SET-listed company.

## The CGSC — Mandate

To oversee and promote good corporate governance, ethical business conduct,
anti-corruption practices, and sustainability strategy/policies (including ESG governance
and material sustainability matters), and to support the Board in ensuring governance
practices are aligned with applicable laws, regulations, and recognized standards for
listed companies.

**Committee class:** Board-level committee. **Type:** standing.

## The CGSC — Key Functions

| Area | Function |
|------|----------|
| Governance policy | Review and recommend corporate-governance policies, code of conduct, anti-corruption policy, whistleblowing policy, and related governance frameworks |
| Group-wide implementation | Oversee implementation of governance practices across the Company and its subsidiaries / controlled entities |
| Sustainability governance | Oversee the sustainability governance framework, sustainability strategy direction, and material ESG topics relevant to the business |
| Goals & disclosure | Review sustainability-related goals, management approach, and disclosure readiness — including annual report / **One Report** sustainability disclosures |
| Incidents & remediation | Monitor governance and ethics incidents, policy breaches, and remediation actions — in coordination with Legal / Compliance / Risk |
| Awareness & training | Promote director and management awareness and training on governance, ethics, and sustainability matters |

## Operational Support — The ESG Hire

The Session 11 talent bench includes a dedicated **Risk & Compliance Senior Officer, ESG**
(monthly salary THB 80,000) — the operational role that supports the CGSC's sustainability
and governance workload. This is one of the 15 roles in the 2026 org-chart expansion.

## Disclosure Context — SET / One Report

As a SET-listed company, Brooker's sustainability disclosures appear in the annual report
/ **One Report**. The CGSC owns "disclosure readiness" for these sustainability sections,
and coordinates with the [[2026-02-21-committee-governance-structure|Audit Committee]] on
compliance with SET/SEC requirements.

## Why It Matters

ESG and sustainability governance is a **listed-company obligation**, not an optional
initiative. Even as Brooker exits carbon-credit *investments*, the CGSC keeps the firm's
*corporate* governance, anti-corruption, ethics, and sustainability-disclosure machinery
running — protecting the firm's listing status and institutional reputation, both central
to the equity-story re-rating.
