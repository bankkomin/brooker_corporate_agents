# The 40% Investment Company Rule

## Quick-answer aliases (for retrieval)

- **Q: When does the 40% Investment Company rule become binding?** A: **30 June 2026** — end of the 2-year grace period the Thai SEC granted; the value-chain carve-out was proposed but denied.
- **Q: What is the hard line for the 40% rule?** A: Passive investment assets must be **≤ 40% of total assets** to avoid Investment-Company reclassification under Thai SEC rules.
- **Q: What is the 2026 OKR target for the investment ratio?** A: **< 38% by 30 June 2026** (operating target ~35%, vs ~50% at the Khao Yai retreat).
- **Q: What happens if the 40% rule is breached after the deadline?** A: Reclassification as an Investment Company by Thai SEC — severely limits capital deployment and operational flexibility.

## Summary

The Thai SEC maintains a threshold: if a company's passive investment assets exceed **40%
of total assets**, it is reclassified as an Investment Company, which severely limits
capital deployment and operational flexibility. As of the Khao Yai retreat Brooker stood
at ~50% (THB 1,728M of a THB 3,476M asset base) — roughly 10 points above the ceiling,
forcing a structural pivot.

## Definition

The rule measures *passive* investment assets against the total asset base. Exceeding 40%
triggers "Investment Company" classification. A secondary historical constraint — a 50%
cumulative transaction-size limit during an investment-company grace period — previously
locked Brooker out of strategically rebalancing token gains without triggering shareholder
meeting requirements.

## How It Works — The VCC "Structural Vent"

The Singapore Variable Capital Company (VCC) is the structural solution. By moving assets
from the direct balance sheet into managed fund structures:

- Brooker becomes the **General Partner**, deriving value from service fees (operating
  income) rather than capital gains (investment income).
- External **Limited Partner** capital in VCC subfunds does **not** count against
  Brooker's 40% limit.
- Investment assets convert into Fund NAV with outside-capital growth.

This is being executed via [[brook-technology-capital-vcc]] and the
[[brook-limited-partners-fof]] (ELCID disclosure, 16 December 2025).

## Why It Matters

Non-compliance risks a forced liquidation of assets at the bottom of a cycle to meet the
rule — a pre-mortem "silent killer." The retreat also flagged the **re-classification
trap**: even with VCC structures, the SEC could apply a "look-through" if Brooker exercises
too much direct control over fund assets. Mitigation requires genuinely independent
investment committees per VCC and documented "Manager vs. Owner" separation.
