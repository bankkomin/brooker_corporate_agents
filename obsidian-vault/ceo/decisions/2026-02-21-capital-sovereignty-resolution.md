# R-03 — Capital Sovereignty & The Stay Liquid Doctrine

## Decision

The Board establishes the [[stay-liquid-doctrine]] liquidity stack, recognising liquidity
as the ultimate form of sovereignty and independence from traditional banking gatekeepers.

## Change Details

- **The Sovereignty Buffer:** a strategic allocation of **100 BTC** held in
  institutional-grade self-custody, used as permissionless collateral for instant, 24/7
  on-chain liquidity.
- **Institutional Maturation:** management is authorised to engage credit-rating agencies
  (target **BBB+ or higher**) to enter public debt markets.
- **Banking Synergy:** maintenance of strategic THB credit lines with Siam Commercial
  Bank (SCB) and Bangkok Bank (BBL) as a TradFi bridge.

## Rationale

The 2021–2025 cycle exposed the fragility of relying solely on centralised counterparties
for emergency liquidity — time latency, covenant tightening under stress, and
uncontrollable regulatory externalities. A dual-track (on-chain + local banking) profile
makes the capital structure antifragile.

## Impact

- **Affected systems:** treasury custody, funding ladder, debt-market access.
- **Custody/liquidity partners:** [[hex-trust]], Aave, Anchorage.
- **Guardrail:** on-chain Debt-to-Value capped (board-level hard cap ~25%); pre-mortem
  failure mode is the "liquidity & margin spiral."
