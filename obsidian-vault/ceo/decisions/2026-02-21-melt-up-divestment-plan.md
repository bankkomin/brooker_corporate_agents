# R-02 — Melt-Up Divestment Plan & 40% Rule Compliance

## Decision

To ensure adherence to the [[investment-company-40-percent-rule]] and mitigate "paper
gains" risk, the Board mandates a trigger-based divestment plan with non-discretionary
melt-up monetization, asset reclassification into the VCC structure, and portfolio
rationalisation.

## Change Details

- **Non-Discretionary Monetization:** pre-set melt-up triggers. When BNB/BTC reach
  predetermined volatility/price thresholds, management must execute a non-discretionary
  sell-down into cash or stablecoins. The CIO may not cancel a trigger without a full
  Board vote.
- **Asset Reclassification & Migration:** the CFO is authorised to migrate passive fund
  assets into the VCC "Operating Product" structure.
- **Portfolio Rationalization:** immediate liquidation of non-core holdings (~THB 100M).

### Phase plan

| Phase | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 1 — Cleaning the Tail (0–6 mo) | Exit stagnant assets: Varuna (THB 100M), reduce trading securities; quarterly Sukhothai redemption ($500k/quarter) |
| 2 — Melt-up Monetization | Tiered exit as DA markets enter melt-up; DAT to 20–30% of total assets |
| 3 — Productize | Build new VCC subfunds from client demand; pool fixed costs |

Sell triggers (from the retreat deck): regulatory events (Clarity Act), Fed / US Treasury
/ China & Japan central bank action, month-to-month gains, retail/parabolic participation,
BTC above ~150k.

## Rationale

At the retreat Brooker stood at ~50% investment-asset ratio — ~10 points above the 40%
ceiling. Operating target is ~35% to allow balance-sheet growth. The 2021–2025 cycle
showed that un-monetized ICO gains suffered large drawdowns because rebalancing was
regulator-locked.

## Impact

- **Affected systems:** balance sheet, DA treasury, VCC migration.
- **Timeline:** Phase 1 within 6 months of the retreat; OKR target <38% by June 2026.
