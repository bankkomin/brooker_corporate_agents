# R-05 — Committee-Led Governance & Decision Rights

## Decision

The Board adopts a committee-led governance structure to decentralise expertise, enhance
oversight velocity, and reduce key-person risk, together with a formal Decision Rights
Matrix. The structure comprises **five Board-level committees** and **four management
committees**, each with a chartered mandate and key functions.

## Change Details — Committee Structure

### Board-Level Committees

| Committee | Mandate (summary) |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Investment Committee (IC)** | Consider/approve the Company's principal investment and digital-asset treasury matters within delegated authority; oversee portfolio risk limits, investment discipline, and performance. |
| **Risk Committee (RC)** | Oversee the enterprise risk-management framework across strategic, market, credit, liquidity, operational, legal/compliance, technology/cyber, and reputational risk; ensure operation within the Board-approved risk appetite. |
| **Audit Committee (AC)** | Oversee the integrity of financial reporting, internal control and internal audit, compliance with laws/regulations, and the independence and performance of the external auditor. |
| **Nomination & Remuneration Committee (NRC)** | Ensure appropriate board composition, qualified leadership succession, and fair, transparent, performance-aligned remuneration for directors and senior executives. |
| **Corporate Governance & Sustainability Committee (CGSC)** | Oversee corporate governance, ethical conduct, anti-corruption, and **sustainability strategy/policies including ESG governance and material sustainability matters**. See [[esg-sustainability-governance]]. |

### Management Committees

| Committee | Mandate (summary) |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Executive Committee (EXCOM)** | Support the CEO in enterprise-level strategy execution, business performance, capital/resource prioritisation, and major cross-functional decisions. |
| **Leadership Committee (LC)** *(management only)* | The Group's **monthly** management operating forum for execution alignment, cross-functional coordination, and resolution of operational issues. |
| **Capital Allocation & ALCO Committee (CAC)** *(management only)* | Manage the Group's balance sheet, liquidity, funding structure, capital-allocation priorities, and asset-liability risk; owns the "Stay Liquid" doctrine operationally. |
| **Operations & Product Committee (OPC)** *(management only)* | Oversee operational readiness, process standardisation, product governance, and product lifecycle "kill criteria" for new and existing platforms. |

> **Note on naming:** Session 9 of the retreat pack details all nine committees above. The
> condensed R-05 resolution text groups them as "IC & RC", "ACC (Audit & Compliance)",
> "CAC", and "OPC"; the Session 9 charter is authoritative and is reflected here in full.

### Key Functions — Highlights

- **IC** — approve investment proposals/divestments within delegated authority; oversee
  digital-asset treasury and non-fund principal investments; review compliance with
  concentration limits, drawdown thresholds, and stop-loss / monetization frameworks;
  escalate material breaches to the Board / RC.
- **RC** — recommend the risk-appetite statement and key risk indicators; oversee
  **internal monitoring of the 40% investment-company rule**; review major risk incidents
  and remediation; coordinate with the AC to avoid overlap (AC = controls/reporting
  assurance; RC = risk framework/appetite).
- **AC** — review quarterly/annual financial statements; review internal control and
  internal audit; review external-auditor independence; review related-party / connected
  transactions; report in the annual report.
- **NRC** — review board composition and director nomination/independence; oversee
  succession planning for the Chairman, CEO, and key senior management; recommend
  remuneration policy aligned with strategy and long-term value; oversee board and
  executive performance evaluation.
- **CGSC** — review governance policies, code of conduct, anti-corruption and
  whistleblowing policies; oversee the sustainability governance framework and material
  ESG topics; review One Report sustainability disclosure readiness; monitor governance/
  ethics incidents and remediation. Full detail in [[esg-sustainability-governance]].
- **EXCOM** — review progress against the strategic and annual business plan; review
  quarterly business performance; approve management matters within delegated authority;
  handle matters escalated from CAC/OPC/LC.
- **LC** — monthly performance-versus-plan review; cross-functional coordination; execution
  governance and dashboards; people/organisation execution.
- **CAC** — capital allocation across operating businesses, principal investments,
  treasury, and strategic reserves; liquidity planning and the "Stay Liquid" doctrine;
  bank facilities and covenant monitoring; on-chain collateral / margin-buffer thresholds.
- **OPC** — operational-readiness approval for new products; product-governance go/no-go
  criteria; product lifecycle and "kill criteria"; process digitisation and AI-enabled
  workflows.

## ESG & Sustainability Mandate

ESG and sustainability oversight is a chartered Board-level responsibility, held by the
**Corporate Governance & Sustainability Committee (CGSC)**. Its sustainability remit:

- Oversee the **sustainability governance framework**, sustainability strategy direction,
  and **material ESG topics** relevant to the business.
- Review sustainability-related **goals, management approach, and disclosure readiness** —
  including the annual report / **One Report** sustainability disclosures.
- Review and recommend the **code of conduct, anti-corruption policy, and whistleblowing
  policy**.
- Monitor governance and ethics incidents, policy breaches, and remediation — in
  coordination with Legal / Compliance / Risk.
- Promote director and management **awareness and training** on governance, ethics, and
  sustainability.

Operationally, the CGSC is supported by a dedicated **Risk & Compliance Senior Officer,
ESG** (one of the 15 roles in the 2026 talent-bench expansion). The full ESG charter is
documented in [[esg-sustainability-governance]].

> ESG *governance* (how Brooker is run as a listed company) is distinct from the firm's
> carbon-credit *investments* (IREC, Laos Carbon), which the
> [[2026-02-21-strategic-stop-list|Stop List]] places in harvest-only mode.

## Decision Rights Matrix (Proposed Thresholds)

| Decision category | Management (CEO/CIO) | Committee approval | Board approval |
|-------------------|----------------------|--------------------|----------------|
| DA treasury trading | < THB 30M within approved limits | Breach of limits | Strategy shift |
| VCC fund launch | < THB 10M / US$1M (to build track record & GP) | Product terms & risk | Final launch & seeding |
| Credit / lending | < THB 10M secured (or facility standard) | > THB 100M | Unsecured / non-core |
| Operational capex | Within budget | > 10% budget variance | Major M&A / divestment |
| On-chain borrowing | < 10% DTV at transaction date | 10–25% DTV | > 25% DTV |

## Org Chart, Execution Cadence & Incentives

- **Next-stage org chart:** headcount shifts from "Execution" toward "Management & Risk
  Control." Critical talent gaps and immediate hires are tracked in the 2026 talent bench
  — see [[2026-budget]]. Key-person risk is a named pre-mortem failure mode
  ([[failure-mode-talent-execution-gap]]).
- **Execution cadence:** Weekly — liquidity buffer & DA treasury snapshot (management);
  Monthly — KPI dashboard (committees); Quarterly — full strategy check-in & OKR review
  (Board); Annual — strategic retreat (Khao Yai).
- **Incentives & alignment:** risk-adjusted metrics (Sharpe / net fee income, not gross
  AUM); **clawback provisions** for Engine 3 if a melt-up gain is lost by failing to
  follow Board exit triggers; ESOP for A-Player hires; the "Elon Moon Shot" 10-year
  stretch incentive (Market Cap ×10, AUM USD 1bn, DAT THB 5bn, net profit THB 2bn → THB
  1.6bn stock-option pool).

## Rationale

Brooker historically operated with high agility but low structural consistency. Scaling
Engines 1 and 2 while safely managing Engine 3 requires governance-led decision-making
rather than reliance on key individuals.

## Impact

- **Affected systems:** all decision workflows; the [[brooker-operating-system]] digitises
  this governance layer. The Capital Allocation & ALCO Committee (CAC) is the committee
  the corporate AI agent system's Phase 1 was built for.
