# Work-From-Home (WFH) Policy

## Summary

The work-from-home (WFH / การให้สิทธิพนักงานทำงานที่บ้าน) control domain covers how
Brooker Group grants and governs the right of employees to work from home, in line with
Thai Labour Protection Act **Section 23/1** (มาตรา 23/1). The control expectations are
captured in an internal-control self-assessment questionnaire — see
[[internal-control-questionnaire-wfh-rights]]. This article describes the considered
control standard the questionnaire surveys; it is a self-assessment, not a separately
published WFH policy document.

## Definition

The domain treats WFH as an employee right that must be formalised, communicated, and
governed: a written WFH policy, a known legal basis, a request form/process, a formal
review-and-response cycle, and employee briefing on WFH rights. The questionnaire frames
this across three sections, each item self-assessed as Yes (ใช่), No (ไม่ใช่), or
Don't Know (ไม่ทราบ).

## Control Sections Surveyed

### Section 1 — Compliance with Labour Law, Section 23/1

| # | Question (paraphrased) | Status (as completed) |
|---|------------------------|-----------------------|
| 1.1 | Does the company have a written WFH policy? | Yes |
| 1.2 | Do employees know they may request WFH under labour law? | Yes |
| 1.3 | Is there a form/system for employees to submit a WFH request? | No — note: HR follows the company's HR practice |
| 1.4 | Does the company review and respond to WFH requests formally and with reasons? | Yes |
| 1.5 | Are employees trained / briefed on their WFH rights? | Yes |

## Why It Matters

Thai Labour Protection Act Section 23/1 gives employees a statutory basis to agree
work-from-home arrangements. Formalising WFH (written policy, request process, documented
review) reduces labour-law and inspection risk. The questionnaire pairs WFH governance
with anti-corruption and internal-control checks, treating WFH administration as part of
Brooker's wider compliance posture. The single gap (item 1.3 — no dedicated request
form/system) indicates WFH requests are handled through general HR practice rather than a
formalised intake form.

## Key Controls / Thresholds

| Control | Expectation | Status |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Written WFH policy | Policy exists in writing | Present |
| Legal basis | Section 23/1 known to employees | Present |
| Request form/system | Dedicated WFH request intake | Gap — general HR practice used instead |
| Formal review | Reasoned, formal response to requests | Present |
| Employee briefing | Staff trained on WFH rights | Present |
| Government inspection history | No prior WFH-related inspection | None recorded |

whether WFH is allowed at Brooker, cite item 1.1 (written WFH policy = Yes) and the
Section 23/1 legal basis, but flag item 1.3 (no dedicated request form) as the noted gap.
